Scene-by-Scene Treatment

Cold Open — The Line
0:00 – 0:05
NYC exterior — apartment stoop or brownstone stairs

Jake, walking, straight to camera. No intro, no logo, no setup.

"Tryps is the travel agent in your group chat. You can book flights, hotels, and activities — directly in iMessage."

Quick, confident, Dollar Shave Club energy. He's already walking. The video is already moving.

Beat 1 — The Unpaid Travel Agent
0:05 – 0:18
Apartment stoop / stairs outside a building

Jake gestures to a group of friends on the stairs. One person — the designated planner — has their laptop open with a Google Doc, a Notion page, a ChatGPT window, AND a Google Spreadsheet all visible. They look stressed. The rest are on their phones, not helping.

"Today, every friend group has this person. The unpaid travel agent. They run a Google Doc, a Notion page, a spreadsheet, and a ChatGPT window — all at the same time — just to get their friends to agree on a hotel."

The planner looks up at Jake, exhausted. Jake keeps walking.

"And when the group still shows up late? This person resents all of them."
Beat 2 — Tryps That Never Happened
0:18 – 0:25
Different stoop or park bench, same neighborhood

Jake pans to another group of friends sitting around doing nothing. Above them, a sign/banner reads:

"TRYPS THAT NEVER HAPPENED"
"Or... this trip never happens. Nobody took the role. Too hard to coordinate. Another one for the graveyard."

Beat. Jake shrugs.

"We fixed both of these."
Beat 3 — Polling
0:25 – 0:35
NYC sidewalk or park — open space with a small group

A group of 5-6 people standing together. Jake walks up on them mid-decision. They're physically raising their hands — actually voting — while simultaneously, we see a close-up of an iMessage poll running on one of their phones.

The physical vote and the digital poll match. Three hands up in real life, three votes on screen.

"Tryps has native polling built into the group chat. Your friends actually make decisions. In real time. No more 'I'm down for whatever.'"
For Sean: This is the money shot. The physical-digital mirror should feel fun and slightly absurd — people theatrically raising hands while phones buzz with votes. Could lean comedic (someone raising both hands, someone voting the opposite of what they said out loud).
Beat 4 — However You Get To Us
0:35 – 0:45
NYC street, quick cuts across multiple spots

Quick montage. Different people, different devices — all contacting Tryps. Every device is RED (red phone case, red flip phone, red landline, red Apple Watch band).

  • Someone CALLING Tryps on a red rotary phone on a street corner
  • Someone texting from a red flip phone
  • Someone checking their red Apple Watch
  • Someone typing the number digit by digit on a red candy bar phone
"Tryps is available where you already are."

No other dialogue. Just the montage. The red devices pop against the muted NYC street. Every phone is red. Nothing else is.

Beat 5 — The App on the Trip
0:45 – 0:55
A genuinely cool NYC spot — NOT Times Square. West Village courtyard, Bushwick rooftop, Studio 151 vibes, Brooklyn local favorite.

A group of college-age girls (obviously tourists, having the time of their lives) at a spot that is decidedly NOT a tourist trap. No line. No crowds. A cool, real NYC spot.

"These girls are visiting New York. But instead of spending their whole trip waiting in line at Times Square — they're here."

Quick shot of the Tryps mobile app on one of their phones showing the itinerary.

"Because Tryps knew they'd like this place."
Beat 6 — Traveler DNA
0:55 – 1:05
Same spot or new NYC corner — cafe or walking

Close-up of phone screen showing the Tryps travel profile — Traveler DNA. Past trips, preferences, a profile building itself.

"Tryps gets to know you. It remembers what you liked last time — builds your Traveler DNA. And it connects to the apps you already use."

Quick flash of app logos (Airbnb, Uber, Booking.com). Not a feature demo — just a visual flash. The point lands in 2 seconds.

Beat 7 — Explore / Friend Travel
1:05 – 1:15
Same or new — wherever the energy is

Phone screen showing the Explore tab: friends' countries visited, activities, post-trip reviews.

"See where your friends have actually been. Not Instagram posts — real reviews. Real recs. So when you go to Barcelona, you're going to the place your friend found — not the place TripAdvisor told you about."
Ending
1:15 – 1:25
NYC street

Jake stops walking. Turns to camera.

"Tryps. The travel agent in your group chat."

A cab pulls up. Jake gets in. Through the window, one last line — or just a look. The cab drives off.

Tryps
Text us. Go while you can.

Decisions for Sean

1. Jake on camera vs. voiceover?

This draft has Jake walking and talking on camera. Could also work as VO over vignettes (never on screen). Sean's call.

2. "Tryps That Never Happened" sign?

Physical banner (funnier) vs. post-produced text (cleaner) vs. chalkboard sign.

3. Polling beat staging?

This is the money shot. How many people? How theatrical? Does someone vote wrong? Change their vote?

4. Location for Beat 5?

Needs a genuinely cool spot that reads as "real NYC." Studio 151, Lucien, Bushwick rooftop, West Village courtyard. Sean's pick.

5. Do Beats 6 & 7 stay?

Traveler DNA and Explore are important but slow the pace. 60s cut might drop them for a separate product video.

Production Notes

Tone

Dollar Shave Club meets Anthony Bourdain. Jake is dry, confident, walking with purpose. The comedy is observational — pointing at real absurdities of trip planning, not slapstick. The warmth comes from the product actually working and people actually having a good time.

Key Visual Motifs

  • Red devices (Beat 4) — Every phone/device that touches Tryps is red. Everything else is NYC-neutral. Direct callback to "However You Get To Us" concept.
  • Physical-digital mirror (Beat 3) — People voting in real life while iMessage polls run. The most creative beat.
  • Real vs. tourist NYC (Beat 5) — Where tourists go vs. where Tryps sends you. Needs a genuinely cool location.

Budget

~$5,000 for a 1-day guerrilla NYC shoot. Red devices need sourcing (cases are cheap, rotary phone is prop rental or thrift). "Tryps That Never Happened" banner is a print shop job ($50-100).
