# Win Your Dream Trip

**Launch Date:** May 2, 2026
**Prize:** All-expenses-paid group trip for 4-6 friends. Flights, hotel, one week.
**Budget:** $15-20K prize + $3-4K launch video

---

## What Tryps Is

Tryps is a travel agent that lives in your iMessage group chat. You add the Tryps number to a group chat with your friends, and Marty — the AI — plans your entire trip through the conversation. Destinations, flights, hotels, itineraries, cost splitting. It works where your friend group already talks.

**The thing that makes people stop and say "wait, what?":** You add a phone number to your actual group chat and it acts like a friend who helps plan the trip. No one else is doing this.

**Core audience:** 22-28 year old post-grads who travel in groups. The person in every friend group who actually makes plans happen.

---

## The Giveaway

One winning friend group gets their dream trip — fully planned by Marty, fully paid by Tryps. Where they want to go, with the people they want to go with.

### How It Works

**Entry (Instagram):**
1. Follow @jointryps
2. Comment your dream destination + tag the 4-6 friends in your squad
3. Each tagged friend who reposts to their Story tagging @jointryps = +1 bonus entry for the whole squad
4. Anyone can also enter separately with their own squad

**Winner:** Random draw. One group. The whole squad flies.

**The reveal:** We video call the winning group and tell them they're going on their dream trip. Film the reaction. That clip becomes launch day content.

### Why This Mechanic Works

Every giveaway entry does three things at once:

1. **Product demo.** The comment names a destination. The tags name a friend group. That's exactly what Tryps does — take a group of friends and a destination and make it happen.
2. **Group pressure drives reach.** When you tag your squad, they have skin in the game. They repost to boost YOUR group's odds. One entry turns into 5-6 Story reposts, each reaching that person's entire follower base.
3. **The entry IS the content.** A feed full of comments saying "Bali with @sarah @mike @jess @alex" is social proof that real friend groups want this. People scrolling the comments see their friends tagged and think "wait, I want in."

## The Content Engine

The giveaway produces four waves of content:

1. **The launch video** (Week 1) — announces the giveaway, introduces Marty, shows what it looks like to add an AI to your group chat. $3-4K production budget.
2. **User entries** (Weeks 2-3) — hundreds of comments and Story reposts from real friend groups naming real destinations. This is organic, free, and the best social proof possible.
3. **The winner reveal** (Week 4) — video call reaction when a group of friends finds out they're going on their dream trip. This is the clip that travels.
4. **The trip itself** (post-launch) — Marty plans the trip, the group goes, they document it. Weeks of authentic content from real users on a real trip that Tryps made happen.

---

## What Makes This Different

Most app launch giveaways say "download to enter." Tryps doesn't need that. The giveaway lives on Instagram where the audience already is. The app download happens *after* they win — when Marty plans the trip and sends them a postcard (their itinerary) that lives inside the app.

The giveaway creates demand. The product fulfills it. In that order.

And the core mechanic — "an AI joins your group chat and plans your trip" — is inherently a media story. It's a morning show segment. It's a "you have to see this" text to a friend. The prize gets people in the door. The product is what they talk about.

**We're looking for three things:**

### 1. Media Introductions

Does Rich know anyone at entertainment press, morning shows, or podcasts who would cover a "win your dream trip" giveaway — especially one built around an AI that joins your group chat?

The winner reveal video is built to be a segment. A group of friends on a video call finding out they're going on their dream trip — that's producible content for a morning show, a podcast story, or an entertainment news hit. One placement turns a startup giveaway into a cultural moment.

### 2. Contest Design Feedback

Rich has produced shows where audience engagement is the product. The Tryps giveaway is the same thing — the entry mechanic IS the entertainment. What would Rich do differently? How would he make the reveal bigger? Is there a format from the entertainment world that would make this land harder?

### 3. Thinking Bigger

This is a $20K giveaway from a startup with zero followers. With the right amplification, it could look and feel like something much larger. If Rich were advising on this, where would he point it? What relationships or platforms could turn a great mechanic into a moment people remember?

## Notes for Jake

**On the "AI in your group chat" hook:** Lead with this, not the prize. The prize is compelling but it's not unique — lots of brands give away trips. Nobody else has an AI that joins your iMessage group chat and acts like a friend. When you say that out loud, watch Rich's face. That's the moment he leans in.

**On the reveal video:** Describe it as a format, not a one-off. "We film the moment a group of friends finds out they're going on their dream trip." Rich thinks in formats — if he can picture this as a recurring series, he'll see the scale.

**On the ask:** Be specific. Don't say "can you help us get the word out." Say "do you know a segment producer at Today or GMA who would run a 'win your dream trip' story?" or "is there a podcast in your world that covers travel or Gen Z culture?" Specific asks get specific answers. Vague asks get "let me think about it."

**On what's still in progress:** Legal compliance for the giveaway (official rules, no-purchase-necessary alternate entry) is being handled separately. If Rich asks, tell him it's in motion — don't let an operational detail slow down the conversation about the vision.

**On budget honesty:** If Rich asks about budget, be straightforward. "$20K prize, $3-4K video." Don't inflate it. The pitch isn't "we have a huge budget" — it's "we built a mechanic that punches way above its weight, and one media placement could 10x our reach." That's what makes Rich's involvement valuable, not the dollar amount.

## Action Items Before the Meeting

1. **Hire a promotions attorney ($300-500).** Giveaways over $5K require official rules, a no-purchase-necessary alternate entry method, and state registrations (New York requires 30 days before launch, Florida requires 7 days). This is not optional — it's federal and state law. If the giveaway launches April 14, the legal work needs to start immediately.

2. **Confirm Rich's current role and affiliations.** Rich may no longer be at Disney. Jake needs to know what Rich is doing now before the meeting so the conversation and asks are calibrated to Rich's current world, not his old one. The media contacts he has today may be different from his Disney-era network.

3. **Lead with the "AI in your group chat" hook, not the prize.** This is in the notes above but it bears repeating as a pre-meeting action: Jake should rehearse describing the Marty mechanic out loud. "You add a phone number to your group chat and it plans your entire trip like a friend" — that sentence is the one that makes Rich lean in. The giveaway is the vehicle. The product is the story.
