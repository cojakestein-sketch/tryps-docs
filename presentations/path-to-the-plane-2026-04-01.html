Key Takeaways

Three levers that determine
which tier Jake lives in

01 — THE BIG ONE

The full booking stack changes everything

With flights, hotels, and activities all bookable, each user is worth $0.60/yr — double the flights-only number. Hotels are the engine: 30% of GMV but nearly half the take rate. Next unlock: direct hotel deals (moving from 6% affiliate to 12–15% OTA) would push blended take to 8% and halve every tier.

02

Paid conversion decides whether this works at all

Moving from 8% to 25% paid conversion is 3x fewer users needed. Planning-first apps convert 2–5%. The iMessage agent must seamlessly convert group chat plans into actual bookings.

03

Founder's take compresses with scale

At $1M revenue, bootstrapped: ~25% flows to Jake. At $10M post-Series A: ~3%. The path to bigger tiers requires accepting a smaller personal cut of a much bigger number.

04 — THE SHORTCUT

The Company Plane path

If the Challenger 350 is a company asset (business travel, team offsites, partner visits) instead of personal income, the math changes. Company OpEx is pre-tax; personal distributions get hit with ~38% tax.
