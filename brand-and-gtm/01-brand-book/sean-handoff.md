## The Positioning (3 sentences)

Tryps is a travel agent in your iMessage. His name is Martin — he saves as "Martin from Tryps" in your contacts and feels like a person, not software. You text Martin your vibe, and he plans the entire trip — flights, hotels, activities — in five minutes.

---

## Key Rules for Every Screen

1. **Martin is the hero.** The travel agent in iMessage is the product. The app visualizes what Martin plans.
2. **Never say AI.** Martin is never disclosed as AI. He's a person you text. Period.
3. **"Travel agent" not "concierge."** Travel agent hits harder. More concrete, more real.
4. **70/30 agent to app.** iMessage conversations dominate the brand book. App screens (trip cards, itinerary) support.
5. **Film camera is a campaign.** iMessage conversation UI is the primary visual language. Film grain/warmth is art direction that supports, not leads.
6. **Tagline is not locked.** "Text your trip into existence" is the lead candidate. Use it as the working tagline but it may change.
7. **Contact name in screenshots:** Always show "Martin from Tryps" as the iMessage contact.

## Three Hero iMessage Conversations to Showcase

These three conversations must appear across the brand book. They show the two extremes of how people use Tryps:

**1. "I know exactly what I want"** (The Sean demo)
> User: "vegas. may 16. penthouse. 6 people. martin garrix at omnia."
> Martin: "on it. let me get back to you."
> Martin: "got everything. flights, penthouse at the Wynn, Omnia table for 6. $2,100 per person. want me to charge your card?"
> User: "done."

**2. "I have no idea"** (The Jake demo)
> User: "i want to see my boys from college. haven't seen them in a year. no idea when or where."
> Martin: "let's figure it out. who's in the group? i'll check everyone's availability."

**3. "Budget-conscious"**
> User: "find me flights under $300 for spring break. anywhere warm."
> Martin: "got 3 options — Tulum, Cabo, San Juan. all under $280 round trip."
