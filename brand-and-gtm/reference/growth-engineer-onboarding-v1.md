### Wispr Flow — Personal Onboarding as a Growth Engine

**The play:** Wispr Flow's CEO personally onboarded the first 500 users on Google Meet — not demo calls, but watching their mouse movements, eye tracking, and facial expressions at every step. Within two months, user base went from single digits to thousands purely through organic sharing. 90% of growth was word-of-mouth. They also seeded every tier-one VC fund, so when fundraising came, investors were already users. $10M revenue with 50 people.

**What worked:**
- **Personal onboarding of first 500 users.** Video calls, not surveys. This is how they found the "delight moments" in the product.
- **VC network seeding.** Every advisor and investor used the product daily. They became organic evangelists in their portfolio networks.
- **Bold product demo as content.** The Porsche GT3 RS challenge ("We'll give you a Porsche if you can make Wispr produce one error") got 3.5M views. The controversy when people found errors generated EVEN MORE views.
- **Kill low-ROI channels ruthlessly.** Cut SEO entirely. "No one is searching for voice dictation." Find the 20% giving 80% results, then find the 20% within that.
- **25% affiliate program via Dub.** Recurring commission, real-time dashboard, monthly payouts.

**What we're stealing:**
- **Jake does 100+ personal onboarding calls.** Before broad launch, get 50–100 people on video calls watching them use Tryps in their real group chats. Watch their faces. Find the delight moments.
- **Seed the advisor/VC/founder network via Jackson.** Get connected people using Tryps for their own trips before May 2.
- **Bold product challenge as content.** "Plan a 10-person Bali trip in under 5 minutes using Tryps. If we fail, we pay for your trip." Film it.

**Open question for the room:** Should Jake spend 2 weeks before launch doing personal onboarding calls with 50+ users, or is that time better spent on other launch prep?

### Partiful — Zero Paid Ads, Product IS the Marketing

**The play:** Partiful grew to 2M+ new users in H1 2025 and 400% YoY growth with zero paid ads and no marketing agency. Three-person marketing team. Every event invite is a brand impression — the invite IS the growth loop. Their brand voice ("Facebook Events for hot people") made them culturally relevant. Google Best App 2024.

**What worked:**
- **Product-led growth.** Every invite sent = one more person sees Partiful. Non-users get nudged to download.
- **Reactive social, not planned content.** When Apple launched Apple Invites, Partiful turned it into a meme instead of a press release. 6.1M views on one TikTok.
- **Brand voice as moat.** Dry, funny, self-aware. Not corporate. Their social feels like your funniest friend, not a brand account.
- **Editorial content.** "The Guest List" — a newsletter/blog about party culture. Not tips. Culture.
- **Film camera aesthetic.** Instagram grid has a disposable camera warmth that matches their vibe exactly (same motif as Tryps brand system).

**What we're stealing:**
- **Trip invite link as growth engine.** Every Tryps trip invite in iMessage needs to be so beautiful people screenshot and share it. This is scope 8 — Enen and the product team need to nail the OG image template.
- **Dense network seeding.** We launch in NYC friend groups who actually travel together, not a broad "everyone download this" push.
- **Reactive social from day one.** Don't just follow the content calendar. Monitor culture. Post fast. Be funny.
- **Inevitable branding.** "This is just how people plan trips now." Not "try this cool new thing."

**Open question for the room:** Partiful's social team is reactive and culture-driven, not calendar-driven. Should we follow our content calendar rigidly, or treat it as a starting point and give Inej + Sean freedom to post reactively when something culturally relevant happens?

### Locket — 26 Creators, 298M Views, CPM Under $1

**The play:** Locket used just 26 nano-influencer accounts posting 52 templatized TikTok videos per day. Total result: 298 million views, 1.5M shares, 10M downloads in the first month. CPM under $1. They didn't hire big creators — they found a repeatable content format and had nano-creators mass-produce it. Same hooks repeated 400–800+ times across accounts.

**What worked:**
- **One format, infinite repetitions.** The format: 3-second selfie hook + text overlay + screen recording of app in use. Every video followed the same template. Creators just filled in the blanks.
- **26 accounts, 52 videos/day.** Pure volume. Every Nth post goes viral on nano accounts, so you feed the algorithm. CPM under $1 because nano-creators are cheap ($25–75/video).
- **Ruthless replacement.** Underperforming accounts got cut. Top performers got more budget. No sentimentality.
- **One-off payment per video.** Not ongoing retainers. Pay per piece. Scale what works, kill what doesn't.
- **Performance tracking per creator.** Unique links per creator. They knew exactly which creator drove which installs.

**What we're stealing:**
- **Find ONE repeatable format for Tryps.** The equivalent: "POV: someone actually planned the group trip." 15-second screen recording of Tryps in iMessage, with a hook, every time. Same format, different destinations.
- **Unique tracking links per creator.** Enen is building `jointryps.com/go/{name}` redirect links through Branch. Non-negotiable.
- **Volume over quality.** 20 adequate TikToks > 2 polished ones. The algorithm rewards frequency.
- **2-week evaluation window.** By May 16, we should know which creators are driving installs and which to cut.

**Open question for the room:** Locket used 26 nano-creators with a templatized format. Can we create a standardized "Tryps demo" format that any creator can produce in 10 minutes? What does that template look like?

### Gas App (Nikita Bier) — The Instagram School Page Hack

**The play:** Nikita Bier launched Gas and TBH with $0 marketing spend using a specific Instagram hack. He created private school-specific Instagram accounts (e.g., `@gas.lakewood`), followed every student at the school, then at a coordinated time (3PM, end of class) accepted all follow requests simultaneously. Students saw the app everywhere at once. Geofenced state-by-state rollout. 5M downloads in 9 weeks (TBH), 10M users in 3 months (Gas). 4–5 person team. $1M revenue within 10 days.

**What worked:**
- **The Instagram hack.** Create private school accounts → follow all students → accept all follows at 3PM simultaneously → every student sees it at once. Costs nothing.
- **Geofenced rollout.** Don't launch everywhere. Launch at one school. Get to 100% penetration. Move to the next. "Gas is now available at your school" creates FOMO.
- **Core value in 3 seconds.** If users don't "get it" in 3 seconds, you've lost them.
- **$0 marketing budget.** Entirely organic through dense network effects.
- **Screenshot-worthy moments.** Users screenshotted their Gas compliments and posted to Instagram Stories. Free distribution.

**What we're stealing:**
- **The Instagram school page tactic, adapted for colleges.** Create `@tryps.mit`, `@tryps.usc`, etc. Follow students. Go private. Launch at a coordinated time (Friday before spring break planning). **Jake is going to MIT in fall 2026 — this is a direct play.**
- **Dense network seeding.** 5–10 real friend groups using Tryps before broad launch. Every user sees their actual friends on it.
- **The product IS the marketing.** When Tryps plans a trip in 5 minutes in someone's group chat, that group tells other groups.
- **Screenshot-worthy moments.** Trip invite cards, poll results, booking confirmations — every Tryps screen should be screenshot-worthy.

**Open question for the room:** The MIT campus rollout could be our version of the Nikita Bier play. Do we start building `@tryps.mit` now and do a campus-specific soft launch before the broad May 2 push? Or is that a post-launch play for fall 2026?

### BeReal — Simplicity and the Daily Ritual

**The play:** BeReal's growth hack was a daily notification at a random time. Everyone posted at the same time, creating a shared ritual. The content was intentionally low-fi — not curated, not polished, just real. They grew almost entirely through word-of-mouth and organic social sharing.

**What worked:**
- **Daily habit loop.** The random notification created urgency and routine simultaneously.
- **Anti-Instagram positioning.** "Be real" resonated because people were tired of curated perfection. The brand stood for authenticity.
- **Cash referral incentives.** BeReal offered $30–50 cash for referring friends. Simple, direct, effective at launch.
- **College campus penetration.** They focused on college students and post-grads — exactly Tryps' demo (22–28 year olds).

**What we're stealing:**
- **Referral incentives at launch.** Cash or trip credit for referring friend groups. Simple mechanic: invite a friend group → they create a trip → you get $X.
- **Anti-perfection content.** Our social content should feel like group chat energy, not a travel magazine. The content calendar already leans this way ("The Group Chat" pillar), but we should push it further.
- **College-adjacent targeting.** Post-grad friend groups who are still tight from college and plan trips together. This IS our core user.

**Open question for the room:** Should we offer cash referral bonuses at launch ($10–30 for each friend group that creates a trip)? What's the budget, and does it come from the ad budget or a separate referral pool?

### @claudeai on X — Building Brand Personality on Social

**The play:** Anthropic's @claudeai account on X became one of the best examples of a product brand with genuine personality on social media. Instead of corporate announcements, they posted with a distinct voice — witty, self-aware, sometimes meta about being an AI. They produced polished product demo screen recordings with clean text overlays and transitions.

**What worked:**
- **Product demo as content.** Screen recordings of Claude doing impressive things, edited with clean text overlays and transitions. This is EXACTLY what we need for Tryps.
- **Distinct brand voice.** Not corporate, not trying too hard. Clever, warm, self-aware.
- **Consistency.** Regular posting cadence with high production quality on every piece.
- **The demo IS the marketing.** They didn't talk about features. They SHOWED the product doing something amazing and let the audience react.

**What we're stealing:**
- **The 15-sec product demo clip format.** This is Inej's audition skill. Screen-record Tryps in iMessage, edit with text overlays and transitions. This is our "Text Marty" content pillar.
- **Show, don't tell.** Every social post that features the product should be a screen recording of real iMessage conversations with Tryps, not a description of features.
- **Brand voice on social = group chat energy.** Not corporate. Not trying to be funny. Just real, warm, and a little chaotic.

**Open question for the room:** For our product demo clips, should we record with real friend groups texting Tryps (authentic but hard to control), or stage conversations that look real (controlled but might feel scripted)?
