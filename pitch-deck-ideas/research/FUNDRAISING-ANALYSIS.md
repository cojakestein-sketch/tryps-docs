## The Three Questions Every Scenario Must Answer

Every report converged on the same three existential questions. The right raise amount is whichever one gives you the best chance to answer them:

### 1. Does the product work?
Can strangers (not friends, not testers) plan and book a real trip through Tryps?

### 2. Does the viral loop work?
Is K-factor > 0.3? Does every trip invite convert to a new user at a rate that makes growth self-sustaining?

### 3. Does the revenue model work?
Do people book through Tryps, or do they plan on Tryps and book on Expedia? Is the planning-to-booking gap solvable?

## What Each Amount Gets You (and Doesn't)

### $500K: Bridge to Proof
**Gets you:** App Store launch, K-factor measurement, 5-10K users, first revenue signal, MIT network activation.
**Doesn't get you:** Growth hire, marketing budget, FTE conversions, protection against key-person risk (Asif leaving = death). No room for a single mistake.

### $1.5M: Answers the Three Questions
**Gets you:** FTE retention for Asif + Nadeem (solving key-person risk), a growth manager (solving "who runs growth"), $180K in marketing spend, 14-16 months to prove all three questions, data to raise a $5-8M Seed.
**Doesn't get you:** US engineers, a design hire, paid social at scale. Still dependent on organic + creator growth.

### $3M: Builds the Company
**Gets you:** 2-3 US senior engineers, Growth Lead, Product Designer, $420K marketing, 15-17 months runway, professional team that can operate while Jake is at MIT.
**Doesn't get you:** 100K users (realistically 25-50K), profitability, protection from a failed viral loop. Creates Series A expectations that require hitting 25K+ MAU.

### $5M: Bets on Speed
**Gets you:** Full US team, VP Eng, Head of Growth, $880K marketing, campus program at 25 schools, margin for one pivot.
**Doesn't get you:** Certainty. Creates a valuation trap ($25M post → needs $75M Series A). 15-20% chance of actually raising this amount pre-launch.

### Bootstrap: Preserves Everything
**Gets you:** 100% equity, forced focus, MIT as free accelerator ($75-100K non-dilutive), discipline, ability to raise later at 3-5x higher valuation.
**Doesn't get you:** Speed, a growth function, FTE retention, protection against a funded competitor. Year 1 revenue covers only 10-25% of costs.

## The Recurring Themes Across All Five Reports

### 1. The Planning-to-Booking Gap Is Existential
Every single report flags this as the #1 risk. TripAdvisor converts 2-5% of planners to bookers. If Tryps can't beat that significantly, the revenue model fails regardless of how much you raise. The iMessage agent's ability to push bookings in-conversation ("Greece it is — want me to book flights for $287?") is the differentiator, but it's unproven.

**Implication:** Don't over-invest in growth before proving booking conversion works. The first $1 of booking revenue matters more than the first 10K users.

### 2. K-Factor Is the Unlock
At K=0.2, you need $300K-$1M in marketing to reach 100K users. At K=0.8, you need $50-100K. The difference between these two scenarios is the difference between needing $3M and needing $500K. Proving K-factor early is the single highest-leverage activity.

**Implication:** Whatever you raise, the first 3-4 months should be about nailing the invite flow and measuring K. Marketing spend before K-factor data is waste.

### 3. Asif Is the Linchpin
Asif built the iMessage agent, manages the dev team, and coordinates across all 13 scopes. Every report identifies losing Asif as a 3-6 month setback. He's a contractor with no equity and no formal retention mechanism.

**Implication:** Solving Asif's retention (FTE conversion + equity) should happen within 30 days of any raise. This is non-negotiable at every funding level.

### 4. The MIT Timing Is a Feature
MIT provides $75-100K in non-dilutive capital (delta v + Sandbox), a campus of ideal target users (grad students who travel), warm intros to Boston/NYC VCs, credibility signal, and a co-founder recruiting ground. Every report identifies MIT as net positive for fundraising.

**Implication:** Time the raise to close before MIT (August 2026). Arrive at MIT with capital and a launched product.

### 5. Solo Founder Risk Is Real but Solvable
Every report flags this. The solution isn't raising more money — it's finding a co-founder or Head of Product at MIT who can share the operational load. Raising $5M with a solo founder doesn't solve the problem; it amplifies it.

## Recommendation: Raise $1.5M Pre-Seed

### Why $1.5M

**$500K is too lean.** It solves none of the structural problems: Asif has no retention, there's no growth function, and there's no marketing budget. You arrive at month 12 with 5-10K users and need to immediately raise again — from a position of "we survived" not "we're winning."

**$3M is too ambitious for pre-launch.** The 50-60% probability of raising and the $12-15M valuation create expectations that require hitting 25K+ MAU in 12 months. If you hit 15K MAU (a great outcome by most standards), you're in a dead zone for the Series A. The hybrid team model (US + offshore) also introduces management complexity that a solo founder at MIT shouldn't take on yet.

**$5M is unrealistic.** 15-20% probability. The valuation trap is dangerous.

**Bootstrapping is viable but fragile.** If Jake has $75K+ in personal runway AND gets MIT delta v, it works. But it caps growth at 1.5-3K users in year 1, which may not be enough signal to raise a Seed later. And it leaves the Asif retention risk unsolved.

**$1.5M is the Goldilocks amount.** It:
- Converts Asif and Nadeem to FTE with equity (solves retention risk)
- Funds a growth/community manager (solves "who runs growth")
- Provides $180K in marketing spend (enough to measure K-factor with statistical significance)
- Gives 14-16 months of runway (launch + 6 months of growth data + 4-month buffer to raise Seed)
- Targets $7.5M post-money (defensible valuation, doesn't create Series A pressure)
- Is raiseable in 4-8 weeks from pre-seed VCs + angels

### Recommended Round Structure

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Instrument** | Post-money SAFE (YC standard) |
| **Valuation cap** | $7.5M post-money |
| **Total raise** | $1.5M |
| **Dilution** | 20% |
| **Lead** | $500-750K from a pre-seed VC |
| **Fill** | $750K-$1M from angels + syndicates |
| **Pro-rata** | Yes, for $25K+ investors |
| **Board seats** | None (SAFE = no governance) |
| **Option pool** | 10-15% reserved for team equity |
| **Timeline to close** | May-July 2026 (before MIT) |

### Target Investors

**Lead candidates:** Precursor Ventures, 2048 Ventures, Lerer Hippeau, Notation Capital, Hustle Fund, Afore Capital.

**Angels:** Travel industry operators, consumer social founders, MIT alumni, NYC tech community.

**Wildcard: YC Summer 2026.** If accepted, YC's $500K solves half the round and makes the other $1M trivial to fill. The 7% dilution is expensive, but the network + Demo Day + brand signal may be worth it. Apply regardless.

### Fallback: Hybrid Bootstrap

If the $1.5M raise takes longer than expected or valuations aren't right:

1. Cut to $12.5K/month burn immediately
2. Raise a quiet $150K angel SAFE at $7M cap (2.1% dilution)
3. Pursue MIT delta v ($75K equity-free)
4. Bootstrap to revenue on ~$225K combined runway
5. Raise a proper Seed at month 12 with revenue data at a much higher valuation

This preserves 98% equity vs. 80% with the full pre-seed.

## Decision Framework

Use this to pressure-test the recommendation:

| If this is true... | Then raise... |
|---------------------|--------------|
| Jake has <$50K personal runway | $1.5M (can't afford bootstrap risk) |
| Jake has $100K+ personal runway | $1.5M still preferred, but bootstrap is viable fallback |
| YC accepts Tryps (S26 batch) | Take YC ($500K) + raise remaining $1M from angels |
| A top VC offers $3M at $15M+ cap | Take it (don't leave money on the table at fair terms) |
| No institutional lead after 8 weeks | Pivot to hybrid bootstrap ($150K angels + MIT delta v) |
| K-factor < 0.2 after first 5K users | Doesn't matter what you raised — fix the product, not the spend |
