## The Concept

Jake Stein walks through the streets of New York City and explains how he just fixed travel planning forever. The tone is Dollar Shave Club meets AllState Mayhem — Jake is dry, confident, and cracks a few jokes himself, but the real comedy comes from what's happening around him. Every person he passes is suffering through the old way of planning a trip. Jake is the only calm one because he has Marty.

90 seconds. One continuous walk. One pitch. Chaos everywhere except in Jake's pocket.

---

## The Gag Beats

As Jake walks and talks, he passes real New Yorkers in real situations:

- **The Stoop Friends** — A group of friends sitting on a stoop on a beautiful Saturday. Not going anywhere. Jake gestures: *"They've been trying to plan a trip to Europe since October."*

- **The Old Person on Hold** — Someone on a bench, phone on speaker, tinny hold music playing. Been waiting on a travel agent for 45 minutes. Jake doesn't even need to say anything. The visual says it.

- **The Nepo Girl** — A girl on the sidewalk yelling at her mom on the phone, trying to get her to plan the trip for her. Jake walks right past.

- **The Couple** — Two people sitting together, clearly not speaking. Jake: *"These two were supposed to go on a romantic trip to Paris two years ago."*

- **The Cabo Guys** — A group of guys looking stressed, huddled around one phone. Jake: *"These guys booked flights to Cabo. That's it. No hotel. Nothing else planned. They leave tomorrow."*

- **NYC Chaos** — Classic city moments woven throughout — Jake almost gets clipped by a cab, rerouted by scaffolding, stared down by a pigeon — and he spins each one into a travel analogy without breaking stride.

---

## The Ending

Jake reveals that while this entire commercial was being shot, he texted Marty and booked a real trip. A cab pulls up. He gets in. He sticks his head out the window and yells one final line at the camera as the cab drives away. End card: **Text Marty. Go while you can.**

## The Bigger Picture — Four-Layer Video Strategy

This launch video is the centerpiece, but it's one piece of a rollout designed like a movie release:

1. **Launch Video** — Jake's founder walk-and-talk (this concept). Day one.
2. **Giveaway Video** — Drops the day after launch. Separate production ($2-3K).
3. **Photo Campaign** — Rolls out across the first three weeks.
4. **UGC Wave** — Creators posting their own content, extending the launch beyond a single moment.
