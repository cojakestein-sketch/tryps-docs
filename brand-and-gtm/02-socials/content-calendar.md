# Tryps 3-Week Social Media Content Calendar

**Launch Date:** May 2, 2026
**Calendar Window:** April 11 - May 1, 2026 (3 weeks pre-launch)
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram, X (primary) / YT Shorts, Facebook, LinkedIn, Snapchat, Pinterest (secondary)
**Total Posts:** 45 unique pieces (15 per platform)
**Cadence:** 5 posts/week per platform

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. "Text Marty" (Product Demos)
Show the magic. Screen recordings of real group chat conversations with Marty. The phone is always in someone's hand. The group chat is always chaotic. Marty always delivers. These are the money posts.

### 2. "The Group Chat" (Relatable Memes + Chaos)
Every group has one friend who plans everything and five who say "I'm down for whatever." Mine that tension. Screenshots, green-text-vs-blue-text energy, group chat dynamics everyone recognizes. These posts don't sell the app. They sell the feeling.

### 3. "Go While You Can" (Trip Inspiration + Urgency)
Beautiful destinations. But not travel-blog beautiful. Real-trip beautiful. The photo your friend took at 2am on the hotel balcony. The one good sunset pic from a parking lot. Always paired with a caption that makes you feel like you're wasting your twenties.

### 4. "Jake Builds" (Founder Story + Behind the Scenes)
Jake talking to camera, building in public, showing the messy middle. Not polished startup content. Real talk about why he built this, what broke, what surprised him. This pillar owns X and shows up on TikTok.

### 5. "Your Friends Are Waiting" (Social Proof + UGC)
Reposts, testimonials, screenshots from beta users. "My group chat just planned a whole trip in 4 minutes." DMs from people who used it. When you don't have UGC yet, manufacture it from the beta group. After launch, this becomes your biggest pillar.

## Platform-Specific Strategy

### TikTok Strategy

**What works:**
- Screen recordings of real iMessage conversations (native, authentic, curiosity-driven)
- POV format ("POV: you add Marty to the group chat")
- Relatable group chat content (universal pain point, high share rate)
- Talking head founder content with a hook in the first 2 seconds
- Trending sounds layered under original content (not lip-syncing to trends)

**Sound strategy:**
- Week 1: Lean on trending sounds for discovery. Melancholy/funny for group chat content. Indie acoustic for trip inspiration.
- Week 2: Mix trending and original audio. Product demos should use notification sounds and natural reactions, not music.
- Week 3: High energy. Building, dramatic. The countdown should feel like something is coming.

**Posting times:** 11am, 5pm, or 8pm ET (test all three in week 1, double down on winner)

**Hook rules:**
- First frame must create a question. "POV: you add a travel agent to the group chat" makes people stay.
- Never open with the brand name. Open with the problem or the surprise.
- Text overlay in the first second. People scroll with sound off.

**Format split:** 60% screen recordings/demos, 25% relatable meme content, 15% talking head

### Instagram Strategy

**Grid plan (9 posts by end of week 2):**
- Row 1 (top, posted last): Text Marty demo (reel), "Your friends are waiting" (red static), Jake founder (carousel)
- Row 2 (middle): Group chat screenshot (static), Trip inspiration carousel, Marty Austin demo (reel)
- Row 3 (bottom, posted first): Go While You Can carousel, Group chat memes (static), Jake intro (carousel)

**The grid should read as:** alternating between warm lifestyle content, bold red/white brand posts, and screen recording demos. When someone lands on the profile, they should immediately understand: this is about group trips, it's in your iMessage, and it launches May 2.

**Feed vs Stories vs Reels:**
- Feed: Carousels (educational, shareable) and grid anchor statics (bold, branded)
- Reels: All video content, repurposed from TikTok with IG-specific crops and captions
- Stories: Daily, informal. Polls ("what trip has your group chat been 'planning' the longest?"), countdowns, behind-the-scenes of Jake building, reshares of any mentions. Stories do not count toward the 15 feed posts.

**Carousel strategy:** Tryps carousels should feel like scrolling through a friend's text messages, not a brand deck. Use iMessage bubble styling. Warm cream backgrounds. DM Sans only. End every carousel with a CTA slide on Tryps red.

### X (Twitter) Strategy

**Jake's voice:**
- First person. Founder energy. Not corporate. Not "we're excited to announce."
- Talk like you're texting a friend about what you built.
- Short sentences. One thought per tweet. Threads only for launch week explainers.
- Reply to EVERY comment in weeks 1-2. This is where community gets built.

**Engagement plays:**
- Ask questions that people can't resist answering ("what trip has your group chat been planning the longest?")
- Quote-tweet your own product demos with a one-liner reaction
- Engage with travel content, startup content, and group chat memes in the replies of other accounts
- DM travel creators and founders a link to the demo video before it's public

**Thread strategy (launch week only):**
- One explainer thread (X12) that becomes the pinned tweet
- Keep it to 4-5 tweets max. No 20-tweet threads.

**Format split:** 60% text only (high engagement), 25% text + video, 15% text + image
