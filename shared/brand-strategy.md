## 1. Brand Purpose

**We exist to turn group chats into group trips.**

Tryps exists because everyone wants the trip to be amazing but people have wildly different levels of effort they want to put into planning. Some want to control every detail, some want to show up and be surprised. Everyone wants their viewpoint heard — they just don't all want to do the work.

Tryps lets the whole group communicate their vibe, and then it handles the rest.

### Origin Story

Planning a trip to Chile with my roommate Quinn. Quinn wanted to plan every detail — Google Docs, Notion, AI research scattered everywhere. I wanted to show up spontaneously. There was no way to consolidate everything, and no easy way for me (the reluctant participant) to engage with what Quinn (the ultra-planner) was trying to get the group to do. I saw the Partiful playbook and thought: nobody's done this for trips.

### Mission

Make group trip planning effortless by democratizing the process — so planning becomes the group's shared experience, not one person's burden.

### Vision

Tryps is the one-stop shop for all things travel. Anything relating to you leaving your hometown and going somewhere else goes through Tryps. Logo on the side of a plane.

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement

**For the post-grad who just wants to see her friends back home, Tryps is the way she gets to see them all — unlike Expedia or Kayak, which are inherently not social, and unlike group chats, where plans get buried and nothing gets done.**

### Category We Own

**Trip planning and going on.** Not just planning — the full lifecycle from vibe to booking to being there to remembering it.

### One-Liner

Partiful but for travel — group trip planning that replaces the scattered mess of iMessage + Google Docs + Splitwise.

### The Shift

Tryps moves trip planning from **one person carries the burden** to **everyone contributes a little**. That single shift is worth the download alone.

### What Only Tryps Does

Only Tryps lets you actually plan and go on the trip from your group chat.

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What They Do Well | Where They Fall Short |
|------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Group chats (iMessage, WhatsApp)** | Easy, natural, everyone's already there | Nothing gets logged. Plans get buried and lost. |
| **Spreadsheets (Google Sheets)** | Organized, scalable, shareable | Not fun. Nobody wants to open a spreadsheet for their vacation. |
| **Partiful** | Social coordination done right | Can't text it. Hard to see what's actually happening beyond "show up at this time." |
| **Expedia / Kayak** | Book everything in one place | Not social. Not easy to share with other people. |

### Positioning Guardrails

- **Never be compared to:** Splitwise, Tricount, or any expense-splitting utility. Those are ledgers. Tryps is a lifestyle brand.
- **Never position as:** A project management tool, a productivity app, or a "travel hack" service.
- **Always position as:** The obvious way your group plans a trip. Inevitable, not novel.

## 3. Brand Values

These are real operating principles, not wall art.

### 1. Sexy & Clean
The app looks so good your coolest friends want to be seen using it. If it doesn't look like something you'd screenshot, it's not done.

### 2. Shareworthy
You feel cool sending it. Sharing Tryps is a flex, not an ask. The trip card is the viral hook — you HAVE to get on Tryps because you want to go on that trip.

### 3. Obvious
A five-year-old and a grandmother both get it instantly. Nothing needs explanation. The reaction we want: "Oh of course this exists."

### 4. Come Correct
Every touchpoint is intentional. Nothing half-assed. From the copy to the colors to the push notification — we meant to do that. This is not a gimmick. This is a best-in-class service.

### What We'd Never Compromise

Everyone uses Tryps for free. Always. A pro plan may exist eventually, but the core experience — planning, vibing, coordinating with your people — is never paywalled. Getting someone into Tryps has zero friction, zero cost.

## 4. Brand Archetype

### Primary: The Host (70%)

Warm, inviting, makes everyone feel welcome. Creates the space where the magic happens. Not the center of attention — the reason attention gathers. The brand essence is **"Welcome."**

*Why not Explorer?* Explorer is about solo discovery and freedom. Tryps is about bringing people together. The trip matters because of who you're with, not where you're going.

### Secondary: The Magician (30%)

Makes the complex feel effortless. "I just texted my friends and all of a sudden we're on a trip together." The invisible orchestration — logistics disappear, connection appears.

*Why not Jester?* Tryps has humor and playfulness, but it's not the class clown. The humor serves warmth and relatability, not entertainment. Cheeky, not silly.

### Archetype in Action

| Moment | The Host | The Magician |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Someone gets invited | "Welcome — you're in." | The trip card is so good they have to join. |
| Planning starts | Everyone's voice matters. | You just say the vibe, the rest happens. |
| During the trip | "Where am I supposed to be?" — answered. | Itinerary updates feel seamless. |
| After the trip | Memories preserved, shared. | Recommendations generated from what you loved. |

## 5. Brand Personality

### We Are

- **Effortless** — makes hard things feel easy
- **Inviting** — warm, never exclusive
- **Obvious** — of course this exists
- **Undeniable** — this is clearly the way to do it
- **Real** — authentic, never performing

### We Are Not

- **Corporate** — no enterprise energy, no jargon
- **Difficult** — if it feels like work, we failed
- **Gimmicky** — not "look at this trick." A pristine, best-in-class service.
- **Precious** — not a luxury brand, not aspirational travel porn
- **Pushy** — never spammy, never desperate for attention

### If Tryps Were a Person at a Party

The most social person there. Friendly — that's the big thing. Dressed sleek, looks good, but not gaudy or designer. More utility-chic — Arc'teryx, Patagonia vibe. Not trying to show off. Just wants connection, wants to see their friends. Talks in a way that's inviting. You walk away thinking "I want to hang out with that person again."

### Celebrity Analog

**Anthony Bourdain.** Traveled everywhere, genuinely curious, warm, no pretense, loved connection over luxury. Made you want to go places and meet people — not because the places were fancy, but because the people were interesting.

## 6. Voice & Tone

### Voice (consistent — who we always are)

Tryps sounds like a text from your most organized friend — the one who's somehow fun AND has their shit together. Lowercase energy. Warm. Direct. Short sentences.

### Tone Spectrums

```
Formal    ●●○○○○○○○○  Casual        → Leans casual (but not sloppy)
Serious   ●●○○○○○○○○  Playful       → Leans playful (but not childish)
Respectful ●●●○○○○○○○  Irreverent   → Stays respectful (warm, not edgy)
Enthusiastic ●●●○○○○○○○  Matter-of-fact → Stays enthusiastic (but grounded)
```

### Tone Modulation by Context

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Social / in-app** | Most playful | "beach day won (4 votes to 1) — what day should we plan it for?" |
| **Onboarding** | Warmest | "welcome — what do you want this trip to feel like?" |
| **Payments / billing** | More trustworthy | "your card was charged $127.50 for the Airbnb deposit" |
| **Errors** | Lighthearted, quirky | Not apologetic or corporate — human and quick to fix |
| **Notifications** | Functional + personality | Feels like a text, not a system alert. Never spammy. |

### Voice North Star

> Every piece of Tryps copy should make the reader feel: "This is not some tacky gimmicky service. This is the way everybody is planning their trip this summer." **Legitimacy + inevitability.**

### Words We Use

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| your friends | Personal, warm |
| your people | Inclusive alternative |
| trip | Simple, universal |
| vibe | Captures the low-effort input we ask for |
| plan | What we help do |

| easy / effortless | Core value prop |

### Words We Never Use

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| crew | Jake hates it. Full stop. |
| journey | Too precious. We're going to Cabo, not finding ourselves. |
| adventure | Same energy as journey. Over-romanticized. |
| users | They're people, friends, travelers at most — never "users." |
| travelers | A bit formal and identity-projecting. Just "people" or "friends." |
| explore | Every travel brand says this. Meaningless. |
| unlock | Growth-hack energy. No. |
| hack | See above. |
| seamless | Overused, says nothing. Show don't tell. |
| leverage | Corporate jargon. |
| curated | Pretentious. |

### Copy Rules

- Lowercase in notifications and conversational UI. Sentence case in formal contexts.
- Short, punchy copy — group chat energy.
- Okay to be funny, never mean.
- No emojis or slang in agent/system copy — looks tacky.
- Notifications: blend of functional and personality, NEVER spammy.

## 8. Value Proposition

### Primary Value Prop (one sentence)

**Tryps turns your group chat into an actual trip — just communicate the vibe and it plans the rest.**

### Three Supporting Messages

**1. Everyone gets a voice without doing the work.**
You don't have to be the planner to have opinions. Say what you want the trip to feel like, vote on the stuff that matters, and Tryps handles the logistics for the whole group.

**2. It lives where you already are.**
Add Tryps to your iMessage group chat. No new app to learn, no spreadsheet to maintain, no "okay who's checking Airbnb." Everything your group texts about gets logged to a real itinerary.

**3. The trip starts the moment you open it.**
Tryps doesn't feel like a project management tool. It feels like the trip has already started — beautiful trip cards, friend activity, and a shared plan that actually gets people excited to go.

## 9. Tagline Candidates

Tagline must encode: effortlessness, group dynamic, legitimacy. Should feel inevitable, not clever.

### Shortlist (Top 3 — review with Trent)

| # | Tagline | Role | Notes |
|---|---------|------|-------|
| 1 | **Less planning. More going.** | Tagline | Cleanest. Works at every size — App Store, billboard, social bio. Universal. |
| 2 | **For every "we should totally do that."** | Campaign line | Instant recognition. Everyone has that group chat. Most emotionally specific. |
| 3 | **The trip you've been talking about? Go on it.** | Campaign line | Direct, activating. Feels like a friend calling you out. |

**Recommendation:** #1 as the tagline (works everywhere, scales down to an icon caption). #2 and #3 as campaign/launch lines — they're too long for a tagline but perfect for launch video, social, App Store hero copy.

### Other Candidates (Explored, Not Selected)

| Tagline | Why it didn't make the cut |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Send the vibe. We'll plan the trip. | Mechanically accurate but doesn't hit emotionally. |
| Trips your friends actually go on. | Decent but reads slightly negative. |
| Where your group chat goes on vacation. | Fun but too long for a tagline. |
| Just say where. We handle the rest. | Generic — could be any service app. |
| Finally, a reason to actually go. | Close but "a reason" undersells it. |
| From group chat to group trip. | Descriptive, not evocative. |
| Stop planning. Start going. | Too close to Nike territory. Commanding tone feels off-brand. |

## 10. Target Audience

### Primary Persona: The Group Chat CEO

**Demographics:**
- 24-30 years old, post-grad
- Has some money after college, not wealthy
- More social than average, travels 2-4x/year
- Has friends who do NOT live in the same city
- Plans trips across dispersed friend groups

**Psychographics:**
- Already the planner — organizes dinners, creates shared albums, sends the "okay so here's the plan" text
- Has taste but doesn't perform it. Picks quality over hype, function over systems, social over private.
- Culturally aware without being pretentious. Effortlessly good, not obviously expensive.
- Weekend: social, probably drinking with friends, trying to go somewhere cool

**Apps on home screen:** Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok, Twitter, Partiful, Canva, Kayak/Expedia

**Brands they identify with:**
- Fashion: Aritzia, Aimé Leon Dore, Patagonia, New Balance
- Food: Sweetgreen, natural wine, Blue Bottle
- Tech: Chrome, Apple Notes (function-first, not a systems nerd)
- Social: Instagram, Spotify, Strava, Venmo, Partiful
- Media: A24, The Ringer
- Travel: Marriott (pragmatic — points matter)

**Communication:** iMessage, primarily. Group texts are where life is organized.

### Secondary Personas (Role Cards)

| Role | Description | What They Need from Tryps |
|------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **The Reluctant Participant** | Happy to be invited, wants to contribute without doing work | Zero-friction engagement. Say the vibe, done. |
| **The Happy Joiner** | Excited to be there, goes with the flow | Easy access to the plan. "Where am I supposed to be?" |
| **The Co-Planner** | Didn't start the trip but secretly wants to help plan everything | Tools to contribute without stepping on the planner's toes. |

### Anti-Persona

No explicit anti-persona. Tryps is for everybody. But the brand should never pander to:
- Corporate travel managers
- Solo backpacker purists who want no structure
- Luxury travel influencers performing aspiration

## 11. Aha Moments

Two moments where someone goes from curious to committed:

### 1. The iMessage Agent
You add this agent to iMessage and it's actually your travel agent on your behalf. That's the "holy shit this is real" moment. The tech disappears — you're just texting, and a trip is forming.

### 2. The Trip Card
A beautiful Partiful-style card that says "so-and-so wants to go on a trip to New York" and you're like "oh fuck yeah, I want in." The card is the viral hook — shareworthy, beautiful, makes you HAVE to get on Tryps.

Spontaneity, fun, and reconnection.

## 13. Visual Direction Inputs

> These inputs feed the Direction Deck (Phase 3). They are strategic constraints, not design decisions.

### Color

- **Tryps Red `#D9071C`** is the hero. Warm, not aggressive. From the logo.
- Warm palette overall. Saturated primary, muted supporting colors.
- Backgrounds should feel like warm passport paper — not random beige.
- Dark mode stays warm. No cool grays, no pure black.
- **Avoid:** Purple. Ugly green.
- **Likes:** Partiful's palette, Airbnb's palette. **Dislikes:** TripIt ("so ugly"), Splitwise ("so ugly").

### Typography

- Editorial warmth meets clean utility.
- Serif + sans pairing inspired by Wispr Flow: EB Garamond (display) + Figtree (body/UI).
- Current codebase uses Plus Jakarta Sans everywhere — this would be a significant departure.
- Space Mono stays for numbers/dates/prices.
- English only at launch, but should support multiple languages eventually.

### Photography & Imagery

- **Film camera aesthetic is the anchor.** Candid, disposable camera energy. Grain, warmth, real moments.
- More relatable than aspirational. Real friends, real chaos.
- Both photography and illustration, but film photography is the core identity.
- Brand play: first 100+ trips get a Tryps-branded disposable camera mailed to them. They take it on the trip, send it back, Tryps develops the film. "Do shit that doesn't scale."
- Core demo in imagery: social people in their 20s, just graduated, authentic.

### Iconography & Motifs

- Existing icon guide on Figma that Jake likes.
- Wants motifs but nothing too obvious — "a lot of this travel stuff has been done before."
- A flying plane exists as a motif already.
- Needs an original Tryps motif — not map pins, not compass roses.
- Signature pattern/texture: yes, but designer should propose options.

### App Icon

- Lowercase "t" that encodes the function of the app — like Wispr (W = flow), Airbnb (A = people linking), Strava (S = mountains).
- The "t" needs to feel like it's going somewhere. Open brief for the designer.

## 14. Content & Social Strategy Inputs

### Content Pillars

1. **User stories** — real people, real trips, real reconnections
2. **Normal people doing amazing things** — pulled off incredible trips because the app made it easy
3. **Trip inspiration** — framed as "bringing people together," not aspirational travel porn
4. **Destination guides** — lighter touch

### Channel Priority

1. Twitter (launch video) — more tech-focused tone
2. Instagram — more polished
3. TikTok — more raw
4. Pinterest
5. Everything else

### Social Rules

- Brand-led, not founder-led. Follow the Wispr Flow playbook.
- Instagram grid stays pristine — only Tryps-produced content. UGC goes in story highlights.
- Organic posts: trip-first, Tryps-second. Product is embedded in the story, not the headline.
- Content ratio: ~50/50 product and lifestyle (validate with content advisor).
- Both original content and seeded UGC.

### Community

- Community identity: yes. Hashtag strategy: feels played out. Naming: TBD.
- **NEVER** use the word "crew."

## 15. Brand Success (12-Month Vision)

### What Success Looks Like

Everybody knows what Tryps is because they've been invited to a group chat with a trip. We own the category. Users describe us as "the only way to go on a trip."

### The One Word

When people hear "Tryps," they think: **Welcome.**

### The Elevator Pitch

It's the easiest way for your group to plan a trip together — you just communicate the vibe and it helps plan the trip for you. It can book stuff, help facilitate what you should do, and plan the itinerary for the group.
