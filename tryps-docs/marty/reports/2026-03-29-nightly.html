4. Deep Dive: The Two-Pillar Thesis

From Jake's 11:30 PM brain dump tonight: "The whole product is literally just making it easy for people to grill me on their trip, and then letting them AB test it for their entire group with native polling. If we make that we win."

What it is

Two pillars that make Tryps undeniable:

  1. Grill the AI about your trip — ask anything, get great answers. Zero friction.
  2. AB test with your group via native polling — every answer becomes a poll. One tap.

The magic is the handoff. Ask → get options → one tap and the whole group is voting.

Why it maps to this week

The voice call initiative (Rizwan) is Pillar 1 taken to its extreme — you don't even need to type, you just call and grill the AI by talking. The travel booking (Asif) is what makes the answers real — you don't just get flight suggestions, you get bookable flights. And clean onboarding (Nadeem) is what makes Pillar 2 possible — if the app doesn't work e2e, there's no group to poll.

Why no other app does this

  • Partiful — events, not trips. No AI, no polling on trip decisions.
  • Google Trips (RIP) — solo planning. No group collaboration layer.
  • WhatsApp polls — manual. No AI generating the options. No trip context.
  • TripIt, Wanderlog — itinerary organizers, not decision engines.
  • ChatGPT / Claude — great at Q&A but no native group polling. No trip state.

Tryps is the only product where "research" and "group decision" happen in one motion.

What to build toward

  • Every AI answer should have a "Poll the group" button. One tap → native poll.
  • Polls should present 2-3 AI-generated options side by side (not just yes/no).
  • The 60% "I Don't Care-ers" only need to vote on polls. They never need to ask the AI anything.
  • The 10% Ultimate Planners curate which AI answers become polls. They drive the trip.
  • Track the funnel: questions asked → polls created → votes received → decisions made.

8. Tools Highlight

Weekend wildcard — voice AI platforms (relevant to this week's directive).

Vapi — Voice AI Platform for Developers

What it is: Developer-first platform for building AI voice agents. Handles STT → LLM → TTS pipeline. Supports custom LLM backends (including Claude).

How Jake would use it: This is the fastest path to "call Tryps." Vapi handles the telephony layer (real phone number, WebRTC, SIP), transcription, and voice synthesis. Rizwan plugs in the Tryps agent as the LLM backend. User calls a number → Vapi handles audio → sends text to agent → agent responds → Vapi speaks it back. Could have a prototype running in hours, not days.

Pricing: Pay-per-minute. ~$0.05-0.15/min depending on STT/TTS providers chosen. Free tier for dev.

→ vapi.ai

Retell AI — Low-Latency Voice Agents

What it is: Voice AI platform optimized for natural conversation. ~600ms response time (fastest in category). SOC 2, HIPAA compliant.

How Jake would use it: If Vapi's latency isn't good enough for the "call Tryps" experience, Retell is the alternative. Their edge: responses feel instant. For a travel booking conversation where you're going back and forth ("What about Wednesday instead? How much cheaper is a red-eye?"), low latency = natural conversation = magic.

Pricing: Pay-as-you-go, no platform fees. Competitive with Vapi.

→ retellai.com
