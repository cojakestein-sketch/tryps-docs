## Executive Summary

Raising $5M at seed for a pre-launch, pre-revenue consumer app is extremely ambitious but not unprecedented. Poke raised $15M at seed with 6,000 beta users at a $100M valuation. BeReal raised $30M Series A with 300K DAU. Partiful raised $20M Series A1 at $140M with 500K MAU.

The honest answer: $5M is possible but requires a specific type of investor who bets on vision + demo + founder + market size. It is NOT the default outcome for a pre-launch consumer startup. The default outcome is $1.5-3M. To get to $5M, you need either (a) a tier-1 VC who falls in love with the demo and believes in the AI-native consumer thesis, or (b) a strong lead at $3M who gets competitive pressure from a second interested party.

This document covers the full case: what $5M buys, whether you can sustain it, what milestones it enables, and what kills you.

### Critical Hiring Decisions

**1. Do You Hire a CTO?**

Yes, but not immediately. The title should be "VP Engineering" or "Head of Engineering" initially. Here's why:

- At seed, "CTO" implies a cofounder-level commitment and equity grant (2-5%). You want someone who can own technical architecture and manage the team, but labeling them CTO at seed means they'll expect cofounder-level equity and decision-making authority.
- What you actually need: a senior engineer (10+ years, has built consumer products at scale) who can (a) architect the system for 100K+ users, (b) manage the offshore developers, (c) make infrastructure decisions (stay on Supabase vs. migrate to own Postgres, etc.), and (d) hire and level engineers.
- Compensation: $180-220K salary + 1-2% equity. In NYC, this is below-market for a true CTO but appropriate for a VP Eng at seed.
- Timeline: Hire in months 2-3. This is your single most important hire.

**2. Do You Hire a Head of Growth?**

Yes, and this is your second most important hire after VP Eng.

- The Partiful playbook: their 3-person marketing team drove 400% YoY MAU growth. You need someone who can replicate that playbook for travel.
- Profile: 5-8 years experience, has scaled a consumer app from 0 to 100K+ users, understands creator programs, campus marketing, and viral loops. Ideally from Partiful, BeReal, Locket, Bumble, or similar.
- Compensation: $140-170K + 0.5-1% equity.
- Timeline: Hire in months 3-4, after the product is stable enough to market.

**3. What About the Existing Offshore Team?**

This is the hardest decision. The current team (Asif, Nadeem, Rizwan) has built the product. They know the codebase. But at $5M, investors will expect US-based senior engineering talent. The right move:

- **Keep the offshore team as contractors through months 1-6.** They maintain velocity while you hire US engineers.
- **Transition the best performer(s) to full-time** if they want it and the relationship works at scale.
- **Do NOT fire the offshore team to hire US replacements.** That destroys institutional knowledge. Layer US seniors on top; let offshore team continue executing.
- **Budget reality:** 3 offshore contractors at $5-8K/month = $15-24K/month. 3 US seniors at $12-18K/month = $36-54K/month. Running both costs $51-78K/month for engineering -- sustainable at $5M.

**4. What You Do NOT Need Yet**

- VP of Marketing (Head of Growth is sufficient at seed)
- Head of Sales (no enterprise sales at this stage)
- Data scientist (premature -- you need users first)
- Customer success team (the agent IS the customer success)
- Full-time legal counsel (use a startup law firm on retainer)

**For Tryps at $20-25M post-money, investors need to believe:**

1. Group travel is a $100B+ addressable market (it is -- $1.9T travel market, group travel is ~20-30%)
2. AI agents in messaging are an inevitable interface shift (strong thesis, accelerating)
3. The Partiful comp is valid -- social coordination apps can command 100x+ revenue multiples at early stage
4. Jake can recruit a world-class team in NYC and execute
5. The iMessage moat (Linq partnership, native polling, no-download-needed for invitees) is defensible for 12-18 months
6. The working demo proves the vision is buildable, not theoretical

**Honest assessment: $20M post-money is a stretch for pre-launch, pre-revenue. $15-20M is more realistic.** You might raise $5M at $15-18M post-money (25-33% dilution), which is aggressive dilution but gets you the capital. Alternatively, raise $3-3.5M at $15M post-money (20-23% dilution) and add a $1.5M tranche that converts at Series A terms if you hit milestones.

### Deal Structure

**Priced round vs. SAFE:**

- At $5M, most leads will want a priced round. SAFEs at this size create cap table complexity.
- Priced round means: board seat for the lead investor, information rights, pro-rata rights, standard protective provisions.
- Acceptable terms: 1x non-participating liquidation preference, no cumulative dividends, standard drag-along, ROFR.

**Board composition at $5M:**

- 3-person board: Jake (founder), lead investor, independent (mutual agreement)
- Do NOT agree to a 5-person board at seed. That gives investors outsized control too early.

**Option pool:**

- Investors will insist on a 10-15% unissued option pool included in the pre-money valuation (this effectively increases dilution).
- At $20M post-money with 15% pool: Jake's effective ownership drops from 75% to ~64%.

## 7. Risks and What Kills You

### Risk 1: The Valuation Trap (CRITICAL)

If you raise $5M at $25M post-money, your Series A needs to be at $75M+ post-money (3x step-up is the minimum investors expect). To justify $75M, you need either:
- $3-5M ARR (at 15-25x revenue multiple), or
- 200K+ MAU with strong engagement (at Partiful-level $/MAU of $280), or
- Some combination

If you hit 50K downloads and $500K ARR in 12 months -- good progress but below these thresholds -- you're in a dead zone. Too much traction to shut down, not enough to raise at $75M+. You'd need a flat or down round, which poisons cap table dynamics and morale.

**Mitigation:** Raise at a lower valuation ($15-18M post-money). A $15M-to-$50M step-up is much more achievable than $25M-to-$75M.

### Risk 2: Spending Pressure (HIGH)

$5M creates organizational pressure to spend. With $3M, every dollar is scrutinized. With $5M, it's tempting to hire a 6th engineer "just in case" or run a $50K event that produces nothing. The most dangerous month is month 3-4, when the money feels abundant but product-market fit hasn't been proven yet.

**Mitigation:** Set hard burn rate gates. Do not exceed $200K/month until K-factor is measured above 0.3. Do not exceed $300K/month until you have 5K+ downloads. Put the gates in writing with your board.

### Risk 3: Scaling Too Fast (HIGH)

Going from 11 contractors to 20+ people in 6 months is organizationally brutal. Every new hire needs onboarding, context, and management attention. Jake is currently the sole full-time employee. Adding 8+ people while also doing product strategy, fundraising, and GTM execution is a recipe for burnout and dropped balls.

**Mitigation:** Hire the VP Eng and Head of Growth first. They manage their respective teams. Jake's job shifts from IC to CEO -- product strategy, fundraising, and team building. This transition is itself a risk (many technical founders struggle with it).

### Risk 4: The Planning-to-Booking Gap (EXISTENTIAL)

Your own pressure test (path-to-the-plane.md) identifies this as the single biggest risk. If users love planning in iMessage but book on Expedia/Google Flights, you have a social tool with zero revenue. TripAdvisor's 2-5% planning-to-booking conversion is the cautionary tale.

**Mitigation:** The iMessage agent's ability to push bookings IN the conversation is the key differentiator. "Everyone agreed on Miami. I found flights for $287 round-trip. Want me to book for the group?" This is fundamentally different from TripAdvisor's "here are some links, figure it out." But it only works if the booking experience is frictionless. Stored cards, one-tap confirmation, group payment splitting -- all need to work perfectly.

### Risk 5: iMessage Dependency (MODERATE)

Tryps is built on iMessage, which Apple controls. Apple could:
- Restrict third-party iMessage integrations (they've tightened before)
- Launch their own group trip planning feature
- Change Linq/SendBlue's access terms

This is a real risk but probably not a 12-month risk. Apple moves slowly on messaging features, and restricting third-party services would face regulatory scrutiny in the current antitrust environment.

**Mitigation:** Build the mobile app as a standalone experience that doesn't require iMessage. The agent layer should work across SMS, WhatsApp, and eventually RCS. iMessage is the wedge, not the moat.

### Risk 6: What if Growth Doesn't Come?

If you spend $5M and get to 10K downloads with a K-factor of 0.2, you're in trouble. The math: $1.46M reserve / $150K lean burn = ~10 months of runway to pivot.

What could you pivot to?
- B2B group travel coordination (corporate offsites, retreats)
- Travel agent tooling (sell the agent infrastructure to travel agencies)
- Social trip sharing (pivot from booking to content/sharing, like BeReal for travel)

**Honest assessment: $5M gives you one pivot opportunity. $3M gives you zero.** This is actually an argument FOR raising more -- but only if you have the discipline not to spend it all before you need to pivot.

### What Kills You (In Order of Probability)

1. **Can't convert planning to booking** -- social tool with no revenue (50% probability if not actively solved)
2. **Slow growth + high burn** -- 12K downloads after 12 months, need to raise, no one bites (30% probability)
3. **Team scaling failure** -- key hires don't work out, offshore team leaves, organizational chaos (20% probability)
4. **Apple/iMessage restriction** -- platform risk materializes (10% probability in 12 months)
5. **Competitor launches** -- Partiful adds trips, Airbnb adds group planning, Google adds agent-to-iMessage (15% probability in 12 months)

We need $5M to build the team, prove the viral loop, and get to 100K downloads before incumbents wake up. The window is 12-18 months.
