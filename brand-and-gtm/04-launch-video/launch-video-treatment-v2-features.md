## Logline

Tryps is the travel agent in your group chat. You can book flights, hotels, and activities directly in iMessage. This video shows what travel planning looks like today — chaotic, scattered, or nonexistent — and what it looks like with Tryps: effortless, everywhere, and actually fun.

### BEAT 1 — The Unpaid Travel Agent (0:05 - 0:18)

**Location:** Apartment stoop / stairs outside a building

Jake gestures to a group of friends sitting on the stairs. One person — the designated planner — has their laptop open with a Google Doc, a Notion page, a ChatGPT window, AND a Google Spreadsheet all visible. They look stressed. The rest of the group is on their phones, not helping.

### BEAT 3 — Polling (0:25 - 0:35)

**Location:** NYC sidewalk or park — open space with a small group

A group of 5-6 people standing together. Jake walks up on them mid-decision. They're physically raising their hands — actually voting on something in real life — while simultaneously, we see a close-up of an iMessage poll running on one of their phones.

The physical vote and the digital poll match. Three hands up in real life, three votes on screen.

> "Tryps has native polling built into the group chat. Your friends actually make decisions. In real time. No more 'I'm down for whatever.'"

Creative direction for Sean: This is the most visually inventive beat. The physical-digital mirror should feel fun and slightly absurd — people theatrically raising hands while their phones buzz with votes. Could lean comedic (someone raising both hands, someone voting the opposite of what they said out loud).

### BEAT 4 — However You Get To Us (0:35 - 0:45)

**Location:** NYC street, quick cuts across multiple spots

Quick montage. Different people, different devices — all contacting Tryps. Every device is RED (red phone case, red flip phone, red landline, red Apple Watch band).

- Someone CALLING Tryps on a red rotary phone on a street corner
- Someone texting from a red flip phone
- Someone checking their red Apple Watch
- Someone typing the number digit by digit on a red candy bar phone

> "Tryps is available where you already are."

No other dialogue. Just the montage. The red devices pop against the muted NYC street. This is the "However You Get To Us" concept compressed into 10 seconds. Every phone is red. Nothing else is.

## Production Notes

### What's New vs. Previous Treatments

The v1 Rich Ross treatment was pure comedy walk-and-talk (Jake narrates, gag beats around him). This treatment keeps the walk-and-talk energy but adds **product showcase beats** — every scene demonstrates a real feature. The comedy comes from the contrast between the chaos of today and the ease of Tryps, not from standalone gag beats.

### Key Visual Motifs

1. **Red devices** (Beat 4) — every phone/device that touches Tryps is red. Everything else is NYC-neutral. Direct callback to the "Generational Red" concept and "However You Get To Us."
2. **Physical-digital mirror** (Beat 3) — people physically voting while iMessage polls run. The most creative beat and the one Sean should art-direct the hardest.
3. **Real vs. tourist NYC** (Beat 5) — the split between where tourists go and where Tryps sends you. Needs a genuinely cool location.

### Tone

Dollar Shave Club meets Anthony Bourdain. Jake is dry, confident, walking with purpose. The comedy is observational (pointing at real absurdities of trip planning), not slapstick. The warmth comes from the product actually working and people actually having a good time.

### What Sean Needs to Decide

1. **Jake on camera vs. voiceover** — This draft has Jake walking and talking, on camera the whole time. Could also work as Jake VO over the vignettes (he's never on screen, just narrating what we see). Sean's call.
2. **Banner/sign treatment for "Tryps That Never Happened"** — Physical prop (someone holds a banner) vs. post-produced text overlay vs. chalkboard sign. Physical is funnier. Post is cleaner.
3. **How to shoot the polling beat** — This is the money shot. Needs creative blocking. How many people? How theatrical? Does someone vote wrong? Does someone change their vote?
4. **Location for Beat 5** — Need a genuinely cool spot that reads as "real NYC" on camera. Studio 151, Lucien, a Bushwick rooftop, a hidden West Village courtyard. Sean knows NYC — his pick.
5. **How many of the "should have" features make the cut** — Traveler DNA and Explore are important product features but they slow the pace. 60-second cut might drop Beats 6 and 7 entirely and save them for a separate product video.

### Platform Cuts

| Cut | Runtime | What's In |
|-----|---------|-----------|
| Hero (YouTube, website) | 80-90s | All 7 beats + ending |
| TikTok / Reels | 30-45s | Cold open + Beat 1 + Beat 3 (polling) + Beat 4 (red devices) + ending |
| Pre-roll | 15s | Cold open + Beat 4 (red devices montage) + end card |

### Budget

Same as v1: ~$5,000 for a 1-day guerrilla NYC shoot. The red devices (Beat 4) need sourcing — red phone cases are cheap, a red rotary phone is a prop rental or thrift store find. The "Tryps That Never Happened" banner is a print shop job ($50-100).
