## 1. Anthropic/Claude: One-Person Growth Marketing Machine

Austin Lau was Anthropic's very first growth hire. For 10 months, he was the entire growth marketing team for a $380B company, running paid search, paid social, app stores, email marketing, and SEO solo. Before Claude Code launched, he had never opened a terminal or written a line of code.

### What Austin Built

**Custom Figma Plugin** (built in ~45 min with Claude Code):
- Paste headline copy from a Google Sheet, click one button, get dozens of ad variants across multiple aspect ratios
- Replaced a workflow of: open Figma > copy frame > switch to Google Docs > grab copy > switch back > paste > repeat x10+ per variation
- Cut ad creation from 30 minutes to 30 seconds per batch

**Google Ads RSA Workflow** (slash command `/rsa` in Claude Code):
- Type `/rsa`, Claude asks for campaign data, existing copy, and keywords
- Cross-references inputs against "Agent Skills" Austin created for:
  - Anthropic's brand tone and voice
  - Product accuracy guidelines
  - Google Ads RSA best practices (character counts, etc.)
- Outputs upload-ready CSV with 15 headlines + 4 descriptions per ad
- Austin riffs and refines interactively before exporting

**All copy and examples fed to Claude were originally written in partnership with product marketing and copywriting teams.** Human judgment baked in before Claude even starts brainstorming.

### Austin's Background

Previous: growth marketing at Dropbox, Webflow, Notion. Not an engineer. His first Claude Code project was a simple calculator app just to see how it worked.

### How He Got Started

A colleague posted a guide in Anthropic's internal Slack on how to install Claude Code as a non-technical employee. Austin followed it out of curiosity.

### What Claude Cowork Is

Claude Cowork is Anthropic's collaborative workspace product. You point it at a folder on your computer, tell it what you need, and it reads your files, creates new ones, edits existing ones, and saves everything directly to that folder. Launched with 11 official plugins (Marketing, Sales, Finance, etc.). It connects to Slack and Drive and handles recurring tasks autonomously.

Ole Lehmann set up Cowork the week it launched. Within a month it was the first thing he opens every morning, before email, before Notion.

### Broader Anthropic Marketing Team Impact

- **Influencer Marketing**: Claude writes scripts for influencers and podcasts, freeing 100+ hours/month
- **Customer Marketing**: Case studies drafted in 30 min instead of 2.5 hours (saves 10 hrs/week)
- **Digital Marketing**: Web dev workflows increased team productivity 5x year-over-year
- **Product Marketing**: Launch briefs via Skills and Projects save 5-10 hours per launch
- **Partner Marketing**: Self-serve event enablement for Sales, reduced trade show prep by 40%

### Austin's Advice (Passionfroot AMA)

1. Start with strategy, not execution. Understand business priorities first.
2. Focus on lifecycle marketing before performance marketing.
3. Amplify network effects through referrals/affiliate programs before paid.
4. Invest in SEO early. Compounds over time.
5. Prioritize what you invest in over 30/60/90 day windows.

## 2. Partiful: "Facebook Events for Hot People"

Tryps positions as "Partiful but for travel." Here's exactly how they did it.

### Growth Numbers

- 500K MAU by Q1 2025
- 400% year-over-year growth
- 2 million new users added in first half of 2025 alone
- 25-person company total

### The Core Growth Loop (Product-Led)

Every time someone receives a Partiful invite, they see a fun, designed cover image. If they don't have the app, they're nudged to install it. Classic PLG, but executed with personality that most PLG playbooks completely lack. Every attendee at a Partiful-hosted event is exposed to the brand. It grows organically with each event created.

### Marketing Team: Tiny

Three-person marketing team monitors trends to decide where it's worth jumping in, not just on social media but within the platform itself. When "Punch the Monkey" went viral, the team created Punch cover photos users could add to events.

### What They Did NOT Do

- No paid marketing initially. Zero ad spend for most of their growth phase.
- No paywalls on the fun stuff. No guilt-tripping "upgrade" flows.
- No polished brand voice. Self-aware, a little unhinged, but never try-hard.

### What They DID Do

**Brand Positioning**: "Facebook Events for hot people" and "Eventbrite but for Gen Z." Made the positioning itself shareable.

**Viral Moments as Earned Media**: The Timothee Chalamet lookalike contest at Washington Square Park drew thousands of attendees. A flyer taped to a lamppost with a QR code and Partiful invite link went viral on TikTok and spawned a GQ feature. Every screenshot of every viral event showed the Partiful link. That's earned media you cannot buy.

**Content Layer**:
- Discover feed for public events, hand-curated by one team member
- Substack newsletter "The Guest List" with in-house writer + guest writers
- Meme-ready invite templates created by in-house designers

**Recent Evolution (2025-2026)**:
- Started experimenting with paid tactics: amplifying UGC on TikTok
- Partnering with creators like comedian Lukas Battle and "Recess Therapy" host Julian Shapiro-Barnum
- Goal is not virality but ubiquity. They want "send a Partiful" to become a verb.

### Key Takeaway for Tryps

Partiful's growth is not from marketing. It's from the product itself being the distribution channel. Every invite is an ad. Every event page is a landing page. The brand voice makes people screenshot and share. Tryps needs the same: every trip invite, every itinerary share, every group link is the marketing.

## 3. BeReal: The Campus Ambassador Playbook

### Growth Numbers

- Launched 2020, exploded 2022
- 920K MAU in Jan 2022 to 73.5M by August 2022
- Downloads increased over 1,000% year-over-year
- #1 most downloaded iPhone app globally in September 2022

### The Playbook

**Ambassador Program (the actual driver):**
- Recruited student ambassadors at dozens of universities
- Brown students got $30 per referral, $50 per download + review
- Targeted: Brown, UConn, Miami, USC, SMU
- Cash for referrals + billboards on campus
- Word-of-mouth in tight social networks did the rest

**Timing:**
- Arrived at the perfect cultural moment. Gen Z disenchantment with Instagram/Facebook was peaking.
- 89% of Gen Z say authenticity matters more than polished advertising.
- Zero filters, zero ads, just everyday life. The anti-Instagram.

**What Went Viral:**
- The daily notification mechanic (you have 2 minutes to post) created a shared ritual
- FOMO was baked into the product, not the marketing
- Dense networks (college campuses) meant rapid saturation once seeded

### Why It Eventually Stalled

By 2023-2024, daily usage declined. The novelty wore off. No meaningful social graph lock-in. BeReal had to pivot toward a "comeback strategy" by 2025. The lesson: virality without retention is a sugar rush.

## 4. Best Consumer App Launches 2023-2026 (Small Teams)

### Gas App (Team of 4, $1M in 10 days)

**Founder:** Nikita Bier (previously sold TBH to Facebook)

**The Playbook:**
- Targeted a single Georgia high school that started its semester earlier than the rest of the country
- Goal: 40% penetration in one school within 24 hours
- Used teen-run burner Instagram accounts, followed every profile tagged at the school's location page, DMed download links
- 40% of students installed on day one
- Social graph expansion from school to school was automatic
- "Geofenced exclusivity" created FOMO in each new region
- State-of-the-art UX with unnecessary steps removed = 40%+ higher activation

**Key Principle:** Dense, closed networks. If you can saturate one network, it spreads to adjacent ones automatically.

### Locket Widget (26 Creators, 298M Views)

**Origin:** Matt Moss built it as a gift for his girlfriend. 80M total downloads.

**Creator Strategy:**
- 26 active creators posting 2-3x daily
- 298 million views, 1.5 million shares, 2.3 million bookmarks
- 46 creators tested total, underperformers replaced after a few dozen videos
- Content format: 2-3 second video, person's face, smiling or dancing, looking at camera
- Eventually shifted to internal growth team posting directly (employees posting the classic Locket TikTok format daily, multiple times per day)

**Ruthless optimization:** Track, cut, replace. Not a "spray and pray" approach.

### Fizz Social ($41.5M raised, 700+ campuses)

**Growth Strategy:**
- Zero paid marketing. Zero influencer campaigns.
- Founder Teddy Solomon personally visited 220+ campuses
- User base doubled between fall 2024 and 2025
- 30 full-time staff + 4,000 volunteer moderators across schools
- Campus-by-campus, in person. Doesn't scale, but it works.

### Wispr Flow (50% MoM growth, 18-person team)

- Voice dictation app, $81M total funding, $700M valuation
- Grew 40-50% month-over-month
- Team of 18 people
- Multi-platform rollout: Mac first, then Windows, iOS, Android
- Referenced as a brand inspiration for Tryps

### Poke AI ($15M seed, iMessage-native)

- AI assistant living inside iMessage (relevant to Tryps' iMessage agent scope)
- $100M valuation at seed
- 6,000 VC insiders as early users (seeded network effect in tech community)
- Beta users replaced ChatGPT usage with Poke
- "Retention and usage numbers unheard of in the personal assistant space"

## 5. AI-Native Marketing Teams: Real Structures

### The Emerging Model: Pod-Based, Not Channel-Based

Best teams in 2025-2026 are moving from "social media person, email person, paid person" to cross-functional pods organized around outcomes, with AI handling production so humans focus on strategy and judgment.

### Core Roles in a Lean AI-Native Marketing Team

1. **AI Operator / Workflow Architect**: Manages AI workflows, selects and structures prompts, quality-checks outputs, builds reusable prompt libraries. Owns the agent adoption roadmap.
2. **Content Specialist**: Refines AI-generated content, ensures brand voice, adds depth, maintains human touch. The taste layer.
3. **Data / Analytics Person**: Monitors performance, provides real-time insights, enables data-driven decisions.

That's three roles. Austin Lau at Anthropic collapsed all three into one for 10 months.

### The 70/30 Production Model

- 70% of content production time handled by AI
- 30% human direction and quality control
- AI-generated first drafts require 20-40 minutes of human editing for 2,000-word articles (replacing 4-8 hours of writing time)
- Brands running this model produce 5-10x more content at 60-80% lower cost per piece

### Real Results

**Series B SaaS team:**
- Tripled content from 4 articles/month to 12 with same 2-person team
- Organic traffic up 40% over 6 months
- Cost per article dropped from $800 to $180

**Social media AI agent example:**
- Identified trending topic 6 hours before it peaked
- Created brand-native post and queued for optimal timing
- Single post generated 155% of the brand's monthly engagement goal

### Tools People Actually Use

- **Claude Code + Cowork**: Austin Lau's entire stack. Figma plugins, slash commands, Agent Skills, CSV exports.
- **Patrick Schaber's Claude Marketing OS**: Pre-built file system you open in Cowork. CLAUDE.md triggers onboarding. Walks you through creating brand voice, audience profiles, content strategy. Shareable across teams.
- **MKT1 Newsletter's MCP Server**: Skills library for Claude (15+ skills). mkt1_newsletter skill searches the full archive. mkt1_templates finds relevant spreadsheets and docs.
- **Stormy AI**: YC-backed. AI agent for influencer marketing. Natural-language search for creators, automated negotiation (reduces fees up to 43%), performance tracking.

## 6. UGC-First Launch Strategy: The Wave Playbook

### The Economics

- One macro influencer post: $20,000+
- 50 micro-influencer posts at $400 each: $20,000 total, far more total engagement and sales
- 84% of consumers trust peer recommendations over branded advertising

### Locket's Playbook (the gold standard)

This is the most documented and successful UGC launch strategy from the past 3 years:

1. **Recruit 25-50 creators** (Locket tested 46 total, kept 26 active)
2. **Templatized format**: 2-3 second video, creator's face, high-energy believable emotion, looking at camera
3. **Volume**: Each creator posts 2-3x daily
4. **Ruthless culling**: Underperformers replaced after a few dozen videos
5. **Transition to internal**: Eventually shifted from external creators to internal growth team posting the same format daily
6. **Results**: 298M views, 1.5M shares, 2.3M bookmarks from just 26 creators

### Coordination Timing for a Wave

**Pre-Launch (2-4 weeks before):**
- Recruit and vet 50-100 micro-influencers
- Platforms like Stack Influence or Stormy AI automate this
- Expect first creator applications within 48 hours, first UGC assets within 14 days
- Brief all creators with a shared truth set (brand voice, key messages) but vary angles

**Wave Structure:**
- 2-4 week sprints with timed waves and creative variation
- Stagger posts to prevent audience fatigue and enable fair performance comparison
- Assign roles: explainers, demonstrators, reviewers. Rotate creative prompts.
- For time-sensitive launches: coordinate 5-10 creators for a tight content arc (build tension, deliver payoff, drive urgency)

**Ongoing:**
- Weekly performance snapshots
- Replace underperformers (Locket's 26/46 ratio = 57% kept)
- Keep a bench roster of pre-vetted alternates
- Maintain a content bank of evergreen angles
- Final readout with learnings and next tests

### Content Formats That Work

1. **UGC reaction video**: Creator reacting to app's core feature. Feels like a peer recommendation. Consistently outperforms high-budget productions.
2. **Day-in-my-life integration**: Creator uses app naturally in their routine
3. **Before/after**: Show the problem, show the solution
4. **First 3 seconds**: Call out specific audience characteristics ("if you're planning a group trip with more than 4 friends...")

### Amplification

- Use TikTok Spark Ads to boost the best-performing organic UGC
- Post 40x/day across accounts if you're going all-in (Stormy AI playbook)
- Optimize onboarding for 10% conversion from ad to active user

## Synthesis: What This Means for Tryps

### The Pattern

Every breakout consumer app in this research shares the same DNA:

1. **The product IS the distribution.** Partiful invites are the ads. Locket widgets are the ads. Gas notifications are the ads. Tryps trip invites need to be the ads.

2. **Dense networks first, broad reach later.** Nikita Bier did one school at a time. Fizz did campus by campus. BeReal did university ambassador programs. Tryps should pick one friend group type or travel niche and saturate it.

3. **Brand voice that people screenshot.** Partiful's "Facebook Events for hot people." BeReal's anti-Instagram positioning. The voice itself is the marketing. Tryps has this with "Partiful but for travel" and the Anthony Bourdain personality.

4. **UGC beats polished production.** Locket's 2-3 second face videos beat any studio content. Reaction videos outperform professional ads. Real beats polished.

5. **One person with AI tools > small team without.** Austin Lau ran Anthropic's growth marketing solo for 10 months with Claude Code. The tools exist to do this now.

### Tryps Launch Playbook (Draft)

**Phase 1: Pre-Launch (4 weeks out)**
- Build Claude Code workflows for ad creation (copy the Austin Lau playbook exactly)
- Recruit 30-50 micro-influencers in the travel/group plans space via Stormy AI
- Create a templatized UGC format ("POV: your friend just sent a Tryps link for the summer trip")
- Set up Tryps invite pages that look as good as Partiful event pages

**Phase 2: Seed Dense Networks**
- Pick 5-10 college friend groups or young professional social circles
- Get one person in each group to create a real trip on Tryps
- Every invite is a product demo. Every trip page is a landing page.
- Ambassador program: cash for referrals (BeReal model)

**Phase 3: Wave Launch**
- Stagger 30-50 UGC creators posting 2-3x daily over 2 weeks
- Amplify top performers with TikTok Spark Ads
- Replace underperformers after first week
- Internal team posts the same format daily alongside creators

**Phase 4: Organic Flywheel**
- Every trip created is marketing (Partiful model)
- Newsletter or Substack for trip culture content
- Meme-ready trip page templates
- Hand-curate a "Discover" feed of public trips

### Team Structure

Based on this research, Tryps needs:
- **1 growth marketer** who can use Claude Code (the Austin Lau role). Sean could fill this.
- **30-50 micro-influencers** on rotating retainer ($400/post)
- **Claude Code workflows** for ad creation, copy generation, content repurposing
- **Total headcount for marketing at launch: 1-2 people + AI + creators**
