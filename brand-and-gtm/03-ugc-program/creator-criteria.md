# Tryps UGC Creator Criteria

## Executive Summary

This document defines exactly who we're looking for in the Tryps UGC creator program. It's designed so that anyone on the team (Molly, Ana, Gianna, Cash, Sean, Jake) can open an Instagram or TikTok profile and know within 30 seconds whether the creator is a yes, a maybe, or a no.

The goal: build a master list of ~300 creators. From that list, engage 80 for launch week (May 2, 2026). Budget: $8,000 ($100/creator average, separate from Sean's contract).

---

## 1. The Audience We're Reaching

Before we pick creators, we need to be clear on who their audience should be.

**Tryps core user:**
- 22-28 years old, post-grad
- Single, social, active lifestyle
- The "group chat organizer" who actually makes plans happen
- Goes on 1-2 group trips a year (spring break, ski trip, beach house, festival)
- Probably went to a 4-year college, still tight with their friend group
- Works out, goes out on weekends, tries new restaurants
- Watches travel and lifestyle content on TikTok and Instagram
- iPhone user (Tryps lives in iMessage)
- Performative in a good way. They want people to see where they are and what they're doing. Travel is social currency.

**Trip types Tryps is built for:**
- Weekend trips (beach house, cabin, city getaway)
- International trips (group vacations)
- Road trips
- Festival groups (Coachella, ski weekends, destination events)

## 2. Platform Priority

| Platform | Priority | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| TikTok | Primary (50%) | Discovery engine. This is where 22-28 year olds find new products. |
| Instagram | Primary (50%) | Social proof. Reels + Stories. Where the performative sharing happens. |
| YouTube | Secondary (for specific creators) | Friend-group ensemble content and travel vlogs. Strongest audience loyalty of any platform. |
| X | Not a focus | Content can be repurposed here but we're not sourcing creators for X. |
| YouTube Shorts | Not a focus | Will be copy-pasted from TikTok. Not a sourcing platform. |

**Key insight:** Creators with YouTube channels are high-priority targets regardless of tier. YouTube audiences are the most loyal and engaged. Tryps can sponsor their travel vlogs and fund trips in exchange for coverage. These creators actively want brand partners who make their content possible.

## 3. Creator Segments (Ranked by Impact)

### Tier 1: Lifestyle / GRWM / Going-Out Creators
The highest priority. These creators show their life. Their audience wants to live like them. When they use Tryps to plan a trip with friends, it feels aspirational and authentic. Think: "get ready with me for Cabo with the girls" or "what I did this weekend in Austin."

### Tier 2: Travel Creators
Direct overlap with the product. They already post about destinations, planning, group trips. Their audience is already thinking about travel when they see the Tryps integration.

### Tier 3: Friend Group YouTubers
The David Dobrik model. A crew of friends who make content together. A group of friends planning a trip on camera IS the Tryps demo. These creators bring friend-group energy that no solo creator can replicate.

### Tier 4: Solo Creators Planning Trips
The organizer archetype. They show themselves wrangling friends, building itineraries, making things happen. They'd demo Tryps as the tool that finally makes group planning easy.

### Tier 5: Festival / Event Creators
Coachella sends influencers to Coachella. Tryps should send creators on trips. This segment is more of a Milestone 2 and long-term play, but worth including in the initial list for future activation.

## 4. Follower Tiers and Budget

**Two tiers only. No in-between.**

| Tier | Follower Range | Role | Budget Per Creator |
|------|---------------|------|-------------------|
| Mid | 50K - 500K | Credibility names. Makes the app feel real and legitimate. | $200-500+ (negotiate individually) |
| Micro | 10K - 50K | Volume layer. Creates the wave of content around the bigger names. | $50-150 |

**Minimum follower count: 10,000.** Below 10K is not considered an influencer for this campaign.

**Budget math ($8,000 total for 80 creators):**
- ~10-15 mid-tier creators at $200-400 avg = ~$3,000-5,000
- ~65-70 micro creators at $50-75 avg = ~$3,250-5,250
- Total: ~$8,000

**The contest mechanic (see Section 7) can stretch this budget significantly** by getting creators to post for free in exchange for a shot at a dream trip.

## 5. The 30-Second Profile Check

When you open a creator's Instagram or TikTok, here's how to evaluate them fast.

### Instant Yes Signals
- Posts Reels weekly (not daily spam). Quality over quantity.
- High-quality video editing. They clearly care about production value.
- Curated lifestyle photos on the grid that perform consistently.
- **Consistent engagement across the entire feed.** Likes are roughly proportional to followers on every post, not just one viral hit.
- Friends visible in their comments. Real conversations happening.
- They look like a normal person. Approachable. Someone you'd want to hang out with.
- Traveling, social, active lifestyle visible on the feed.
- Bonus: they have a YouTube channel.

### Instant No Signals
- Posting 5x a day of random content (quantity over quality churn)
- Inconsistent engagement (one post has 50K likes, the next has 200. That's bought followers or algorithm lottery.)
- Salesy energy. If their feed looks like a billboard or every other post is #ad, pass.
- Fake or bought followers. Sean can spot this. Common tells: follower count way higher than engagement, bot comments ("nice pic!!" from accounts with no posts), sudden follower spikes.
- No real community in their comments. If comments are just emojis from strangers, the audience isn't real.
- They only post curated flat lays and hotel room tours. Tryps needs energy, not aesthetics.
- Luxury-only travel. Tryps is for the crew going to Cabo, not the influencer at the Four Seasons.
- No Stories posted in the last 2 weeks (inactive or feed-only, no casual engagement).

### The Engagement Check
Pull their last 10 posts. Average the likes. Divide by follower count.
- **Above 4%:** Excellent. Prioritize.
- **3-4%:** Good. Include.
- **2-3%:** Acceptable if content quality is high.
- **Below 2%:** Skip. Audience is fake or disengaged.

## 6. Demographics (Loose, Not a Hard Filter)

Creator demographics are not a strict box. What matters most is content quality, video editing skill, and audience fit.

**General guidance:**
- Age: 21-30 is the sweet spot, but not a hard cutoff
- Gender: Aim for roughly 60/40 female-to-male split (women drive more travel planning content), but don't force it
- Location: US-based preferred. International creators are fine if their audience is US-based and English-speaking.
- The deciding factor is always: does their content fit the Tryps brand, and is their audience real?

**Prioritize video editing quality above all demographics.** Creators who make visually strong, well-edited content are the ones whose posts will be worth promoting, reposting, and potentially running as paid ads.

## 7. Contest Mechanic (Stretch the Budget)

In addition to paid placements, Tryps should run a contest that gets creators to post for free.

**The hook:** "Where do you want to go? We'll send you there."

**How it works:**
- Creators post about Tryps (Story, Reel, or TikTok) to enter
- Prize: Tryps pays for the winner's dream trip. Their choice of destination. Possibly first-class flight.
- Top entries (judged by views, engagement, and content quality) win
- Low-effort entries are disqualified. Quality bar is enforced.
- Smaller incentive layer: post on your Story, get a discount from a Tryps partner brand

**The ambition:** This should feel like a cultural moment, not a marketing campaign. Think ALS Ice Bucket Challenge scale. A simple, repeatable action that spreads person-to-person.

**Note:** The contest mechanic needs its own detailed rollout plan, including a commercial explaining it. This is flagged for further development.
