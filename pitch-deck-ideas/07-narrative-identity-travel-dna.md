# Narrative Identity & Travel DNA

## The Slide (Single Slide — Pitch Deck)

### "We don't just know what you like. We know why you travel."

**Three layers of traveler understanding:**

| Layer | What It Captures | How Tryps Gets It | Example |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Traits** (Trip Vibe) | Beach vs. city, chill vs. packed, early vs. late | A/B vibe quiz at trip creation | "Prefers chill mornings, packed evenings" |
| **Adaptations** (Trip Intent) | What you want from *this specific trip* | One open-ended question + NLP extraction | "Trying to reconnect with college friends after 3 years" |
| **Narrative** (Travel DNA) | Why you travel at all — your evolving story | Cross-trip pattern recognition over time | "Became more spontaneous after years of overplanning" |

**The insight:** No travel app thinks about personality at this depth. If Tryps builds the richest traveler personality graph — traits + adaptations + narrative — that's a genuine data moat that compounds with every trip.

**The science:** Based on Dan McAdams' (Northwestern) three-level personality framework from "The Psychology of Life Stories" (2001). Traditional personality research stops at traits. McAdams proved that the *story you tell about yourself* — redemption arcs, generativity themes, how you connect past to future — is a distinct, measurable layer of who you are.

**Why this matters for the agent:** When 6 people's narratives conflict, the agent doesn't just average preferences — it understands *roles*. The Ultimate Planner gets amplified. The "I Don't Care-er" gets protected from decision fatigue. The agent is facilitating a group story, not optimizing a spreadsheet.

**The flywheel:** Traits are captured on day 1. Adaptations emerge per trip. Narrative identity builds across trips. Each layer makes the agent smarter, which makes the next trip better, which deepens the data. Nobody else has this.
