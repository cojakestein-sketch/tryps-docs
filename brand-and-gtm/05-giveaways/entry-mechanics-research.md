# Giveaway Entry Mechanics: Deep Research

**Purpose:** Practical breakdown of what specific entry mechanics make giveaways go viral -- especially for app launches targeting Gen Z friend groups. Covers tag/repost structures, multi-action systems, SMS/text-to-enter, group chat virality, legal compliance, conversion funnels, and app-launch case studies.

**Date:** March 29, 2026

## 1. Tag Friends + Repost: What Worked, What Flopped, and Why

### The Standard Structure

The "follow + tag + repost" giveaway is the most common format on Instagram and TikTok. At its simplest: follow the account, tag 1-3 friends in the comments, and optionally repost to your story.

### What Worked

**Cash App Fridays (2017-present)**
- Format: Retweet + reply with your $cashtag + tag friends
- Cash App gave away $10K-$75K per week, depositing $100-$500 per winner
- The viral loop: new people saw the retweet, downloaded Cash App to get a $cashtag, followed the account, entered next week's giveaway
- Result: became a self-sustaining growth engine contributing to 190M+ total downloads. Cost per impression was a fraction of traditional ads because every entry was organic distribution.
- **Why it worked:** The entry mechanic (share your $cashtag) required having the product. The prize was cash -- universally desirable, zero friction to understand. Weekly cadence created habit.

**Fashion Nova / Boohoo / Fast Fashion "Tag to Win"**
- Simple "tag 3 friends, follow us" mechanics on Instagram
- Posts with giveaways get 3.5x more likes and 64x more comments than regular posts
- Fashion Nova reportedly grew from ~60K to 20M+ followers in part through constant giveaway loops
- **Why it worked:** Prize was aspirational but accessible (free clothes, not a car). Low barrier. The audience (young women) was already conditioned to tag friends in outfit posts, so the mechanic felt native.

**Algorithm Alignment (2025-2026)**
- Instagram and TikTok's algorithms in 2025-2026 heavily weight engagement signals: comments, shares, saves, DMs
- Every tag = a comment. Every repost = a share. Giveaway posts ride the algorithm because the entry mechanic IS the signal the algorithm rewards
- This means a well-structured giveaway post gets organic reach amplification on top of the direct shares

### What Flopped

**Overly Complex "Like + Follow + Tag + Repost + Comment Why You Deserve This"**
- When entry requires 4-5 actions, completion drops significantly. Users see the wall of rules and scroll past.
- The "comment why you deserve to win" or "share your story" requirement kills casual participation. People don't want to write an essay for a chance to win.

**Generic Prizes with Tag Requirements**
- "Win a $500 Amazon gift card -- tag 3 friends!" attracts bot accounts, engagement farmers, and people with zero interest in your brand
- The followers gained are low-quality and unfollow within days
- **Lesson:** Prize-audience fit matters more than prize size. A $200 prize your audience actually wants outperforms a $1,000 generic gift card.

**"Tag as Many Friends as Possible for Extra Entries"**
- Unlimited tagging turns comments into spam. People tag random accounts, inactive accounts, or celebrity accounts
- It degrades the comment section and creates a negative brand impression
- Better approach: cap at 3-5 tags per entry, or give 1 bonus entry per unique friend who also enters

### Key Takeaways for Tryps

1. The tag mechanic works best when it feels like a natural social action, not a chore. "Tag the group chat that needs this trip" is better than "tag 3 friends to enter."
2. Repost-to-story works better than repost-to-feed because stories feel temporary and low-commitment.
3. The entry mechanic should require product usage (like Cash App's $cashtag requirement). Tryps's existing "screenshot your Marty conversation" approach nails this.

## 2. Multi-Action Entry Systems: The Sweet Spot Before Friction Kills Conversion

### How Multi-Action Systems Work

Platforms like Gleam, SweepWidget, KickoffLabs, RafflePress, and VYPER let you assign different point values to different actions:

| Action | Typical Points | Friction Level |
|---|---|---|
| Follow on Instagram | 1 entry | Low |
| Repost to story | 2 entries | Low |
| Tag a friend | 1 entry per tag | Low |
| Sign up for email list | 3 entries | Medium |
| Download the app | 5 entries | Medium-High |
| Refer a friend who enters | 5 entries | High |
| Create and post UGC | 10 entries | High |
| Text a keyword to a number | 3 entries | Medium |
| Share to group chat | 3 entries | Medium |

### The Drop-Off Curve

**The data:**
- Average giveaway conversion rate (saw it -> entered): 30-35% for well-structured campaigns. This is dramatically higher than standard landing pages (6-7%).
- But that 30-35% is for SINGLE-ACTION entries (follow, or tag, or repost)
- Each additional required action drops completion by roughly 30-50%
- If action 1 gets 100% completion, action 2 gets ~60-70%, action 3 gets ~35-45%, action 4 gets ~20-25%
- By action 5, you're down to the hyper-motivated 10-15%

**The 67% Facebook Connect wall:**
- Over 67% of people will abandon a giveaway if asked to connect via Facebook. Any action that requires granting account access or permissions is a conversion killer.

**What kills drop-off:**
- Requiring personal information beyond name + email (phone, address, birthday)
- Actions that take more than 10-15 seconds each
- Actions that require leaving the current platform (click this link, go to this site, come back)
- Actions that feel "permanent" (post to your main feed vs. post to your story)
- Any action that requires creating an account on an unfamiliar platform

### The Sweet Spot

**1 required action + 2-3 optional bonus actions is optimal.**

Structure it as:
- **Required:** 1 low-friction action (follow + tag, or download + screenshot)
- **Bonus tier 1:** 1-2 easy actions for extra entries (repost to story, tag additional friends)
- **Bonus tier 2:** 1 high-value action for significant extra entries (refer a friend who also enters, create UGC)

This way, casual participants enter quickly (protecting your top-of-funnel), while motivated participants can increase their odds (giving you more distribution). You get volume AND depth.

### The "Points for Actions" Model Best Practices

From ShortStack, KickoffLabs, and VYPER data:
- Cap bonus actions at 5 maximum. More than 5 options creates decision paralysis.
- Every action should take under 30 seconds to complete
- Show a progress bar or entry count ("You have 3 entries -- do X for 5 more!") -- gamification increases completion of bonus actions by 20-40%
- The highest-friction action should have the highest reward (10x entries for UGC vs. 1x for a follow)
- Auto-fill and one-tap actions dramatically increase completion. Social auto-fill increases conversion by 189%.

### Recommendation for Tryps

**Required entry:** Download Tryps + add Marty to group chat + text Marty a trip idea (this is the product demo -- non-negotiable)

**Bonus entries:**
- +3 entries: Screenshot the Marty conversation and post to IG Story or TikTok with #MartyPlanMyTrip
- +5 entries: Every friend in the group chat who also posts their own screenshot
- +2 entries: Share the giveaway post to a group chat (via IG's "share to DM" or iMessage link)

This keeps the required action focused on product usage and makes the bonus actions about distribution.

## 3. SMS / Text-to-Enter Giveaways

### How They Work Technically

**The basic flow:**
1. Brand leases an SMS short code (a 5-6 digit number, e.g., 55555)
2. Brand picks a keyword (e.g., "TRYPS" or "VACATION")
3. User texts the keyword to the short code (e.g., text TRYPS to 55555)
4. System instantly sends a confirmation SMS ("You're entered! Reply STOP to opt out")
5. User is now entered in the drawing AND opted into the brand's SMS list
6. Winner is selected via random number generator from all valid entries

**Technical requirements:**
- You MUST use an SMS short code. US and Canadian carriers explicitly prohibit running text-to-win campaigns on toll-free numbers, long codes, or 10DLC numbers.
- Short code lease: ~$650 setup fee + ~$995/month
- Per-message carrier fees: ~$0.0018 per message
- Per-message platform fees: $0.01-$0.05 per SMS depending on platform and volume
- Carrier approval process: 4-8 weeks (submit at least 2 months before launch)
- Once approved, you don't need to re-apply for each new campaign

**Platforms that power text-to-win:**
- **SimpleTexting** -- popular for small-to-mid campaigns, easy setup, shared short codes available
- **SlickText** -- strong text-to-win specific features, good for first-time campaigns
- **Textedly** -- budget-friendly, good for startups
- **EZ Texting** -- established platform, good documentation
- **Call Loop** -- focused specifically on text-to-win
- **Infobip** -- enterprise-grade, for large-scale campaigns
- **Klaviyo** -- if you're already in the Klaviyo ecosystem for email, their SMS add-on handles sweepstakes compliance

**Shared vs. dedicated short codes:**
- Shared short code: cheaper ($50-100/month), but you share the number with other brands and have less control
- Dedicated short code: $995/month lease, but it's yours alone -- better for brand recognition and trust

### What Makes Texting a Number Feel Easy vs. Sketchy

**Easy:**
- Short, recognizable keyword ("TRIP" not "SUMMERVACATION2026GIVEAWAY")
- Clear call-to-action: "Text TRIP to 55555 for a chance to win"
- Immediate confirmation response (within seconds)
- Clear opt-out instructions in the confirmation ("Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
- The brand is already trusted/known -- people text numbers they see from brands they recognize
- Displayed on a screen they're already looking at (TikTok, Instagram, event signage)

**Sketchy:**
- Long or random-seeming number (10-digit numbers feel like spam)
- No immediate response after texting (silence = "did I just give my number to a scammer?")
- Asking for personal info via text after entry ("Reply with your full name and address")
- No clear brand association -- the number appears without context
- Follow-up texts that feel like spam (too frequent, too salesy)
- No opt-out language

### Brands That Used Text-to-Enter Successfully

**Radio stations and TV shows (the OG):**
- "Text VOTE to 55555" has been standard for American Idol, The Voice, etc. for 15+ years
- This is why the mechanic feels familiar to most Americans -- it's been normalized by entertainment

**Chipotle "Rewards Exchange":**
- Chipotle used SMS entry for giveaways tied to their rewards program
- Texting a keyword entered you for free burritos, merch, and experiences
- The simplicity (one text = entered) drove massive participation

**H-E-B "Big Thanks Texas Giveaway" (2025):**
- Texas grocery chain H-E-B ran a text/scan-to-enter giveaway
- Shoppers texted a keyword or scanned at checkout to enter
- Combining physical presence (in-store) with digital entry (text) bridged both channels

**Amazon Treasure Truck:**
- Amazon used SMS notifications to alert users about surprise deals available at specific locations
- Not a traditional giveaway, but the "text to get access to something special" model built anticipation and drove foot traffic

### Relevance for Tryps

Text-to-enter could work well for Tryps in specific contexts:
- **Event activations:** At college campuses, festivals, or pop-ups, "Text MARTY to 55555" on signage is faster than asking someone to download an app on the spot
- **As an AMOE (see legal section):** A text-to-enter option satisfies the "no purchase necessary" / alternate entry requirement
- **The "Marty Hotline" concept** from the existing brainstorm doc is essentially a text-to-enter giveaway -- and it works because Marty IS a text-based product, so the entry mechanic demonstrates the product

**Cost estimate for Tryps:**
- Shared short code + platform (e.g., SimpleTexting): ~$100-200/month
- Dedicated short code: ~$1,000/month + $650 setup
- For a 2-week campaign expecting 5,000-10,000 texts: budget $500-1,500 total for platform + messaging fees
- Lead time: start the carrier approval process 8+ weeks before launch (i.e., by early March for a May launch -- this may already be tight)

## 4. Group Chat Virality: Targeting iMessage, WhatsApp, and DMs as Distribution

### Why Group Chats Matter

Group chats are where Gen Z actually makes decisions. The funnel is: see content on social -> screenshot or share link to group chat -> group discusses -> group acts. For a travel app targeting friend groups, the group chat IS the conversion environment.

The problem: group chats (iMessage, WhatsApp, SMS) are "dark social" -- brands can't track shares, can't see conversations, and can't insert themselves. This makes group chat virality hard to engineer but incredibly powerful when it happens.

### How Brands Have Targeted Group Chats

**Direct examples are rare, but the mechanics are known:**

**Easypromos WhatsApp/Telegram integration:**
- Easypromos (giveaway platform) lets organizers create a share button that opens WhatsApp/Telegram/Line with a pre-written message + link
- The user taps "Share via WhatsApp," selects a group chat, and the message drops in with one tap
- The pre-written message includes the giveaway link and a brief hook
- **Key insight:** reducing the share action to a single tap (vs. "copy this link, open WhatsApp, paste it, write a message") is the difference between 20% share rate and 2%

**Temu's referral mechanic:**
- Temu's "invite friends, get free money" mechanic was designed for group chat distribution
- Users got a unique referral link and were rewarded when friends signed up through it
- People posted screenshots of PayPal deposits in group chats, creating social proof
- The mechanic was so aggressive it felt spammy to many -- but it drove Temu to #1 most-downloaded app in 2024 across multiple countries
- **Lesson:** Group chat referral works, but there's a fine line between "my friend is sharing something cool" and "my friend is spamming me for a coupon"

**"Wrapped" / Year-in-Review sharing:**
- Spotify Wrapped, Apple Music Replay, and the third-party "Wrapped 2025" for iMessage/WhatsApp stats all generate group chat sharing
- The mechanic: give people a personalized, visual result they want to share. The sharing happens organically in group chats because it's conversational ("look at mine!")
- **Tryps angle:** What if entering the giveaway generates a shareable "trip personality" card or "Marty roasted my travel taste" result that people naturally screenshot and share to group chats?

### Making Group Chat Sharing Frictionless

**Technical approaches:**

1. **Deep links that open messaging apps directly:**
   - Use platform-specific URL schemes: `whatsapp://send?text=...` opens WhatsApp with pre-filled text
   - `sms:?body=...` opens the default SMS/iMessage app with pre-filled text
   - These reduce sharing from 5 steps to 1 tap

2. **Share sheets:**
   - On iOS, the native share sheet lets users pick any messaging app. Triggering the share sheet with pre-filled content is the most universal approach.
   - The content should be a short message + link, not a long paragraph

3. **The "invite your group chat" flow:**
   - After someone enters the giveaway, prompt: "Share with your group chat for +3 bonus entries"
   - Pre-fill the message: "marty just planned our dream trip and it's insane. your group chat needs to try this -- [link]"
   - Track via unique referral links per user

4. **In-app group invite:**
   - If Tryps has a "add friends" or "invite to group" flow within the app, this is the most trackable distribution channel
   - The giveaway can reward users who get their friends to join their Tryps group

**What makes group chat sharing feel natural vs. forced:**

| Natural | Forced |
|---|---|
| "Look at this trip Marty planned for us" (screenshot of a fun Marty conversation) | "Enter the giveaway at this link!" (generic marketing link) |
| A funny, personal result worth sharing ("Marty says our group is 'chaotic good travelers'") | A request to share for bonus entries with no intrinsic reason to share |
| Content that sparks conversation ("where should we actually go though?") | Content that dead-ends ("I entered, you should too") |

### Recommendation for Tryps

The existing giveaway concepts already have strong group chat mechanics (add Marty to your group chat, get your friends to also post). To strengthen the group chat virality specifically:

1. **Make the Marty conversation inherently shareable.** If Marty's first response to a trip request is funny, surprising, or roast-worthy, people will screenshot it and send it to other group chats without being asked.

2. **Give groups a shared identity.** "Marty ranked your group chat's travel compatibility" or "Marty says your group is a Level 7 Adventure Squad" -- something the group wants to screenshot and post.

3. **Use the "everyone in the group chat who enters = bonus for the whole group" mechanic.** This creates internal peer pressure that plays out in the group chat itself. "Guys, just post the screenshot so we get more entries."

4. **Pre-fill share messages that sound human.** Not "Enter the Tryps giveaway!" but "bro marty just told our group chat to go to portugal. you need to try this" -- informal, lowercase, sounds like something a friend would actually type.

## 5. Legal / Compliance Considerations

### The Three Elements That Make a Lottery (and How to Avoid It)

A promotion becomes an illegal lottery if it has all three:
1. **Prize** (something of value)
2. **Chance** (winner selected randomly)
3. **Consideration** (participant must pay or provide something of significant value to enter)

Remove any one element and it's legal. Giveaways/sweepstakes remove consideration (no purchase necessary). Contests remove chance (winner is judged, not random).

### AMOE -- Alternate Means of Entry

**What it is:** Federal law requires that sweepstakes offer a free way to enter that does not require a purchase or significant effort. This is the "No Purchase Necessary" / AMOE requirement.

**What counts as "consideration" (and therefore requires an AMOE):**
- Requiring an app download to enter (this IS consideration -- downloading an app has value to the brand)
- Requiring a purchase
- Requiring submission of UGC (effort = consideration in some interpretations)
- Requiring social media follows or engagement (debated, but increasingly viewed as consideration)

**What this means for Tryps:** If the primary entry mechanic requires downloading Tryps and using Marty, you MUST offer an AMOE. Common AMOE formats:
- Mail-in entry (send a postcard with your name/address to a P.O. box)
- Online form entry (a simple web form with no app download required)
- Email entry (send an email to a specific address with "ENTRY" in the subject line)

**Critical rule:** The AMOE must provide EQUAL chances of winning. You cannot give app-based entries more weight or more entries than AMOE entries. If app users get bonus entries for extra actions, AMOE entrants must be able to earn equivalent bonus entries through non-purchase means.

**Practical approach:** Bury the AMOE in the official rules (this is standard practice). Make the primary CTA about the app-based entry. The AMOE exists for legal compliance -- very few people will use it. But it must exist and must be discoverable.

### State Registration and Bonding

Three states require pre-registration for sweepstakes above certain prize thresholds:

| State | Prize Threshold | Filing Deadline | Bond Required? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New York** | >$5,000 total prize value | 30 days before launch | Yes -- equal to total prize value |
| **Florida** | >$5,000 total prize value | 7 days before launch | Yes -- equal to total prize value |
| **Rhode Island** | >$500 (retail-associated) | Before launch | No bond, but registration required |

**For Tryps:** If the prize is a $5,000 trip, you're right at the threshold. Depending on how you value the prize (retail value of flights + hotel + activities), you may exceed $5,000 and need to register in NY and FL. This requires:
- Filing registration paperwork in both states
- Posting a surety bond equal to the prize value (~$5,000)
- Surety bond cost: typically 1-5% of the bond amount ($50-250)
- Timeline: NY requires 30 days lead time -- plan accordingly

**To avoid registration hassles at this budget level:** Value the prize at exactly $4,999 or structure it as a "$5,000 travel credit" and value it at face value. Consult a promotions attorney -- the $300-500 legal review budgeted in the existing concepts doc is appropriate and necessary.

### FTC Disclosure Requirements

- All giveaway posts must clearly disclose the material connection between the brand and the promotion
- If influencers or UGC creators promote the giveaway, they must use #ad, #sponsored, or #giveaway disclosures
- Official rules must be accessible from the giveaway post (link in bio, pinned comment, etc.)
- Rules must include: eligibility (age, geography), entry period, prize description and ARV (approximate retail value), odds of winning, winner selection method, and sponsor information

### The 1099-MISC Threshold

As of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (July 2025), the threshold for issuing a 1099-MISC for prize winnings was raised from $600 to $2,000. This means:
- Prizes under $2,000: no tax reporting obligation for the sponsor
- Prizes over $2,000: sponsor must collect winner's SSN/TIN and issue a 1099-MISC
- For a $5,000 trip prize: you WILL need to collect the winner's tax info and issue a 1099

### Platform-Specific Rules

- **Instagram:** Requires that the promotion includes a "release of Instagram" clause and an acknowledgment that the promotion is not sponsored by Instagram
- **TikTok:** Requires compliance with TikTok's community guidelines and branded content policies
- **SMS/Text-to-enter:** Must comply with TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) -- explicit opt-in required, opt-out must be available via "STOP" keyword

### Quick Compliance Checklist for Tryps

- [ ] Draft official rules (use a promotions attorney -- budget $300-500)
- [ ] Include AMOE (web form or mail-in entry)
- [ ] Register in NY and FL if prize value exceeds $5,000
- [ ] Post a surety bond if required
- [ ] Include "No Purchase Necessary" language in all promotional materials
- [ ] Include FTC-compliant disclosures on all posts
- [ ] Include Instagram/TikTok platform disclaimers
- [ ] Prepare 1099-MISC collection process for the winner
- [ ] If using SMS entry: get carrier approval 8+ weeks in advance, comply with TCPA
- [ ] Ensure AMOE entrants have equal odds of winning

## 6. The Conversion Funnel: From Impression to Completed Entry

### Benchmark Conversion Rates

| Funnel Stage | Conversion Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **See giveaway post -> Click/engage** | 5-15% | Industry average for organic social posts |
| **Click -> Start entry process** | 40-60% | Landing page visitors who begin entry |
| **Start entry -> Complete single-action entry** | 30-35% | Average giveaway conversion rate (OpinionStage, GiftAFeeling) |
| **Complete entry -> Complete 1 bonus action** | 60-70% of entrants | Drop-off from required -> first bonus |
| **Complete 1 bonus -> Complete 2 bonus actions** | 35-45% of entrants | Cumulative drop per step |
| **Complete 2 bonus -> Complete 3+ bonus actions** | 15-25% of entrants | Only the highly motivated |
| **Entrant -> Refers a friend who also enters** | 5-15% | Varies wildly by prize and mechanic |

### What This Means in Real Numbers

**Scenario: Tryps giveaway with 100K impressions (organic + paid + creator posts)**

- 100,000 people see the giveaway content
- ~10,000 click through or engage (10% CTR)
- ~5,000 start the entry process (50% of clickers)
- ~1,750 complete the primary entry -- download Tryps + add Marty + text a trip idea (35% completion)
- ~1,050 also post a screenshot to their story (60% of entrants do bonus action 1)
- ~525 get at least one friend to also post (30% of entrants do bonus action 2)
- ~175 complete all bonus actions (10% of entrants are max-effort)

**Net result from 100K impressions:** ~1,750 app downloads with real product usage, ~1,050 public social posts showing Marty conversations, ~525 friend groups with multiple members active. This is strong for a $5K prize investment.

### What Kills Drop-Off (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Platform switching** (biggest killer): "Go to this website, then download the app, then come back and submit proof." Every platform switch loses 40-60% of remaining participants.
2. **Time-to-complete > 2 minutes**: If the entry process takes more than 2 minutes, casual participants bail. Keep required entry under 90 seconds.
3. **Unclear instructions**: If someone has to re-read the rules to understand what to do, they won't do it. One sentence per action. Visual instructions (video or carousel) outperform text rules.
4. **Asking for personal data**: Name + email is fine. Phone number loses 20%+. Address loses 40%+. SSN/tax info (save this for the winner only).
5. **"Post to your main feed" requirement**: Stories/temporary posts feel low-commitment. Feed posts feel permanent. Requiring a feed post reduces participation vs. story-only by 50%+.
6. **No progress indication**: If bonus actions exist but users don't see how many entries they have, they lose motivation. Show the count.
7. **Verification delays**: If users have to wait for manual verification ("we'll confirm your entry within 48 hours"), the immediacy is lost and trust drops.

### What Boosts Completion

- **Instant confirmation**: "You're entered! You have 3 entries." Immediate feedback after each action.
- **Social proof**: "12,847 groups have entered so far" -- seeing others participate validates the effort.
- **Visible countdown**: "3 days left to enter" creates urgency.
- **Tiered rewards**: "Everyone who enters gets a Tryps sticker pack. Top entries win the trip." Guaranteed small reward + chance at big reward > only a chance at a big reward.
- **Reels/video instructions**: A 15-second video showing someone entering is worth more than a paragraph of text rules. Film a creator doing the entry process in real-time.

## 7. App Launch Giveaways That Drove Massive Downloads

### Case Study: Cash App Fridays

- **Mechanic:** Follow @CashApp, retweet, reply with $cashtag, tag friends
- **Prize:** $100-$500 per winner, $10K-$75K per week
- **Result:** Self-sustaining viral loop. New users downloaded to get a $cashtag. Contributed to 190M+ total downloads.
- **Key insight:** The entry required the product (you needed a $cashtag). The weekly cadence created habit. The cost per impression was negligible compared to paid acquisition.
- **Applicable to Tryps:** The Tryps giveaway already requires using Marty. This is the same structural advantage. Protect it.

### Case Study: Coinbase Super Bowl QR Code (2022)

- **Mechanic:** Scan a QR code from a 60-second Super Bowl ad (just a bouncing QR code on black screen)
- **Prize:** $15 in free Bitcoin per sign-up, plus a $3M giveaway
- **Result:** 20 million hits in 60 seconds. App went from #186 to #2 on the App Store. Crashed the app.
- **Key insight:** Radical simplicity. One action (scan). Mystery drove curiosity ("what IS this?"). The coordinated moment (everyone scanning simultaneously) created collective energy. The prize was tiny ($15) but the MOMENT was massive.
- **Applicable to Tryps:** A coordinated moment (launch day, specific hour) where everyone enters simultaneously could create a similar energy spike. "At noon on May 2, Marty is giving away trips. Be ready." One moment, not a rolling 2-week window.

### Case Study: Temu Referral Machine (2023-2024)

- **Mechanic:** Download app, get referral link, share with friends, earn cash/credits per signup
- **Prize:** $50+ in credits per referral chain, direct PayPal deposits
- **Result:** #1 most downloaded app in US, UK, Germany, Spain, Australia, Mexico, and more in 2024. 130M+ downloads by mid-2023.
- **Key insight:** Temu turned every user into a distribution channel. The reward (real money) was immediate and tangible. Screenshots of PayPal deposits became social proof in group chats.
- **Warning:** Temu's approach was so aggressive it created significant backlash -- many users felt spammed by friends. The mechanic works, but the aggressiveness level needs calibration.
- **Applicable to Tryps:** The "every person in the group chat who also posts = bonus entry for the whole group" mechanic from Concept 1 is a gentler version of this. It creates peer pressure without financial incentive that feels MLM-adjacent.

### Case Study: SHEIN App-Exclusive Giveaways

- **Mechanic:** Gamified in-app promotions (spin-to-win, scratch cards, daily draws) available only to app users
- **Prize:** Credits, free items, mystery boxes
- **Result:** Consistently #1-2 most downloaded shopping app globally. The gamification kept users opening the app daily.
- **Key insight:** The prizes were small but frequent. Daily engagement mechanics (log in to spin) created habit. App-exclusive access forced downloads.
- **Applicable to Tryps:** Post-giveaway, a "daily Marty surprise" or "weekly trip drop" mechanic could retain users who downloaded for the giveaway. The giveaway gets them in; the ongoing engagement keeps them.

### Case Study: Dropbox Space Race

- **Mechanic:** College students referred classmates for extra storage. Campus-level competition (your school vs. others).
- **Prize:** Extra Dropbox storage (the product itself)
- **Result:** 230,000 new users from the campaign alone
- **Key insight:** Competition at the community level (campus vs. campus) created collective motivation. The prize was the product itself. Dense social networks (college campuses) amplified word-of-mouth.
- **Applicable to Tryps:** A "which college's friend groups travel the best?" competition using Tryps could replicate this at campuses. Each school competes for the most groups using Marty, with the winning school's top group getting the trip.

### Case Study: Pokémon GO Launch (2016)

- **Mechanic:** Not a giveaway per se, but the launch mechanics are instructive. AR gameplay required going to specific locations. Social sharing was organic -- people posted their catches.
- **Result:** 500M downloads in the first two months. #1 app in 70+ countries.
- **Key insight:** The product experience was inherently social and shareable. People playing in public created visible social proof. FOMO drove downloads ("everyone is playing this and I'm not").
- **Applicable to Tryps:** If Marty conversations are inherently funny/surprising/shareable, every user interaction becomes organic marketing. The giveaway seeds the behavior; the product sustains it.

**AMOE (required for legal compliance):**
- Free entry via web form at tryps.com/giveaway (no app download required)
- Or: mail a 3x5 card with name, email, and "dream destination" to [P.O. Box address]

### Timing

- **Entry window:** 5-7 days maximum (not 2 weeks). Urgency drives action. Momentum dies after day 7.
- **Launch the giveaway 2-3 weeks before app launch (mid-April)**. Close entries on May 1. Announce winner live on launch day (May 2).
- **Consider a "flash" element:** Within the entry window, have a 24-hour period where entries count double. This creates a spike of coordinated activity.

### The Group Chat Virality Play

The existing Concept 1 mechanic is already strong. To maximize group chat spread:
1. Make Marty's initial response to a trip request genuinely funny or surprising -- something worth screenshotting
2. After entry, show a "Share with your group chat" button that opens the native share sheet with a pre-filled message
3. Pre-fill message: lowercase, casual, sounds like a friend texting -- not marketing copy
4. Track group chat sharing via unique referral links to award bonus entries
5. Consider a "group entry" mode: the group chat is the entry unit, and the group's combined activity (total screenshots posted, total friends who enter) determines entry count

### SMS/Text Angle

If budget allows, add a text-to-enter component for specific moments:
- Campus activations: "Text MARTY to 55555" on physical signage
- Serves as AMOE for social-first campaign
- Captures phone numbers for post-giveaway SMS marketing
- Requires 8+ weeks of carrier approval lead time -- start immediately if pursuing this

### What to Avoid

1. Don't make "post to your main feed" a required action. Stories only. Main feed posts feel too permanent for Gen Z.
2. Don't allow unlimited friend tags. Cap at 5 to prevent spam.
3. Don't use "tag as many friends as possible" language -- it attracts bot behavior and degrades comment sections.
4. Don't make the entry window longer than 7 days. Energy dissipates.
5. Don't offer a generic prize. "$5K trip planned by Marty" is better than "$5,000 travel credit" because it ties the prize to the product.
6. Don't skip the legal review. The $300-500 for a promotions attorney is the cheapest insurance against an FTC complaint or state AG inquiry.
