Role 1

GTM Infrastructure Engineer

Builds every tool, workflow, and system the brand and go-to-market org runs on.

The Job

You're the person who makes everyone else faster. Sean says "I need to publish across 8 platforms without spending 2 hours a day." You set up Buffer, connect the accounts, build the auto-repost workflows, and hand Sean a dashboard. The Growth Engineer says "I need 50 ad variants from this UGC clip." You build the Recharm pipeline and Figma plugin that lets them do it in 5 minutes.

You evaluate tools, trial them, set them up, wire them together, and teach the team how to use them. When a tool doesn't exist, you build it — with Claude Code, n8n, Remotion, or whatever gets it done fastest. Once systems are live, you maintain them and improve them based on what the team needs.

Role 2

GTM Growth Engineer

The flexible, AI-native operator that Sean and Jake point at whatever the GTM org needs that week.

The Job

You're not a specialist. You're the person who makes things happen across the entire brand and go-to-market operation. One week you're writing App Store copy and running A/B tests. The next week you're building a journalist outreach list. The week after that you're managing Meta ad spend and flagging which UGC clips are winning.

You use the systems the Infrastructure Engineer builds — but you can also build your own when needed (you know Claude Code, you can spin up a quick script or workflow). You're technical enough to be dangerous, marketing-savvy enough to know what good looks like, and fast enough to produce volume. Sean directs what to make and approves before it goes live. You execute.

The goal is 100+ distributed attempts per month — across social, ads, SEO, App Store, email, and anywhere else Tryps needs to show up. You're not precious about any single piece. Volume and speed over perfection. Let the data tell you what's working.
