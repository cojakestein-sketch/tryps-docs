Brand & GTM Team Structure

Three options for building Sean's execution team. All hires are AI-capable engineers, not designers.

Constraints from the Jake + Sean Meeting

  • Sean is creative director. Full autonomy to direct the team. Calls shots, reviews everything before it goes live.
  • No more designers. Random Figma designers are not helpful. Engineers + Claude > creatives.
  • All hires must be AI-capable first. They should be able to use Claude, build workflows, and leverage AI tools as a multiplier.
  • Workers, not strategists. The vision is set. The scopes are defined. We need execution.
  • Goal: 100+ viral attempts per month with 2-3 major successes. Systematic reach building.
  • One person already being hired who's strong on AI agents and automation (n8n, workflow engineering).
  • Connecting with Jackson's Meta ads expert doing $500K/month — may handle paid acquisition externally.
  • Budget: hiring 2 people this week. Contractors, not full-time. Slack access, 2-3x weekly meetings.

Recommendation: Option C

Two engineers under Sean. One builds the machine (AI & Automation Engineer — likely already hired). One runs growth end-to-end (Growth Engineer — the Austin Lau role). Both are AI-native. Both can use Claude Code. Neither is a traditional designer or traditional marketer.

The Growth Engineer is the harder hire but the higher leverage. This person turns Sean's creative direction into 100+ distributed pieces per month, manages $5K in ad spend, writes ASO and SEO copy, and tracks what's working — all using AI tools as a multiplier. When the automation engineer's infrastructure is ready, the growth engineer runs even faster on it.

If the Growth Engineer unicorn proves hard to find, fall back to Option A — it's the safest split with the clearest role boundaries.
