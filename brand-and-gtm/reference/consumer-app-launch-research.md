## 1. WISPR FLOW

### What they are
AI-powered voice dictation app. Speak naturally, get clean formatted text. Mac (Oct 2024), Windows (Mar 2025), iOS (2025), Android (Feb 2026).

### Launch Strategy

**Personal onboarding as growth engine.** CEO Tanay Kothari personally onboarded the first 500 users on Google Meet. Not a demo call. He watched their mouse movements, eye tracking, facial expressions at every step. This let him identify the exact "delight moments" and systematically preserve them in the product.

**Result:** Within two months, user base went from single digits to hundreds to thousands, purely through organic sharing. 90% of growth was word of mouth. Zero salespeople on the team.

**VC seeding loop.** Every tier-one VC fund in the Valley started using Wispr Flow for emails, memos, docs. Because VCs used it daily, when fundraising came, there was no product explanation needed. Conversations went straight to vision and execution. This also created a downstream effect: VC portfolio founders got exposed to the product.

### Content & Platforms

**Two viral product launches** in five months, both going viral on X, LinkedIn, and ProductHunt simultaneously.

**The Porsche GT3 RS Challenge (Feb 2026, Android launch):**
- Offered 5 people a Porsche 911 GT3 RS if they could make Wispr produce a single transcription error
- Challengers spoke fast, mixed languages, used filler words, unusual names
- 3.5 million people watched the challenge video
- Comment "Porsche" to get a link to try it yourself (engagement bait that worked)
- Internet controversy when frame-by-frame analysis found errors, generating even MORE views
- Accompanying stunt: live activation outside Apple Store in Union Square with a full brass band, street interviews, signs roasting Siri
- Team joked: "Who let former band kids be in charge of marketing launches?"

**LinkedIn virality engine.** Users showcasing 3x faster workflows on LinkedIn drove external virality. Show-off moments (screen recordings of voice-to-text in action) created organic word-of-mouth in professional networks.

**Content philosophy:** Tell a relatable story, or bring up controversy. Wispr leaned hard on relatability: "This product changed my life" testimonials from real users.

### Creator/UGC Program

**Affiliate program (not a creator army):**
- 25% recurring commission on first year of customer payments
- 60-day cookie window
- Managed through Dub dashboard (real-time clicks, trials, earnings)
- Payouts monthly once balance hits $100
- Requirement: share unique link + FTC disclosure
- Target audience: productivity, tech, and creator communities

**No massive UGC army.** Growth was primarily organic/word-of-mouth, not paid creator campaigns.

### GTM Infrastructure

- **Affiliate tracking:** Dub (partnership platform with real-time dashboard)
- **No SEO.** Cut it entirely. "No one is searching for voice dictation." Most people don't know the tech has reached usability thresholds.
- **Extreme 80/20 rule.** Find the 20% giving 80% results. Then find a new 20% within that. Kill everything else.

### Team Structure

- 18 people at Series A (June 2025), grown to ~50 by late 2025
- Self-described as "a small, focused team of designers, marketers and engineers"
- Rejected ~1,000 candidates per hire
- Two months defining company values before first hire
- Four values: integrity, high agency, user obsession, detail orientation
- People managers from day one (reduces communication overhead)
- Reduced meeting time 75% after scaling past 5 people
- $10M revenue with 50 people (Oct 2025)

### What Worked (Breakout Moment)

The inflection point was the pivot from hardware (3 years building a wearable) to software. The moment the voice-to-text product shipped and users experienced half-second response time, organic sharing exploded. The breakout wasn't a single viral moment but compound word-of-mouth accelerated by VC network effects.

The Porsche challenge was the biggest single marketing moment, but growth was already 50% MoM before it.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **Personal onboarding obsession.** Jake or a team member doing 100+ video calls with early users, watching exactly where they get confused/delighted. Not surveys. Video calls.
2. **Seed the VC/founder network.** Get every investor, advisor, and connected founder using Tryps for their own trips. They become organic evangelists.
3. **Kill low-ROI channels ruthlessly.** If no one is searching for "group trip planning app," don't do SEO. Find where your users actually are.
4. **Bold product demos as content.** The Porsche challenge was a product confidence bet. Tryps equivalent: "Plan a 10-person international trip in under 5 minutes" challenge.
5. **Controversy generates more views than polish.** The Porsche video errors generated MORE engagement than if it had been perfect.

## 2. PARTIFUL

### What they are
Social event planning/invitation app. "Facebook Events for hot people." Founded 2020 by Joy Tao (CTO, ex-Palantir/Meta) and Shreya Murthy (CEO, ex-Palantir).

### Launch Strategy

**Zero paid ads. No marketing agency. Pure product-led growth.**

The viral loop is built into the product: every time someone creates an event and sends invites, every recipient sees the cover image, the brand, the vibe. Non-users get nudged to download. This is classic PLG but executed with a personality that most PLG playbooks completely lack.

**Key milestones:**
- 2020: Founded
- 2022: $20M Series A from a16z (total funding $27.3M)
- 2023: Millions of monthly active users. iPhone app launch.
- 2024: Google "Best App of 2024." Apple finalist for cultural impact. Android app launch.
- 2025: 500K MAU in Q1, 400% YoY growth. Added 2M new users in H1 2025. Time 100 Most Influential Companies. 60% weekly active rate.

### Content & Platforms

**TikTok:**
- Party planning ideas, themed gathering guides, aesthetic mood boards
- One post hit 6.1 million views featuring "hot girl party concepts"
- Shares content that educates, entertains, and inspires

**Instagram:**
- Showcases real parties hosted on the platform
- Film camera aesthetic photography (disposable camera energy)
- Builds FOMO through aspirational "it crowd" imagery
- Exclusivity signaling

**X (Twitter):**
- Self-aware, slightly unhinged brand voice
- The Apple Invites response: posted a screenshot of App Store rule 4.1 (copycat apps) instead of a press release. No corporate whining.
- User reviews highlight: "Whoever runs their Twitter account is hilarious and that's probably my dream job"

**The Guest List (Substack/Blog):**
- Editorial content that reads like a magazine for people who love hosting
- Party trend data, hosting guides, cultural essays, community spotlights
- In-house writer + guest contributors (creators like Daniela Meyler)
- Quality over quantity philosophy

**Creator partnerships (selective, not scaled):**
- Lukas Battle (comedian)
- Julian Shapiro-Barnum ("Recess Therapy")
- Brand collaborations (e.g., Lionsgate for The Housemaid screening)
- UGC amplification on TikTok (boosting organic content, not producing it)

### GTM Infrastructure

Minimal. Three-person marketing team. One hand-curator for Discover feed. One in-house writer. The product IS the marketing machine.

### Team Structure

- ~33 employees total
- **Three-person marketing department**
- One content curator for Discover feed
- One in-house writer for The Guest List
- Luis Ocampo (external initiatives)
- CEO Shreya Murthy is the brand voice steward

### What Worked (Breakout Moment)

No single breakout moment. Partiful "chased consistency over virality." The compounding effect of every event invite acting as a brand impression, combined with a genuinely distinctive brand voice, created steady exponential growth. The Google Best App of 2024 award was a validator, not a catalyst.

The Apple Invites copycat moment (Feb 2025) was their biggest earned media spike, turning a competitive threat into a flex.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **Product IS marketing.** Every Tryps trip invite should be a brand moment. Beautiful, shareable, personality-filled. The invite experience itself recruits new users.
2. **Brand voice is the moat.** Partiful sounds like real people who genuinely like parties. Tryps needs to sound like real people who genuinely love travel. Not corporate. Not productivity. Group chat energy.
3. **Three-person marketing team is enough.** Partiful hit millions of users with 3 marketers. Quality over quantity. One great writer, one curator, one person doing partnerships.
4. **Reactive social media.** Don't plan a content calendar 6 weeks out. Monitor culture, react fast, be funny. The Apple Invites meme was worth more than a month of planned content.
5. **Film camera aesthetic.** Already in Tryps brand system. Lean into it harder on social.
6. **Editorial content.** A Tryps equivalent of The Guest List: travel culture essays, group trip guides, destination mood boards. Not "5 tips for planning a group trip" but cultural, aspirational, entertaining.

## 3. LOCKET

### What they are
Photo-sharing widget. Send photos directly to friends' home screens in real time. Built in 14 days by Matt Moss (22, UCSB CS grad) as a gift for his long-distance girlfriend.

### Launch Strategy

**Origin story virality.** Moss posted a TikTok about building the app for his girlfriend. The video racked up millions of views. Organic TikTok sharing followed in waves.

**Growth metrics:**
- 2 million signups in first 2 weeks
- 5 million signups in first 3 weeks
- 30+ million total signups
- 9 million daily active users
- Nearly 2 billion photos sent
- Apple Cultural Impact Award winner

### Content & Platforms

**TikTok nano-influencer army (the core growth engine):**

**Structure:**
- 26 active ambassador accounts posting daily
- 46 total creators identified (underperformers get replaced)
- 52 videos posted every day across the network
- 4,984 total videos produced

**Content format (templatized):**
- 3-second close-up selfie video
- Hook text above face (e.g., "Life after deleting Snap", "I'm crying, my bf is so cute", "POV: long distance")
- Screen recording of Locket widget in action
- High-energy, believable emotional expressions

**Top hooks (by frequency):**
- "Life after deleting Snap" -- used 838 times
- "POV" and "long distance" -- each used 699 times
- "I'm crying, my [bf/mom/...] is so cute" -- "crying" variant used 423 times

**Payment:** One-off fee per video (not ongoing). Nano/micro-influencers only.

**Results:**
- 298 million total views
- 1.5 million shares
- 2.3 million bookmarks
- CPM under $1
- Consistent top 5-10 App Store ranking

**Retargeting hack:** When videos gained momentum, Locket commissioned response videos to comments from the same creators, effectively using them as free retargeting.

### GTM Infrastructure

- Internal creator management (no named platform mentioned)
- Performance monitoring with ruthless optimization (replace underperformers fast)
- Statistical approach: 70+ videos/day means at least some will hit the algorithm

### Team Structure

- ~10 people total
- Matt Moss (solo founder, built v1 in 14 days)
- Marketing intern role includes influencer outreach, video production, brand refinement
- Lean team with in-house content machine

### What Worked (Breakout Moment)

The original TikTok about building the app for his girlfriend was the ignition. But the sustained growth came from the systematic nano-influencer program: 26 accounts, 52 videos/day, templatized format, ruthless replacement of underperformers. Quantity beats quality on TikTok when you have a repeatable format.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **Templatized TikTok format.** Find ONE video format that works for Tryps and repeat it hundreds of times across dozens of accounts. "POV: the group chat actually agreed on a restaurant" -> screen recording of Tryps in action.
2. **Nano-influencer army at CPM under $1.** 26 accounts x 2-3 posts/day = 52-78 pieces of content daily. One-off payments per video. Replace underperformers ruthlessly.
3. **Hook database.** Build a hook library specific to group travel: "Life after someone else planned the trip", "POV: your friend who always says 'I'm down for whatever' actually committed", "I'm crying, my friends actually showed up."
4. **Comment response videos as retargeting.** When a Tryps TikTok gets traction, have the creator post response videos to top comments.
5. **Origin story content.** Locket's "I built this for my girlfriend" was authentic. Tryps equivalent: the real story of chaotic group trips that inspired the app.

## 4. BeREAL

### What they are
Photo sharing app. Daily random notification, 2-minute window, dual camera (front + back). Founded June 2019 by Alexis Barreyat and Kevin Perreau (France).

### Launch Strategy

**Slow burn then college ambassador explosion.**

**Timeline:**
- 2019: Founded
- 2020: App Store launch, mostly unknown
- 2021: Gradual growth to 500K users, mostly French college campuses. $30M Series A (a16z + Accel).
- Jan 2022: College ambassador program launches in UK and US
- Early 2022: Goes viral on TikTok organically
- 2022: iPhone App of the Year. 20 million DAU peak. $60M Series B at $586M valuation.
- 2023: DAU crashes to 6M (March), recovers to 20M (August)
- 2024: Acquired by Voodoo for $537M with 10 months of runway left

### The College Ambassador Program (the playbook)

**Payment structure:**
- $30 per referral
- $50 for an app download + review

**Campus activations:**
- Hosted parties at colleges (Harvard, others) where admission was FREE if you downloaded the app and added 5 friends
- Ambassadors managed their own marketing budgets
- Ambassadors hosted parties, identified key campus moments, executed their own creative activations
- Carefully curated based on campus influence

**Results:**
- 1,000% increase in downloads
- 315% increase in usage
- TikTok organic virality amplified the campus growth (students started making BeReal content on TikTok)

### Content & Platforms

BeReal didn't produce content. The PRODUCT was the content. The dual-camera, 2-minute-window mechanic was inherently shareable and created endless organic TikTok content from users.

### What Worked

The college ambassador program was the ignition switch. The product mechanic (random daily notification, 2-minute window) created urgency, FOMO, and social pressure that drove daily engagement.

### What Didn't Work

- Core mechanic (daily posting window) proved insufficient for long-term retention
- Close-friends-only model saturated individual networks quickly
- DAU collapsed from 20M to 6M after initial hype
- Churn rate 20.7%, only 9% of users opened daily
- Month-over-month growth went from 75% (April 2022) to 3% (December 2022)
- Acquired at below peak valuation with 10 months of runway

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **College campus ambassador program.** Travel is huge for college students (spring break, study abroad, graduation trips). Pay campus ambassadors $30-50 per referral. Give them budget to host trip-planning parties.
2. **Download + invite as admission.** "Free pregame if you download Tryps and add 5 friends." Direct conversion event.
3. **Let the product create the content.** Design Tryps moments that are inherently screenshottable/shareable. Trip countdowns, group photo collages, vote results.
4. **Geographic/campus-by-campus rollout.** Don't launch everywhere. Pick 5 college campuses, saturate them, then expand.

### What Tryps Should NOT Steal

- The daily notification mechanic. Worked for short-term engagement but killed retention.
- Over-reliance on one growth loop without monetization plan.

## 5. GAS APP (NIKITA BIER)

### What they are
Anonymous compliment app for high schoolers. Poll-based: "Who has the best smile?" with classmates as answer options. Pay to reveal who voted for you ("God Mode"). Founded by Nikita Bier (also created TBH, acquired by Meta for $30M).

### Launch Strategy (The School-by-School Playbook)

**Step-by-step Instagram hack:**

1. For each target high school, create a private Instagram account named after the school (e.g., `@gas.georgiahigh`)
2. Crawl the school's Instagram location page. Teens list their school in their bio.
3. Follow every account associated with that school
4. Leave a mysterious CTA in the bio: "You're invited to Gas at [School Name]. Stay tuned."
5. Keep the account PRIVATE. This builds curiosity. Teens see a follow request from their school's name.
6. At 3:00-4:00 PM on launch day (when school lets out), simultaneously:
   - Switch the account from private to public
   - Accept ALL pending follow requests at once
   - Add the App Store download link to the bio
7. Every student gets the "follow accepted" notification at the exact same time
8. Cascade effect: everyone downloads at once, the app is immediately full of classmates

**Geofenced rollout:**
- Started with a single Georgia high school (40% adoption on day one)
- Next day: 3 more schools
- Scaled to 300 schools
- Limited to 12 states initially to maintain scarcity
- State-by-state expansion created FOMO in states that didn't have it yet

**Growth metrics:**
- TBH: 5 million downloads in 9 weeks, 2.5 million DAU, acquired by Meta in 48 days
- Gas: 1 million DAU by October 2022, 30,000 new users/hour at peak, 10 million users in 3 months
- Gas revenue: $7M in 2023
- $0 spent on paid marketing for either app

### Team Structure

- 4-5 people total
- Nikita Bier + Isaiah Turner + Dave Schatz + Michael Gutierrez + 1
- $1M revenue within 10 days of Gas launch

### Key Principles (from Nikita Bier)

1. **Three reasons people download apps:** Find a mate, make/save money, unplug from reality
2. **Core value in 3 seconds.** If users don't "get it" in 3 seconds, you've lost them.
3. **Invitations sent per user drops 20% for every year of age from 13 to 18.** Younger users are the best viral vectors.
4. **Launch from your couch with zero dependencies on other people.** MVP must self-propagate.
5. **Big tech takes 12-24 months to respond.** You have a window.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **The Instagram school page tactic, adapted for colleges.** Create `@tryps.mit`, `@tryps.usc`, etc. Follow students. Go private. Launch at a coordinated time. This is directly applicable to Tryps' college strategy.
2. **Geofenced rollout for scarcity.** Don't launch in every city. Launch at 5 colleges. "Tryps is now available at MIT." FOMO drives demand.
3. **Coordinated timing.** Launch at the exact moment your audience is most active (end of class, Friday afternoon before spring break planning).
4. **Core value in 3 seconds.** When someone opens Tryps for the first time, they need to immediately see: "Oh, this is how my friend group plans trips now."
5. **Monetization from day one.** Gas charged for "God Mode" (reveal who voted for you). Tryps should have premium value from launch, not "we'll figure it out later."

## 6. POKE AI

### What they are
AI assistant that lives inside iMessage/WhatsApp/SMS. Connect email, calendar, files. It drafts replies, manages invoices, reschedules meetings, books travel. Founded by Marvin von Hagen and Felix Schlegel (met at a middle school hackathon in Germany, led 65 engineers to win SpaceX's Not-a-Boring Competition).

### Launch Strategy

**VC insider seeding (Wispr Flow playbook variant):**
- 4-month private beta with 6,000 Silicon Valley insiders
- 200,000 messages/month during beta
- Near-perfect retention among beta users
- Users started replacing ChatGPT with Poke organically

**$15M seed at $100M valuation** from General Catalyst, Village Global, Earlybird VC, plus angels from PayPal, Coinbase, Dropbox, Vercel, Google.

### Content & Platforms

**The Paris short film.** Instead of a typical "we're excited to announce" startup video, Poke released a short film: a love story set in Paris that naturally shows the product. No feature list. No demo. A story.

**Philosophy:** "We'd rather tell a story than make claims. We'd rather show the product naturally than list features."

**Dynamic pricing as brand statement.** Pricing adjusts based on who you are, taking "student discounts" to an extreme. This became a talking point itself.

### Team Structure

- Engineering talent from Jane Street, Apple, Robinhood, Amazon
- Research backgrounds at Stanford, MIT, Cambridge
- Previous roles at Tesla, Apple
- General Catalyst provided PR support and strategic introductions

### What Worked (Breakout Moment)

The VC insider beta created word-of-mouth among the most connected people in tech. When the product launched publicly, it already had 6,000 power users who were evangelists. The Paris short film generated earned media and stood out from every other "we raised money" announcement.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **Short film instead of launch video.** Don't make a product demo. Make a 2-minute film about a group of friends trying to plan a trip, and Tryps naturally solves the chaos. Sean could produce this.
2. **VC/insider seeding.** Get the 100 most connected people in your network using Tryps before public launch. Jackson can help identify these people.
3. **Dynamic pricing as brand.** "Student discount" taken to the extreme. College students are the core demo for group trips.
4. **Story over features in all content.** Every piece of Tryps content should be a story about friends, not a feature walkthrough.

## 7. CLAUDE AI (@claudeai on X)

### What they are
Anthropic's AI assistant. The @claudeai account on X launched July 2025 and has 956K followers.

### Brand Strategy

**Anti-hype positioning.** In a market where every AI company races to ship faster and be bigger, Anthropic built its identity on: "What if responsible development is actually the competitive advantage?"

**Brand personality designed by ethicists, not marketers.** The tone is thoughtful, precise, principled. Avoids hype. Communicates with clarity and caution.

**Visual language:** Hand-drawn illustration system. Talks about deeply technical work in a way that feels accessible. "This is serious work, but it belongs to everyone."

### "Keep Thinking" Campaign (Sept 2025, with Mother agency)

**The campaign:**
- 90-second hero ad: "There's never been a worse time... there's never been a better time"
- Targets "the people up at 3am because they can't let go of an idea"
- Multimillion-dollar media investment across Netflix, Hulu, NYT, WSJ, top podcasts

**Super Bowl Campaign ("A Time and a Place", Feb 2026):**
- Four darkly comedic spots asking "Does advertising belong everywhere?"
- Two ads during Super Bowl 60 reaching ~120 million viewers
- At ~$10M for a 30-second slot, Anthropic spent big on ads saying they won't show ads
- Won 2026 Super Clio for Creative Excellence
- Directed by Jeff Low (Biscuit Filmworks), created by Mother

**The irony play worked.** Making ads about not having ads created a distinctive brand moment that every competitor's feature-focused ad couldn't match.

### Social Presence

The @claudeai account maintains the same thoughtful, slightly witty tone as the product itself. Not "brand twitter." Not "unhinged." More like a very smart friend who happens to be an AI.

### What Tryps Can Steal

1. **Counter-positioning.** Every travel app is either a booking tool (Kayak) or an influencer-driven luxury play (Fora). Tryps is neither. Lean into the counter-position: "We're not a travel app. We're the friend who actually plans the trip."
2. **Personality designed into the product, not bolted on.** Claude's personality comes from the model itself. Tryps' personality should come from the agent itself (the iMessage agent, push notifications, in-app copy).
3. **Irony and self-awareness in advertising.** Anthropic made ads about not having ads. Tryps could make content about not being another travel app: "We're not going to show you sunset photos. We're going to get your friends to actually commit to a date."
4. **Premium brand partners (when ready).** Mother agency, Super Bowl. This is aspirational for Tryps, but the principle applies: when you DO spend on marketing, spend on quality creative, not volume.
5. **Hand-drawn, accessible visual language.** Tryps already has the film camera/disposable aesthetic. This is the same principle: make tech feel human and warm.

## SYNTHESIS: THE TRYPS PLAYBOOK

### Patterns Across All 7 Brands

| Tactic | Who Did It | Applicability to Tryps |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Product IS the marketing (viral loops built in) | Partiful, BeReal, Locket | HIGH -- every trip invite = brand moment |
| Personal onboarding of first 500 users | Wispr Flow | HIGH -- Jake should do this |
| VC/insider network seeding | Wispr Flow, Poke | HIGH -- Jackson + advisors |
| College ambassador program | BeReal, Gas | HIGH -- MIT is Jake's campus |
| Nano-influencer TikTok army | Locket | MEDIUM -- needs content format first |
| School-by-school Instagram hack | Gas (Nikita Bier) | HIGH -- adaptable to colleges |
| Short film instead of launch video | Poke | HIGH -- Sean can produce |
| Editorial content (blog/newsletter) | Partiful | MEDIUM -- later stage |
| Reactive social media (memes, culture) | Partiful | HIGH -- low cost, high impact |
| Bold product demo as content | Wispr Flow (Porsche) | HIGH -- "plan a 10-person trip in 5 min" |
| Kill low-ROI channels ruthlessly | Wispr Flow | HIGH -- don't do SEO if nobody searches |
| Counter-positioning | Claude AI | HIGH -- "not another travel app" |
| Story over features | Poke, Claude | HIGH -- friends, not features |
| $0 marketing budget plays | Gas, Locket | HIGH -- Instagram hack costs nothing |

### Priority Plays for Tryps (Ranked)

**Tier 1: Do immediately (pre-launch / launch week)**

1. **Trip invite as viral loop.** Every invite a non-user receives should be beautiful, personality-filled, and convert to download. This is the Partiful play. The invite IS the ad.

2. **College campus rollout starting at MIT.** Jake is going to MIT. Create `@tryps.mit` on Instagram. Follow every student. Go private. Launch at a coordinated time (Friday before spring break). Expand to 5 more schools. This is the Gas/Nikita Bier play.

3. **Personal onboarding of first 100-500 users.** Video calls, not surveys. Watch their faces. This is the Wispr Flow play.

4. **Short film, not a launch video.** 2-3 minute film about a real group of friends trying to plan a trip. The chaos, the group chat, the flaking. Then Tryps. Sean produces. This is the Poke play.

**Tier 2: Do within first month**

5. **Seed the advisor/VC/founder network.** Get 50-100 connected people using Tryps for their actual trips before public launch. They become evangelists. This is the Wispr Flow / Poke play.

6. **TikTok content format + nano-creator program.** Find ONE repeatable format. "POV: someone actually planned the group trip." 15-20 nano-creators, 2-3 posts/day each, one-off payment per video. This is the Locket play.

7. **Reactive social media from day one.** Three-person (or one-person) social team. Monitor culture. Post fast. Be funny. Not a content calendar. A vibe. This is the Partiful play.

**Tier 3: Do within first quarter**

8. **Bold product demo challenge.** "Plan a 10-person Bali trip in under 5 minutes using Tryps. If we fail, [something]. If we succeed, [something]." Film it. This is the Wispr Flow Porsche play.

9. **Campus ambassador program.** Pay college students $30-50 per referral. Give them budget for trip-planning parties. "Download Tryps and add 5 friends = free entry." This is the BeReal play.

10. **Editorial content.** "The Trip Report" -- a newsletter about group travel culture. Not tips. Culture. This is the Partiful Guest List play.

### What NOT to Do

- Don't spend on SEO early (Wispr Flow lesson)
- Don't plan a content calendar 6 weeks out (Partiful lesson)
- Don't rely on one viral moment for sustained growth (BeReal lesson)
- Don't launch everywhere at once (Gas lesson)
- Don't make a feature walkthrough video (Poke lesson)
- Don't use the word "crew" (Jake's rule)

### Team Implication

Partiful did this with 3 marketers. Locket did it with ~10 people total. Gas did it with 4-5 people. Wispr Flow started with 18.

Tryps doesn't need a big GTM team. It needs:
1. One person who owns social and can write in brand voice (reactive, funny, fast)
2. One person who manages the creator/ambassador program (TikTok + campus)
3. Sean for creative production (short film, brand content)
4. Jake for strategy, VC seeding, personal onboarding
