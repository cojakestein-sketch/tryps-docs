Three-Layer Architecture

Each layer reinforces the others. We're building the only product that integrates all three.

Layer 1
iMessage
Where conversations happen. Zero behavior change.
  • Natural language trip creation
  • Group polling via Linq
  • Expense logging by text
  • Daily facilitation check-ins
LINQ
Layer 2
Mobile App
Where plans take shape. Visual, interactive.
  • Trip itinerary + drag-and-drop
  • Expense tracking & splitting
  • Travel DNA & preferences
  • Trip completeness checklist
STRIPE
Layer 3
Agentic Layer
Where execution happens. AI acts on behalf.
  • Flight & stay search + booking
  • Vote-on-behalf from preferences
  • Memory that learns over time
  • Personalized recommendations

Competitive Differential

Our Right to Win

First to market with native polling in iMessage

We're building the agent where people already coordinate — their group chat. If we can make it effortless to share preferences, we win in this era of human-shared, agent-executed decisions.

Data Moat: Shared Trip Memory

No one else holds the full record of what a group decided, preferred, and experienced together. More trips = smarter agent = more return usage.

Three Layers, One Product

We're building the only product that combines iMessage, a native mobile app, and autonomous booking into one seamless experience.

The Group Traveler (20s–30s)

People who believe travel is innate. Bachelor/bachelorette parties, friend groups, reunions. Willing to spend — looking for someone to handle it.

The Grill: Agent as Facilitator

Our agent runs on schedule, steering the group toward completion — polling, guiding, nudging — until the trip is booked. Facilitation, not dictation.
