# Tryps Brand Intake Questionnaire

**Purpose:** Once every question below is answered, a designer has everything they need to build the complete brand book. Each question is tagged with the brand book section it feeds and whether it must be answered before design begins or can be refined later.

**Priority Key:**
- **[MUST]** = Must answer before any design work begins
- **[REFINE]** = Can start with a rough answer and sharpen during the design process

---

## 1. Brand Purpose & Mission

These answers drive the **Brand Story**, **Mission Statement**, and **Brand Values** pages of the brand book.

### Origin & Why

1. **Why did you start Tryps? What personal frustration or moment sparked it?**
   Feeds: Brand Story page, Founder narrative
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Planning a trip to Chile with my roommate Quinn. Quinn wanted to plan every detail — Google Docs, Notion, AI research scattered everywhere. I wanted to show up spontaneously. There was no way to consolidate everything, and no easy way for me (the reluctant participant) to engage with what Quinn (the ultra-planner) was trying to get the group to do. I saw the Partiful playbook and thought: nobody's done this for trips.

2. **Complete this sentence: "Tryps exists because ________."**
   Feeds: Mission Statement
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Tryps exists because everyone wants the trip to be amazing but people have wildly different levels of effort they want to put into planning. Some want to control every detail, some want to be surprised. Everyone wants their viewpoint heard — they just don't all want to do the work. Tryps lets you communicate your vibe and it all happens through iMessage.

3. **If Tryps disappeared tomorrow, what would users genuinely miss that they cannot get anywhere else?**
   Feeds: Mission Statement, Value Proposition headline
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Planning a trip via iMessage where everything actually gets done and logs to the itinerary. No more copying from the group chat to a Google Doc. Plus the trip card — a fun, shareable artifact you actually want to send to friends. Not a spreadsheet link, a card.

4. **What is the single most important problem Tryps solves? (One sentence, no commas.)**
   Feeds: Tagline exploration, Hero copy on all brand materials
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** People have a vibe for a trip but don't know how to execute on it across a group. Three phases: coordination before (vibe → plan), "where am I supposed to be" during, and codifying what you enjoyed after. Plus recommendations from friends who've been there to curate future trips.

5. **What does the world look like in 5 years if Tryps succeeds at scale?**
   Feeds: Vision Statement page
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Logo on the side of a plane. Tryps is the one-stop shop for all things travel — flights, bookings, everything end-to-end. Anything relating to you leaving your hometown and going somewhere else goes through Tryps. Everyone plans all their trips through Tryps. It's huge.

6. **What would you never compromise on, even if it slowed growth?**
   Feeds: Brand Values page
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Everyone uses Tryps for free. Always. A pro plan may exist eventually, but the core experience — planning, vibing, coordinating with your crew — is never paywalled. Getting someone into Tryps has zero friction, zero cost.

7. **List 3-5 core values that guide every product and business decision.**
   Feeds: Brand Values page, internal culture section
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:**
   > 1. **Sexy & Clean** — The app looks so good your coolest friends want to be seen using it. If it doesn't look like something you'd screenshot, it's not done.
   > 2. **Shareworthy** — You feel cool sending it. Sharing Tryps is a flex, not an ask.
   > 3. **Obvious** — A five-year-old and a grandmother both get it instantly. Nothing needs explanation. "Oh of course this exists."
   > 4. **Come Correct** — Every touchpoint is intentional. Nothing half-assed. From the copy to the colors to the push notification — we meant to do that.

8. **What is the deeper emotional outcome Tryps delivers beyond logistics? (e.g., "belonging," "spontaneity," "feeling included")**
   Feeds: Brand Essence / Brand Promise page
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** Spontaneity, fun, and reconnection. "I have a group chat from college — I haven't seen these boys in a year or two. If I could just send a Tryps link, it would probably get a lot of people to actually fly to a specific location. I'd get to see them more often. I miss these boys. Tryps helps me do that."

## 2. Target Audience & User Persona

These answers drive the **Audience Personas**, **User Journey Maps**, and inform every visual and verbal decision in the book.

### The Core 1% Power User (Trent's Question #1)

9. **Who is your single most important user archetype? Describe them: age, lifestyle, income bracket, social habits, travel frequency.**
   Feeds: Primary Persona card in the brand book
   Priority: **[MUST]**

   > **ANSWERED:** 20–30 years old, just post-grad. They have a little bit of money after college. Pretty social people who travel a couple times a year — more than the average person. They like going on trips, and in particular they have friends who do NOT live in the same city, and they want to get coordinated to go on a trip together.

10. **What does this person's weekend look like? What apps are already on their home screen?**
    Feeds: Persona context, competitive landscape
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** They're social. Probably drinking with their friends. Trying to go somewhere cool. Taking a weekend trip maybe once a month or once every other month. Apps on home screen: Instagram, Pinterest, maybe TikTok, maybe Twitter. They use Canva. They definitely use Partiful. They may use Kayak or Expedia.

11. **What is this person's relationship to group travel today -- are they the organizer, the joiner, or the reluctant participant?**
    Feeds: Persona card, messaging hierarchy
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** The core 1% power user is definitely the organizer — the planner who gets everybody else involved. The other core power user is the reluctant participant who just wants to show up and make it as easy as possible to contribute without feeling like they have to give too much. Those are the extremes, but the real 1% is the planner.

12. **What social platforms does this person spend the most time on? How do they communicate with friends (text, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, Discord)?**
    Feeds: Social/Content Strategy section, Channel selection
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** They text and iMessage mostly.

13. **What brands does this person already love and identify with? (Not just travel -- fashion, food, tech, lifestyle.)**
    Feeds: Brand Affinity map, Visual Direction moodboard
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** (Via brand A/B test exercise)
    >
    > **Fashion:** Aritzia, Aimé Leon Dore, Patagonia, New Balance — quality-aware and culture-forward but not flashy. Knows what's cool without trying too hard.
    >
    > **Food & Drink:** Sweetgreen, natural wine, Blue Bottle — taste-driven, willing to pay more for quality experiences. Not pretentious, but has a palate.
    >
    > **Tech & Apps:** Chrome, Apple Notes, IKEA — function-first on daily tools. Not a systems nerd. Just wants stuff that works.
    >
    > **Social & Community:** Instagram, Spotify, Strava, Venmo, Partiful — social-native across everything. Shares activity, playlists, splits. The social layer matters.
    >
    > **Travel:** Marriott (pragmatic — points and loyalty matter), open to both deal-hunting and premium convenience.
    >
    > **Media:** A24, The Ringer — culturally literate, indie-leaning taste but not snobby about it.
    >
    > **Grooming/Wellness:** Aesop (cares about aesthetics in personal space), Alo (tentative), Strava (fitness is social and tracked).
    >
    > **Summary profile:** This person has taste but doesn't perform it. They pick quality over hype, function over systems, and social over private. They're culturally aware without being pretentious. They'd rather look effortlessly good than obviously expensive.

### Pain Points (Trent's Question #2)

16. **List every specific pain point Tryps solves. Be granular. (e.g., "17 texts to pick a date," "Venmo chaos after the trip," "one person does all the work")**
    Feeds: Messaging matrix, Feature benefit copy, Ad creative briefs
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:**
    > - Way too many texts to pick a date
    > - Nobody's ever free — and then you DO pick a date and people still aren't free
    > - Venmo chaos for logging expenses after the trip
    > - One person does all the work and resents it
    > - You have a group chat you'd love to activate but nobody takes the initiative because planning a whole trip is too much responsibility
    > - The planner doesn't enjoy the trip as much — they're worried about everyone else having a good time
    > - Nobody knows when the trip is happening or what the deal is
    > - Plans live in scattered group chats, get buried, get lost
    > - People are unhappy with decisions they didn't get a say in
    > - Trips aren't as good as they could be because the process is broken

17. **Which single pain point, if you solved only that one, would still make Tryps worth downloading?**
    Feeds: Primary value proposition, Tagline candidates
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Democratizing the planning process. Planning a trip becomes the responsibility of the entire group, not just one person. That single shift — from "one planner carries the burden" to "everyone contributes a little" — is worth the download alone.

18. **What do users currently do to solve these problems without Tryps? (Google Sheets, group chats, one friend doing everything?)**
    Feeds: Competitive Positioning page, "Before/After" narrative
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** One friend does everything on a Google Sheet, manually. Plans come out of group chats — stuff is all over the place, gets lost. The trips aren't as good as they could be.

### Retention & Engagement (Trent's Question #3)

19. **Why do users come back? What is the specific trigger or moment that brings them back into the app?**
    Feeds: Brand Promise, Lifecycle messaging guidelines
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** The last trip they got invited to via Tryps was just so easy — "oh yeah, of course, this is the obvious way to organize trips." Plus it already has their data, knows everything about them, so next time it's even easier. They keep everything travel-related stored in one place and can just do everything on here.

20. **How often does a typical user plan a group trip? What happens between trips -- does Tryps have a role or go dormant?**
    Feeds: Content strategy cadence, Engagement loop documentation
    Priority: **[REFINE]**

    > **ANSWERED:** A couple times a year. Between trips, Tryps has a robust engagement layer:
    > - **Explore tab** — 3D globe where you mark countries visited, maintain a ranked top-5 bucket list, and see where friends have been or want to go
    > - **Travel DNA** — "this or that" quiz that builds your travel personality profile (used for recommendations & friend matching)
    > - **Shared Travel Wishes** — shows countries where you and friends both wishlisted, with a "Plan" CTA to start a trip there
    > - **Friend Activity Feed** — see when friends create trips, add to bucket lists, complete Travel DNA, join Tryps
    > - **People tab** — browse friend profiles with stats (countries visited, tripmates, past trips, wishlists)
    > - **Calendar tab** — timeline of all upcoming trips with countdown
    > - **Discover Feed** — browse/like/clone public or curated trip templates
    > - **Profile completeness ring + QR sharing** — gamified profile buildout
    >
    > The between-trip layer is built — the question is whether users know it's there and find it compelling enough to open the app when they're not actively planning.

21. **What is the "aha moment" -- the point in the user experience where someone goes from curious to committed?**
    Feeds: Onboarding flow design, Key screen visual treatment
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Two aha moments:
    > 1. **The iMessage agent** — you add this agent to iMessage and it's actually your travel agent on your behalf. That's the "holy shit this is real" moment.
    > 2. **The trip card** — you see a beautiful Partiful-style card that says "so-and-so wants to go on a trip to New York" and you're like "oh fuck yeah, I want in." The card is the viral hook — you HAVE to get on Tryps because you want to go on that trip.

These answers drive the **Brand Personality**, **Tone of Voice**, and **Copywriting Guidelines** pages of the brand book.

### Personality Definition

22. **If Tryps were a person at a party, who are they? How do they act, dress, and talk?**
    Feeds: Brand Personality page (the "character" description)
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** The most social person at the party. They love to travel. They're friendly — that's a big thing. They dress sleek, look good, but not gaudy or designer. More utility-chic — Arc'teryx, Patagonia vibe. Not trying to show off. They just want connection, want to see their friends. They talk in a way that's inviting.

23. **Pick 3-5 adjectives that describe how Tryps should feel. (e.g., effortless, inclusive, energizing, warm, playful)**
    Feeds: Brand Personality keyword system
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Effortless. Inviting. Easy. Obvious. Undeniable — as in, "this is obviously the way we're planning the trip."

24. **Pick 3-5 adjectives that Tryps should NEVER feel like. (e.g., corporate, pushy, exclusive, complicated)**
    Feeds: Brand Personality "Not This" guardrails
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Corporate. Difficult. Gimmicky — not "look at this trick," but a pristine, best-in-class service. Never a trick. Never feels like it might not actually work.

25. **If Tryps were a celebrity, public figure, or fictional character, who would it be and why?**
    Feeds: Brand Personality reference point, Designer shorthand
    Priority: **[REFINE]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Anthony Bourdain. Traveled everywhere, genuinely curious, warm, no pretense, loved connection over luxury. Made you want to go places and meet people.

26. **What existing brands (in any industry) have a personality closest to what you want for Tryps?**
    Feeds: Tone moodboard, Reference library for the designer
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Partiful, Poke AI, Venmo, Beli. All share a quality of being social-native, fun to use, and feeling like they just work without being gimmicky.

### Voice & Tone

27. **Where does Tryps fall on each of these spectrums? (Mark a position)**
    Feeds: Tone of Voice scale diagram in the brand book
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:**
    > - Formal `--○-------` Casual (leans casual)
    > - Serious `--○-------` Playful (leans playful)
    > - Respectful `--○-------` Irreverent (stays respectful)
    > - Enthusiastic `--○-------` Matter-of-fact (stays enthusiastic)

28. **Write a sample push notification in the voice you imagine for Tryps. (e.g., trip reminder, friend joined, poll results are in)**
    Feeds: Voice examples page, Do/Don't copy samples
    Priority: **[REFINE]**

    > **ANSWERED:**
    > - **Trip reminder:** "hey guys - excited for new york next week!"
    > - **Friend joined:** "cameron is now on tryps! see where he's been or plan a trip with him!"
    > - **Poll results:** "Beach day won (4 votes to 1) - what day should we plan it for?"
    >
    > Note: Lowercase, warm, conversational. Feels like a text from a friend, not a system notification.

29. **Should Tryps ever use slang, emojis, or Gen-Z language? Where is the line?**
    Feeds: Copywriting Guidelines, Do/Don't page
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Not really. It shouldn't feel robotic, but when agents use emojis and slang it looks tacky. Keep it natural and human without reaching for trendy language.

30. **Does the voice change depending on context? (e.g., playful in social, more trustworthy in payments/billing, warmer in onboarding)**
    Feeds: Tone modulation chart (by context/channel)
    Priority: **[REFINE]**

    > **ANSWERED:** Yes. Playful in social, more trustworthy in payments/billing, warmer in onboarding.

31. **What is the one thing every piece of Tryps copy should make the reader feel?**
    Feeds: Voice north star / guiding principle
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** "This is not some tacky gimmicky service. This is the way everybody is planning their trip this summer. This is the way forward — finally there's actually a solution worth using." The feeling = legitimacy + inevitability. Tryps is the obvious answer.

48. **Complete this positioning statement: "For [target user] who [need/pain], Tryps is the [category] that [key benefit], unlike [competitor/alternative] which [competitor limitation]."**
    Feeds: Positioning Statement page (the single most important strategic sentence)
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:** "For the post-grad who just wants to see her friends back home, Tryps is the way she gets to see them all, unlike Expedia or Kayak, which is inherently not social."

49. **Who are your top 5 competitors or alternatives? (Include both direct competitors and indirect alternatives like group chats or spreadsheets.)**
    Feeds: Competitive landscape map
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:**
    > 1. Group chats (iMessage, WhatsApp)
    > 2. Spreadsheets (Google Sheets)
    > 3. Partiful
    > 4. Kayak
    > 5. Expedia
    > (Likely more — needs further competitive research)

50. **For each competitor, what do they do well and what do they get wrong?**
    Feeds: Competitive landscape map, Opportunity gap identification
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > **ANSWERED:**
    > - **Partiful** — Great at social coordination, but you can't text it. Feels too hard to actually see what's happening beyond "here's an event, show up at this time."

    Feeds: Category definition, Positioning strategy
    Priority: **[MUST]**

    Priority: **[MUST]**

    > Tryps needs this same trick. The "t" needs to feel like it's going somewhere. One early idea was an arrow — didn't love it. Another thought: something with the T and Y interacting, like they're "tripping" each other. Open brief for the designer — this is the puzzle to crack.
