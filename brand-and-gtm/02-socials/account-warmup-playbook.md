# Social Account Warm-Up Playbook

## Executive Summary

We have 25 days from warm-up start (April 7) until launch (May 2). Our brand accounts on Instagram, TikTok, and X are new or nearly new. All four platforms aggressively flag new accounts that spike activity — posting 5x/week from a cold account will get us shadowbanned before launch week.

**The fix:** A structured warm-up that mimics real human behavior. Browse before posting. Engage before broadcasting. Ramp slowly.

**Key constraints:**

| Platform | Warm-up needed | Days available | Margin | Risk |
|----------|---------------|----------------|--------|------|
| Instagram | 21 days | 25 | 4 days | HIGH — tightest window |
| Facebook | Same as IG (shared Meta trust) | 25 | 4 days | HIGH — same Meta system |
| TikTok | 7-10 days | 25 | 15-18 days | MEDIUM — most forgiving |
| X | 14 days | 25 | 11 days | LOW — least aggressive |

**Sean owns warm-up on all four platforms.** He handles the accounts, does all the browsing/liking/posting during warm-up. Enej builds the infra (Metricool setup, analytics, cross-posting automation) once each platform is ready.

**Sean starts by Monday April 7 (Day 1). All four platforms, same day:**

1. Instagram — 15 min browsing travel content, likes only, no posts, no follows, no DMs
2. Facebook — set up page, connect to IG in Meta Business Suite, browse travel groups, like 3-5 posts
3. TikTok — 25 min watching travel TikToks (full watch, don't rapid-scroll), like 10-15 videos, follow 15-20 US travel creators. No posts.
4. X — log in, scroll 5 min, like 1-2 travel/tech posts. Nothing else.

**Four rules for the next 10 days:**

1. No scheduling tools (Metricool) on Instagram until April 17. Post natively from phone.
2. No links in any tweet until April 17.
3. No feed posts on Instagram until April 12 at the earliest. Stories first.
4. No feed posts on Facebook until Instagram has its first feed post.

**The content calendar starts April 11.** Instagram will be on day 4 of warm-up — this is tight. Week 1 of the content calendar should scale back to 3 posts/platform instead of 5 on IG and TikTok to stay within safe warm-up limits. By Week 2 (April 18+) we're past the critical warm-up period and can ramp to full cadence.

**Seed followers:** We need 50 followers by April 11 and 100 by April 14. Jake asks 20 friends + team + advisors to follow @tryps this week. An account with 0 followers posting content looks fake.

**Profile pictures and art are still being finalized by the design team.** They'll get updated on the design timeline. Warm-up can begin with placeholder or interim assets — what matters is the behavioral signals (browsing, liking, engaging), not the visual polish.

**Sean does all Instagram warm-up activity from his phone, logged into the @tryps account.** This keeps the device, IP, and behavior pattern consistent. Do not log in from multiple devices during the first 2 weeks.

**Note: Starting April 7 means IG warm-up completes around April 28 — only 4 days before launch. This is tight but workable. Do not skip days.**

### Instagram — What Gets You Flagged

**Instant shadowban triggers:**
- Using banned hashtags (search any hashtag first — if "recent posts are hidden," avoid it entirely)
- Unauthorized automation tools (anything that requires your IG password instead of OAuth)
- Following/unfollowing more than 30 people/day in week 1-2

**High-risk behaviors:**
- Same hashtag set on every post (change weekly minimum)
- More than 1 feed post/day before account is 2 weeks old
- Using a scheduling tool before day 10 (Meta detects API-based posting on new accounts)
- Engagement from accounts that don't follow you right after posting (looks like a pod)
- Switching between multiple IP addresses or devices
- Logging in from multiple devices in the first 2 weeks

**Hard ceilings (never exceed, any week):**
- 150 likes/hour
- 60 comments/hour
- 60 follows or unfollows/hour
- 30 hashtags/post (but only use 3-5 during warm-up)

**Shadowban recovery times:** Minor (banned hashtag): 2-7 days. Moderate (bot-like activity): 7-14 days. Severe (automation tools): 14-30 days. Some are permanent.

## TikTok Warm-Up (7-10 Days) — Sean Owns

TikTok is the most forgiving platform. The algorithm is content-first — even 0-follower accounts can go viral. But "newborn shadowban" is real: accounts that post immediately get reach-throttled, sometimes permanently.

The warm-up is less about "looking real" and more about training the algorithm to show our content to the right audience. Every video we watch during warm-up teaches TikTok what niche we're in.

### Days 1-2 (Apr 7-8) — Browse Only

| Action | Daily limit |
|--------|------------|
| Browse FYP | 25 min/day |
| Watch videos fully (no rapid-scrolling) | 50/day |
| Like videos in travel niche | 10-15/day |
| Follow travel creators | 15-20 on day 1, then 5-8/day |
| Save trending sounds | 5 total |
| Post | 0 |

**Be specific with browsing.** Don't watch random viral content. Watch: group trip planning videos, travel hack content, destination reveals, travel vlogs, trip coordination content. This trains the algorithm to categorize us correctly.

### Day 3 (Apr 9) — First Post

Post a 15-second intro video. Not promotional, not polished. Just "who we are and what you'll see here."

- Open with a hook in the first 2 seconds
- 100-character caption max
- 2 relevant hashtags (not 4+)
- Post at 2-4 PM ET
- After posting: spend 30 min replying to every comment and engaging with similar creators

**Target: 150-250 views in first 24 hours.** If you cross 400, it's breaking out of the test pool.

### Days 4-7 (Apr 10-13) — Build

| Action | Daily limit |
|--------|------------|
| Post | 1 video every 1-2 days |
| Likes | 10-15/day |
| Comments | 5-10/day |
| Follows | 5-8/day |

Day 4: Duet or Stitch a saved clip (links your content graph to theirs). Day 5: 30-second tip/tutorial with captions (70% of US users watch muted). Days 6-7: Try a 2-part mini-series.

**No links, no CTAs, no promotional language in your first 10 videos.** TikTok flags this from new accounts.

### Days 8-14 (Apr 14-20) — Daily Posting

| Action | Daily limit |
|--------|------------|
| Post | 1/day |
| Likes | 15-20/day |
| Comments | 8-12/day |
| Follows | 10-15/day |

Keep videos 15-30 seconds. Shorter clips have higher completion rates, and completion rate is the #1 trust signal for new accounts. Avoid highly produced content — the algorithm interprets professional editing from a new account as potential spam.

**Can connect Metricool by day 7 (April 14).** TikTok doesn't penalize scheduled posts through official API partners.

### Days 15+ (Apr 21+) — Full Cadence

1-2 videos/day. Longer content OK (30-60 seconds). Trending sounds. Pre-launch teasers. Start introducing links cautiously.

### TikTok — What Gets You Flagged

- Following 100+ accounts on day 1
- Liking 50+ videos in 5 minutes
- Posting 5+ videos in an hour
- More than 4 hashtags per post (signals spam)
- Links or CTAs in first 10 videos
- Highly produced content from a brand-new account
- Managing multiple accounts on one device/IP

**Hard ceilings:** 50 likes/hour, 30 follows/unfollows per day, 15 videos/day via API, 4 hashtags/post.

**Shadowban detection:** If analytics show 0% of views from FYP and 100% from "Personal Profile," you're shadowbanned.

## Shadowban Detection

Check these signals daily:

**Instagram:** Post a Story with a poll or question sticker. If zero non-followers see it, you may be restricted. Check Insights — if reach drops to 0 for non-followers, investigate.

**Facebook:** Check page Insights. If post reach drops to near-zero, the page may be restricted. FB is less aggressive than IG but shares the same Meta trust system.

**TikTok:** Check Analytics. If 0% of views come from FYP and 100% from "Personal Profile" or "Following," you are shadowbanned.

**X:** Search for your own tweets while logged out. If they don't appear in search results, you may be restricted.

**Sean checks all four platforms daily.** If shadowbanned on any platform: stop all activity for 24-48 hours. Delete any content that might have triggered it (banned hashtags, links posted too early). Resume at lower activity levels. Minor bans clear in 2-7 days. Flag in Slack immediately.

## Account Type: Creator vs Business — Why This Matters

Both Instagram and TikTok offer Business and Creator account types. **Use Creator accounts on both platforms.**

**Why not Business:**
- Business accounts on TikTok lose access to the full commercial sound library — you can't use trending sounds, which kills reach on a platform driven by audio trends
- Business accounts on Instagram historically had lower organic reach (Meta pushes them toward paid)
- Creator accounts get full analytics, full music library, and better algorithmic treatment for organic content

**When to switch to Business:** Only if/when we need Instagram Shopping, advanced ad tools, or API features that require a Business account. For launch, Creator is the right call.

**Facebook is the exception** — FB pages are inherently Business entities. There's no "Creator page" equivalent that matters here.
