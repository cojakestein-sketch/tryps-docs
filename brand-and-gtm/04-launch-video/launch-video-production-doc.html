Script

Beat 1 — The Cab Open
INT. Yellow Minivan Taxi — Day — Moving
Wide lens (14mm), mounted on dashboard facing backward into the cab. No one in the front passenger seat — just the driver's shoulder visible at the edge of frame.
Jake is crammed in the middle seat with FOUR GUYS in identical RED HAWAIIAN SHIRTS. These are post-college frat degenerates — make it obvious. Summer energy. They're going to Cabo for the summer.
— GUY TO JAKE'S LEFT: chewing gum, texting, cheesy sunglasses on
— GUY TO JAKE'S RIGHT: earbuds in, completely zoned out
— GUY IN BACK LEFT: asleep against the window
— GUY IN BACK RIGHT: doing something else entirely (scrolling TikTok, eating, etc.)
The shot starts tight on Jake's face (cropped in post from 4K), then slowly scales out to reveal the full chaos around him. Jake is the only one who is calm and still.
Jake
These guys are going to Cabo. They booked flights three hours ago. No hotel. No plan. They think they'll figure it out when they land.

Beat. Looks around at them. Back to camera.

Jake
I'm good.

He reaches across the guy next to him, pops the sliding door, and climbs out of the middle seat. The physicality of escaping is inherently funny. HONK.

Beat 2 — Quinn
EXT. NYC Street — Day
Jake is on the sidewalk. He falls into stride next to QUINN — a guy walking to work. Earbuds in, coffee in one hand, backpack on. Quinn is on autopilot. Quinn does NOT notice Jake. Quinn does NOT make eye contact with camera. They walk in the same direction but in completely different worlds.
Jake breaks the fourth wall — talking directly to the audience while walking alongside someone who has no idea he's being talked about.
Jake
This is Quinn. He plans every trip our friend group takes. He books our flights, hotels, and even restaurants — and all we do is Venmo him. We love Quinn. We're supposed to go to Patagonia next month. I haven't done a single thing.

Jake turns the street corner. Quinn keeps walking straight. Gone. Never knew he was there.

Beat 3 — Bachelorette Planner
EXT. Brownstone Stoop — Day
Jake rounds the corner. A GIRL is sitting on brownstone steps, crying. Her BEST FRIEND sits next to her, rubbing her back. The girl is wearing something that visually signals bachelorette party — a "BRIDE TO BE" sash, or she's holding a planner/notebook with wedding stickers, or there's a veil peeking out of her bag. Make it obvious what she's planning without dialogue. Just 2 people on a stoop.
Jake
And Quinn's not the only planner. She's been trying to plan a bachelorette party since December. Seven friends. Seven jobs. Seven different lives and schedules. The wedding is next month.

Keeps walking.

Beat 4 — Grandma on Hold
EXT. Central Park — Day
Jake walks into Central Park. An OLD GRANDMA is sitting on a park bench. She has a CUTE SMALL DOG on a leash next to her. She's holding an obviously old phone — not a smartphone. Phone is on speaker. We hear the unmistakable tinny hold music of a 1-800 number. She's annoyed but clearly used to this. She's been doing this her whole life.
Jake
She's been on hold with a travel agent for forty minutes. And she won't be getting through anytime soon. Because that's how she's always done it.
Alt Lines for Middle Phrase

"Spoiler: she doesn't." / "Nobody is." / "Forty-one now." — pick on shoot day based on Jake's delivery.

Beat 5 — Nepo Girl
EXT. Manhattan Sidewalk — Day
New part of the city. We're in Manhattan now. A NEPO GIRL is walking down the sidewalk — iced coffee in hand, nice purse, the whole look. She's on the phone with her mom. Full stereotype. Not subtle.
Nepo Girl
(background, on phone)
Mom — Mom — I told you, I'm not staying at the Marriott, I want the one Sophia went to —
Jake
Then there's this approach. She outsourced all the planning to her mother. Honestly? Respect. But most of us can't do that.
Beat 6 — The Turn
EXT. NYC Street / Citi Bike — Day
Jake hops on a CITI BIKE. Cut to him biking through the city. The energy shifts — this is the emotional core. He's moving, the city is moving, and for the first time he's being completely honest.
Jake
Here's my problem. I'm the guy who never plans anything. I show up, I have a great time, then I go home. And for my whole life, that worked — because I had Quinn. And Quinn handled everything for me.

Beat.

Jake
We really all need a Quinn. That's why I built this. Because we all need a Quinn. Quinn even wants a Quinn.
Beat 7 — The Product
EXT. NYC Street — Day
Jake parks the Citi Bike. It CLICKS and LOCKS. He steps off and faces camera.
Jake
I put a travel agent in your group chat.
Jake pulls out his RED PHONE. Holds it up to camera. We see the app on screen — a real demonstration of how Tryps works.
Jake
Add it to the group chat. Text it where you want to go. It comes back with flights, a hotel, and a plan. Your group can't decide? It runs the vote. You can even call it. One number. Whole trip.
Product Note

The app works via iMessage — you add the Tryps phone number to an existing group chat. The AI agent (Martin) appears like a real contact. It handles itinerary, polls, expense tracking, and cost splitting. Everyone participates from iMessage — no download required to use basic features. "You can even call it" = Wave 2 feature (specced, not yet shipped). Confirm with Jake if this claim goes in the commercial.

Beat 8 — Street Vendor + Cab Exit
EXT. NYC Street Vendor / Curb — Day
Jake puts his phone in his pocket. Passes a classic NYC STREET VENDOR. He spots something on the table — it's the SAME RED HAWAIIAN SHIRT the Cabo guys were wearing. He grabs it. Puts it on over his Tryps tee. Throws on a straw hat. Corny sunglasses.
He continues talking as he dresses — 1-2 transition lines before the Cabo callback.
Jake
(transition lines TBD — continuing to sell the product or reflecting on the journey)
Jake
You know what, Cabo actually sounds pretty good.
Jake raises his wrist. He speaks into his APPLE WATCH — tells Tryps to book Cabo. We see a notification pop up on the watch face showing Tryps features in action. Trip confirmed.
Jake puts his hand up and hails a taxi. A yellow cab pulls up. He opens the door. Looks back at the camera one last time.
Jake
Tell Quinn I'll be back by Thursday.
ANIMATED OVERLAY: A full Tryps itinerary pops up on screen — flights, hotel, activities, all organized. The trip comes together visually before the audience's eyes. This is a post-production graphic layered over the footage.

Gets in. Door closes. Cab pulls away into NYC traffic. Hold on the empty street for 2 seconds.

TEXT TRYPS. GO WHILE YOU CAN.

Narrative Arc

Comedy (Cabo) → Confession (Quinn) → Empathy (Bachelorette) → Observation (Grandma) → Comedy (Nepo) → Real (The Turn / Citi Bike) → Product (Citi Bike Park) → Payoff (Vendor + Cabo + Cab)

Bookend: Gets OUT of a cab at the start. Gets INTO a cab at the end.
Bookend: Red Hawaiian shirts in the cab at the start. Same red Hawaiian shirt from the vendor at the end.
The twist: The guy who never planned anything just planned Cabo from his Apple Watch while buying a tourist shirt on a sidewalk.
