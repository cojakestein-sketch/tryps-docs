4. Deep Dive: Dan McAdams' Narrative Identity & Travel DNA

Topic from Jake's late-night brain dump (Mar 23, ~1:30am). No 3pm topic suggestion response found — defaulting to this.

What It Is

Dan P. McAdams (Northwestern) argues personality operates on three levels: dispositional traits (Big Five), characteristic adaptations (context-dependent goals, values, motivations), and narrative identity (the internalized life story that gives a person unity and purpose). Traditional personality stops at traits. McAdams says the story you tell about yourself — redemption arcs, generativity themes, how you connect past to future — is a distinct, measurable layer of who you are.

Why It Matters for Tryps Right Now

Travel DNA already captures the first level (Big Five-adjacent traits via vibe questions). McAdams' framework reveals two richer layers. Characteristic adaptations = what someone wants from this specific trip, which shifts per group and context. Narrative identity = why someone travels at all. If the agent understands not just "likes beaches" but "is trying to be more spontaneous after years of overplanning," it makes fundamentally better recommendations and vote-on-behalf decisions.

Key Takeaways

  • Travel DNA → Three Tiers: Tier 1 = vibe quiz (traits), Tier 2 = trip intent parsing (adaptations), Tier 3 = cross-trip pattern recognition (narrative). Rizwan's memory architecture loosely mirrors this — user_memory ≈ traits, trip_memory ≈ adaptations, destination_patterns ≈ narrative.
  • The "Why" Question: Add one open-ended question to trip creation: "What are you hoping this trip will be?" NLP extraction from this single question yields richer signals than 20 A/B preference cards.
  • Narrative ≠ Static: McAdams emphasizes narratives evolve. The agent should weight recent trip signals higher — Rizwan's recency-based contradiction resolution already handles this.
  • Group Narrative Tension: When 6 people's narratives conflict, McAdams' generativity concept maps to the "Ultimate Planner" archetype. The agent should amplify planners' contributions while protecting "I Don't Care-ers" from decision fatigue.
  • Competitive Moat: No travel app thinks about personality at this depth. If Tryps builds the richest traveler personality graph — traits + adaptations + narrative — that's a genuine data moat.

Resource: McAdams, D.P. (2001). "The Psychology of Life Stories" — Review of General Psychology. The foundational paper on narrative identity as a personality layer.
