Role 1 — Hiring Now

GTM Growth Engineer

Makes things: content, clips, copy, ads. The person Jake and Sean point at whatever needs producing.

The audition: Screen-record any app on your phone. Edit it into a polished 15-sec product demo clip with text overlays and clean transitions. Attach it to your proposal. If you skip this, we skip your proposal.

The Job

You're not a specialist. You're the person who makes things happen across the entire brand and go-to-market operation. One week you're screen-recording the app and cutting polished 15-sec demos with text overlays and transitions. The next you're writing App Store copy. The week after that you're managing Meta ad spend and flagging which UGC clips are winning.

The core skill: you can screen-record a mobile app and produce a polished product demo clip in under 30 minutes — with text callouts, clean transitions, and branded elements layered on top. Not raw recordings. Think @claudeai on Twitter or Partiful's App Store video. If you can do that, everything else follows from the same muscle.

Role 2 — Hiring Now

GTM Infrastructure Engineer

Builds every system, workflow, and tool the brand and go-to-market org runs on. The Asif of GTM. Founding role.

The Job

You're the person who makes everyone else faster. Sean says "I need to publish across 8 platforms." You set up Buffer, connect the accounts, build the auto-repost workflows, and hand him a dashboard. The Growth Engineer says "I need 50 ad variants from this UGC clip." You build the Recharm pipeline and Figma plugin that lets them do it in 5 minutes.

This is a founding role. You're not filling a seat — you're building the entire marketing infrastructure from zero. If you do this well, you grow with the company.
