# Why Now & Right to Win

## The Honest Assessment

Everybody can make a mobile app. Everybody can make a mobile app that's viral. We've seen it:

- **Partiful** did it for parties
- **Strava** did it for running
- **Beli** did it for restaurants

You have a simple UI front end that people share with their friends and it captures data — instead of restaurants, runs, or parties, we do it for travel.

We believe that's a compelling thesis that will spread. **But we believe there's tons of competition in that market, and we don't have any right to win as a late entry.** Other players probably have a better right to win than we do on mobile alone.

## Where We Actually Win

Where we are unique and special is that **nobody has taken the mobile app and integrated these two layers on top and beneath it.**

### Layer Above: iMessage Integration

We've partnered with Linq to build best-in-class infrastructure for capturing trip details and planning where they currently live — in iMessage.

**The killer feature:** Nobody — no provider, not Linq, not SendBlue, nobody — can do native polling today to facilitate group decisions about a trip in iMessage. That's coming on Linq's roadmap in the next couple of months.

- We are already the first initial beta tester of this feature
- When it deploys, we will be the **only platform** where people can vote on what they're doing in iMessage and log it back to a trip planning platform

### Layer Below: Agentic Execution (X402)

We're building on cutting-edge X402 protocols.

What X402 enables: our agent can make API calls and do things on the user's behalf — search, book, coordinate — all triggered from the iMessage conversation.

## The Full Picture

The combination of these three layers is the moat:

1. **iMessage** — where the conversation already lives (no behavior change)
2. **Mobile app** — for people who want visibility and control
3. **Agent layer** — AI that executes the plan end-to-end

Nobody else has all three. That's the right to win.
