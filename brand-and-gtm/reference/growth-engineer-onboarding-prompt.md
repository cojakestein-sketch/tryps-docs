1. What was their launch strategy?
2. What content did they produce and on which platforms?
3. Did they use creators/UGC? How?
4. What tools/infrastructure did they use?
5. What was their GTM team structure? How many people, what roles?
6. What worked? What didn't?
7. What can Tryps steal and adapt?

Also research:
- **GTM team structures for consumer app launches** — What does a lean, effective GTM team look like for a pre-seed/seed consumer app launching in 2026? Beyond founder + creative director + infra engineer + growth engineer, what roles are typically needed? Content editor? Community manager? Paid acquisition specialist? PR person? ## Document Structure

### 1. The Mission (half a page max)

Set the tone. Two mottos:
- **"We are taking over and controlling the media."**
- **"We are students of the game."**

This is not a normal marketing job. We are launching a consumer app in 30 days. Every single thing this person does is in service of making Tryps feel like it's everywhere. We are studying what Wispr, Partiful, Locket, BeReal, Gas, and every other breakout app did — and we are executing their playbook with our own brand on top.

Frame the energy: this is a wartime operation. Speed over perfection. Volume over polish. Ship it, measure it, iterate.

### 2. What Tryps Is (1 paragraph)

Not a product explainer. Just enough for Inej to pitch it in a sentence: "You text Tryps in your group chat and it plans your whole trip." Link to the pitch deck and the app.

### 3. The GTM Team (org chart)

Jake → Sean (Creative Director, approves everything) → Growth Engineer + Sami (Infra Engineer). Show who does what, who approves what, how work flows.

### 4. Case Studies: How the Best Brands Launched (from research) — DISCUSSION SECTION

This is the heart of the strategy conversation. For each brand studied, include:
- What they did (2-3 bullet summary)
- What we're stealing from their playbook
- How we're adapting it for Tryps
- **Discussion question for the room** — one open question per case study that Jake, Sean, and Inej should debate

This section should feel like a briefing that kicks off a real conversation, not a lecture. Jake wants to study these cases WITH Sean and Inej, not present AT them. The goal is for the three of them to look at what worked for other brands and commit to which plays Tryps is running.

Include 3-5 case studies. Go deep enough that the room can have a real discussion — not just "Wispr did social media." What SPECIFICALLY did they do? What was the content? What was the cadence? What was the team structure? What was the result?

Format each case study as a discussion card:
```
### [Brand Name] — [One-line what they did]
**The play:** [2-3 sentences on their strategy]
**What worked:** [Specifics]
**What we're stealing:** [Concrete actions for Tryps]
**Open question for the room:** [Something to debate]
```

### 5. Strategy Commitments — DECISION SECTION

This is where the meeting gets concrete. After discussing the case studies, Jake, Sean, and Inej need to commit to decisions. Structure this as a list of open strategic questions that need answers by the end of the meeting:

- **Content strategy:** What are the 3-4 content pillars we're committing to? (e.g., product demos, group chat memes, trip inspiration, founder story). Which do we lead with pre-launch?
- **Platform priority:** We say 8 platforms but realistically, which 2-3 are we going ALL IN on? What's the minimum viable presence on the others?
- **Creator program model:** Are we running the Locket playbook (50 creators, cut underperformers) or a different model? Are we doing the contest ("Go on Your Dream Trip") at launch or saving it?
- **Paid strategy:** When do we turn on Meta ads — day 1 or after we see what organic content works? What's the trigger?
- **Launch week choreography:** What does May 2 actually look like hour by hour? Who posts what, when, where?

Frame each as a decision card with the options, Jake's current lean, and space for the room to debate and commit. Granola will capture the final decisions.

### 6. GTM Team Needs — What Roles Are We Still Missing? — DISCUSSION SECTION

Now that we know what Inej does, map out the full GTM org and identify the gaps. This section should answer: **what does the complete GTM team need to look like to control the media for this launch?**

Structure:
1. **Current coverage map** — table of all 13 scopes showing who covers what today (Jake / Sean / Sami / Inej / nobody)
2. **The gaps** — which scopes or capabilities have no owner or thin coverage? Be specific. Examples might include: dedicated paid acquisition person, community manager, PR/press outreach, video editor, someone to manage the App Store listing ongoing, someone to run the Product Hunt launch day.
3. **Role recommendations** — For each gap, recommend: (a) is this a new hire, (b) can an existing person absorb it, (c) can an AI agent handle it, or (d) can we skip it for launch and add later?
4. **Discussion question:** "If we could hire one more person before May 2, what role would have the highest impact?"
5. **Inej's input** — explicitly ask Inej during the meeting: "Now that you've seen the full scope, what do you think is missing? What roles have you seen on GTM teams that we don't have?"

This should be informed by the research on how other brands structured their GTM teams.
