# Market Cap to User Growth Graph

## Concept

A research-backed graph that shows the statistical correlation between user count and market cap across companies in our competitive space.

### Axes

- **X-axis:** Users (MAU or total users — whichever has better available data)
- **Y-axis:** Market cap (at the time of that user count)

### Companies to Include

Plot each company at multiple stages of growth (10K users, 100K, 1M, etc.):

- **Expedia** (public — press releases, SEC filings)
- **Kayak** (public pre-acquisition — IPO data)
- **Partiful** (private — funding round valuations as proxy)
- **Beli** (private — funding round valuations as proxy)
- Additional travel/social companies TBD

### What This Proves

Show investors a statistical correlation: as user count grows in this space, market cap follows a predictable curve.

### The Punchline

> "To get a 10x return on your investment — at 2-and-20, that's $200M of market cap — in theory we would need X many MAUs. If you just believed we could capture 1% of Expedia's current user base (X users, $30B market cap), we'd be a $300M organization."

### Data Sources to Explore

- SEC filings for public companies (Expedia, Booking, TripAdvisor, Kayak pre-acquisition)
- Crunchbase/PitchBook for private company valuations at funding rounds
- Press releases announcing user milestones
- Earnings call transcripts mentioning MAU/user counts
