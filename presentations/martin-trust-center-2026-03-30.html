The Problem

Trip planning happens in iMessage.
Nothing acts on it.

1

Coordination is natural in group chat

Friends already text about where to go, when, who's in. This is where travel decisions start.

2

But plans get buried

Dates, prices, preferences — all scattered across 200 messages. One person becomes the unpaid travel agent.

3

Nothing captures + executes

There's no way to extract what's being discussed and immediately act on it — search flights, book stays, split costs.

4

6+ apps, one frustrated planner

iMessage + Google Docs + Splitwise + Kayak + Airbnb + Calendar. No single tool for group travel.

5

Existing tools are solo-first

Expedia, Kayak, Google Flights — all built for one person booking one trip. Groups are an afterthought.

6

Trips die in the group chat

Without someone driving it forward, trips stall. The "we should totally go" never becomes "we're going."

The Solution

An agent in your iMessage that
captures, facilitates, and books.

01 — Capture

Reads the group chat

Agent picks up on travel intent, dates, destinations, preferences — everything your group is already saying.

02 — Facilitate

Steers the group

Runs natural polls, surfaces options, drives toward decisions. Like a designated driver for planning — not a backseat passenger.

03 — Book

Executes on behalf

Once the group decides, agent books flights, stays, and activities. In one text, a group can book their entire trip.

Product Deep Dive

Three-Layer Architecture

Each layer reinforces the others. Nobody else integrates all three.

Layer 1
iMessage
Where conversations happen. Zero behavior change — people already coordinate trips here.
  • Natural language trip creation
  • Group polling & voting via Linq
  • Expense logging by text
  • "Martin" agent (passes Jennifer Test)
Linq →
Layer 2
Mobile App
Where plans take shape. Visual, structured, interactive — for people who want to see it all.
  • Trip itinerary with drag-and-drop
  • Expense tracking & splitting
  • Travel DNA & preferences
  • Completeness checklist
Stripe →
Layer 3
Agentic Layer
Where execution happens. AI books, coordinates, and acts on behalf of the group.
  • Flight & stay search/booking
  • Vote-on-behalf from preferences
  • Memory architecture (learns over time)
  • Recommendation engine

Competitive Differential

Our Right to Win

First to market with native polling in iMessage

We believe agents will facilitate interactions between people and execute on their behalf. If we can put an agent where people already are — in a way that's super convenient to communicate preferences — we win in this new era of human-shared, agent-selected preferences.

Data Moat: Shared Trip Memory

No other platform holds the full record of what a group decided, preferred, voted on, and experienced together. More trips = richer memory = smarter agent = users return.

Three-Layer Integration

Anyone can build a travel app. Nobody integrates iMessage, a native mobile experience, and an autonomous agentic booking layer into one product.

Target: Group Travelers 20s–30s

People who believe travel is an innate part of the human experience. Bachelor/bachelorette parties, friend group trips, reunion trips. Willing to spend, looking for someone to handle the logistics.

Agent as Facilitator — "The Grill"

Our Trip Agent runs on cron jobs, steering the group chat toward completion. It grills the group — polls, guides, nudges — until the trip is booked. Facilitation, not dictation.
