# UGC Program — Research Topics

These topics came up during the creator criteria interview. Each one would strengthen the strategy but needs dedicated research before acting on it. Jake and Sean should review this list and decide what's worth pursuing.

---

## 1. Gender Split on Group Trip Planning
**Question:** What's the actual gender split on who plans group trips? Should we weight creator targeting toward women?
**Why it matters:** If 70% of group trip planners are women, the creator list should reflect that. Right now we're guessing 60/40 female-to-male.
**How to research:** Look for surveys or data from travel platforms (Airbnb, Expedia, Hopper) on who books group trips. Check if any travel marketing agencies have published this data.

## 2. Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform
**Question:** What's the engagement rate benchmark for travel/lifestyle creators on TikTok vs Instagram in the 22-28 demo?
**Why it matters:** We set a 3% minimum engagement rate threshold. Is that too high or too low for this niche? Need real benchmarks to calibrate.
**How to research:** Check influencer marketing platforms (HypeAuditor, Social Blade, CreatorIQ) for industry benchmarks in travel/lifestyle verticals.

## 3. Reference Creator Deep Dive
**Question:** For each of the 13 reference creators, what's their follower count, engagement rate, platforms (IG/TikTok/YouTube), and content style?
**Why it matters:** Confirms they actually fit the criteria we locked. Gives Jake a clean reference sheet. Sets the benchmark for what "good" looks like with real numbers.
**How to research:** Pull each creator's profile manually or via an influencer analytics tool. Log in a shared Google Sheet.

## 4. Travel YouTubers Seeking Sponsors (50K-500K)
**Question:** Which travel YouTubers in the 50K-500K subscriber range are actively looking for brand sponsors?
**Why it matters:** YouTube creators with loyal audiences are our highest-value targets. Tryps can sponsor their vlogs and fund trips. These creators actively want this kind of partnership.
**How to research:** Search YouTube for travel vloggers in this range. Check if they have sponsorship inquiry emails in their bio. Look at who's running mid-roll ads vs who has open sponsor slots.

## 5. ALS Ice Bucket Challenge Mechanics
**Question:** What specifically made the Ice Bucket Challenge go viral? (Tag 3 friends, 24-hour deadline, public accountability, etc.) What's the Tryps version of that repeatable mechanic?
**Why it matters:** Sean's vision for the contest is ALS-scale. That means the mechanic needs to be simple, repeatable, and self-spreading. Understanding exactly what made Ice Bucket work will inform the Tryps contest design.
**How to research:** Case studies on viral challenge mechanics. Look at Ice Bucket, Mannequin Challenge, and more recent viral brand challenges for common patterns.

## 6. Dream Trip Prize Costing
**Question:** What would a "dream trip" prize actually cost? First-class flight + trip for a group of friends. Need real numbers.
**Why it matters:** The contest prize is "Where do you want to go? We'll send you there." Jake needs to know: is that $2K? $5K? $15K? This determines how many winners and whether the contest is financially viable.
**How to research:** Price out 3 sample trips (domestic weekend, international week, first-class flight + hotel for 4 people). Use Google Flights + Airbnb for real numbers.

## 7. Partner Brand Discounts
**Question:** What brands would offer 10% discounts to Tryps creators in exchange for exposure? (Away luggage, Patagonia, airlines, hotel apps, etc.)
**Why it matters:** A smaller incentive layer ("post on your Story, get 10% off Away luggage") could drive free posts from creators who don't win the contest. Needs real brand partners.
**How to research:** List 10-15 brands that align with the Tryps demo (travel gear, lifestyle, airlines). Check if they have influencer/affiliate programs. Some may already offer creator discount codes.

## 8. What @eddyrosenthal and @gracedonner Actually Charge
**Question:** What do these two specific creators charge for a sponsored post? What's their engagement rate?
**Why it matters:** These are Sean and Jake's reference picks. Their rates set the benchmark for the mid and micro tiers. If Grace Donner charges $2K/post, the budget math changes significantly.
**How to research:** Check if they have rate cards. Use influencer pricing estimators. Or just DM and ask.

## 9. Sean's Account vs Jake's Account Mutual Analysis
**Question:** How many mutuals does Sean's personal account vs Jake's account vs @tryps have with target creators?
**Why it matters:** The DM strategy says "whoever has the most mutuals sends the DM." Before launch, we should know which account is the strongest sender by default.
**How to research:** Pick the top 20 target creators from the reference list. Check mutual followers for each account. Log which account has the edge for each creator.

## 10. Coachella / Festival Influencer Comping Model
**Question:** How does Coachella's influencer comping actually work? Structure, costs, expectations, deliverables.
**Why it matters:** Sean's vision is that Tryps should send creators on trips the way festivals comp influencers. Understanding the actual mechanics of how festivals do this would inform a "Tryps sends you on a trip" creator program.
**How to research:** Look for case studies or interviews about how Coachella, Bonnaroo, Art Basel, etc. manage their influencer programs. Check if any agencies publish their festival activation playbooks.
