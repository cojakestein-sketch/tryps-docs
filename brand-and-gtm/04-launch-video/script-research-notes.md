## 1. Dollar Shave Club Directing Notes

**Key coaching for Jake:**
- Speak 20% slower than feels natural
- Never smile at your own jokes — the second you signal "this is funny," it dies
- Treat the camera like one friend, not an audience
- Rehearse until the script sounds improvised (Dubin was UCB-trained improv — the "casual" was extremely practiced)
- One continuous movement — walking gives energy without requiring performance

**The engine:** Jake stays calm and on-script. Background gags contradict his competence — he doesn't react to the chaos. The tension between his composure and everyone else's disaster IS the joke.

**Mayhem hybrid:** Dean Winters (AllState) is the chaos itself narrating calmly. Dubin is the calm person walking through chaos. Jake is Dubin-style: calm and competent, but the travel disasters around him are the Mayhem.

**Key difference from DSC:** Tryps needs a slightly longer setup than "a dollar a month." The hook has to work harder before the gags start.

**Script structure from DSC:**
1. Hook (0-15s): Direct-to-camera value prop. No preamble.
2. Credibility jab (15-30s): Undercuts formality immediately.
3. Escalating gags (30-75s): Each background bit gets more absurd while delivery stays flat. 70% jokes / 30% product.
4. CTA (75-90s): Back to dead-simple pitch. Done.

## 2. NYC Shoot Route

**Route: West Village → SoHo (~12 blocks)**

| Beat | Location | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Cold open | 1 train, Christopher St station | Jake delivers opening line on subway, walks up stairs into daylight. Free production value. |
| Cabo Guys | Christopher & Bleecker | Tight brick, fire escapes, zero chain signage. |
| Stoop Friends | Perry St or Bank St | Most photogenic brownstone stoops in Manhattan. |
| Nepo Girl | Hudson St south toward Tribeca | Wide sidewalk, leafy, upscale. |
| Old-School Traveler | Benches near West Broadway & Canal (SoHo) | Cast-iron buildings in background. |
| Cab pickup | West Broadway & Grand St | Taxis queue naturally, cobblestone-adjacent, zero competing brand logos. |

**Visual variety:** Subway (enclosed) → staircase emergence → narrow Village streets → wider Hudson St → SoHo's broad avenues. Five textures in under a mile.

**Permits:** Guerrilla is legal on sidewalks with no ground equipment. Skip Central Park (permit hassle). Subway is technically MTA permit but unenforced for gimbal-only crew. Shoot midday weekday.

## 3. Pitch Lines for Jake

### Category A — Dead simple
1. "Add the number. That's it." — Final line before cab
2. "One contact. Whole trip." — Opening line
3. "You text it. It books." — Mid-video

### Category B — Explain-by-contrast
4. "Your group chat already exists — why are you planning your trip somewhere else?" — Early, frames the problem
5. "Everyone's got 14 tabs open and a Venmo request nobody paid — we just text one number." — Middle, after chaos montage
6. "People are out here copy-pasting flight links into four different apps. We put the whole trip in the thread you already have." — Mid-video, fast delivery

### Category C — Mic drop
7. "I put a travel agent in your group chat. You're welcome." — Final line before logo card
8. "Tryps plans your flights, hotels, itinerary, splits the bill — nobody had to download anything." — Feature stack moment
9. "Your group chat just got a lot smarter." — Opening, cocky
10. "We didn't build an app. We moved into your iMessage." — Mid-to-late reframe

**Recommended combo:** Open with #2, use #5 in the middle, close with #7 before the cab.

## 5. Cold Opens (first 3 seconds)

### Top 3 recommended:

**#1 — The Group Chat From Hell**
- See: Phone screen, texts flying — "I can't do that weekend" / "wait who's booking" / "can someone check flights" / "nvm I'm broke"
- Hear: Rapid iMessage sound effects stacking
- Transition: Smash cut to Jake walking. "That was 47 messages. Nobody booked anything."

**#6 — The Conspiracy Board**
- See: Bird's eye of a guy on apartment floor surrounded by printed directions, guidebooks, sticky notes
- Hear: Printer whirring
- Transition: Jake's voice over: "This is my friend Matt. He's going to Cancun in theory."

**#8 — The Fake Prestige Open**
- See: Gorgeous slow-mo travel montage — beaches, cocktails, golden hour
- Hear: Dramatic orchestral swell
- Transition: Record scratch. Cut to cramped apartment. Jake at kitchen table: "Yeah, none of that's happening without a plan. Here's the thing —" stands up, walks out, we're in NYC.

### Other options:
- **#2 Spreadsheet Guy** — Close-up of color-coded travel spreadsheet with tabs like "DAVE'S ALLERGIES"
- **#3 Mid-Argument** — Two friends heated, one pointing at phone: "I TOLD you we should've booked in January!"
- **#4 Scroll of Doom** — Someone doom-scrolling flight prices, going up with each refresh
- **#5 Bold Text Over Black** — "The average group trip takes 62 texts, 3 spreadsheets, and one friendship."
- **#7 Already Defeated** — Person face-down on couch, laptop on floor showing Kayak
