**Brand tagline: "Travel Agent for Your Group Chat"**

## 1. Who Sends the DMs

### Option A: Jake (Founder) sends them

**Pros:**
- Founders get 3-5x higher response rates than brand accounts. People want to talk to the person building the thing.
- Creates a personal relationship from day one. Creator feels chosen, not targeted.
- Jake can speak authentically about why he built Tryps and what the vision is.
- If Jake already follows or knows someone, the DM feels natural.

**Cons:**
- Doesn't scale past 10-15 conversations at once. Jake has a company to run.
- If Jake's personal brand isn't built up yet, creators may not take it seriously.
- Mixing founder DMs with business outreach can get messy.

### Option B: Brand account (@tryps) sends them

**Pros:**
- Scales better. Anyone on the team can manage the inbox.
- Looks "official" to creators who care about that.

**Cons:**
- Brand DMs get ignored. Creators get dozens of these a week and most are trash.
- No human connection. Feels like a campaign, not a conversation.
- Response rate will be 5-10% at best.

### Option C: Sean (Agency) sends them

**Pros:**
- Sean can position himself as the creative director running the launch. Adds credibility.
- Separates the relationship management from Jake's plate.
- Sean already knows the influencer outreach game.

**Cons:**
- One degree removed from the founder. Some creators want to hear from the source.
- If the creator googles Sean and doesn't see a big following, they may not respond.

### Recommendation: Jake sends the first message. Sean manages the follow-ups.

Here's why. The first DM is everything. A founder reaching out personally is the highest-converting cold outreach in the creator economy. Period. Jake sends a short, casual DM or voice note. Once the creator responds, Sean takes over the logistics (negotiation, brief, timeline, content review). Jake stays looped in for the relationship but isn't buried in back-and-forth.

For the warm contacts (Lily, Simone Stone), Jake sends everything personally. No question. These are friends, not prospects.

## 2. First Contact Message Templates

### Template A: Voice Note Script (30 seconds, casual)

Jake records this on his phone. No script reading, just hit these beats:

> "Hey [Name], it's Jake. I've been watching your stuff for a minute and I love what you're doing with [specific thing about their content]. I'm launching an app called Tryps on May 2nd. It's basically a travel agent that lives in your group chat. You plan trips with your friends right in iMessage. I think your audience would actually love it and I'd love to send you early access and talk about doing something together for launch. No pressure at all, just lmk if you're down."

**Why voice notes work:** They can't be faked or mass-sent. The creator knows a real person recorded it for them. Response rates on voice DMs are 2-3x higher than text.

**Rules:**
- MUST mention something specific about their content. "I love your stuff" alone is not enough.
- Keep it under 35 seconds. Respect their time.
- Sound like you're talking to a friend, not pitching an investor.

### Template B: Text DM (Short, Personal)

> Hey [Name]! Been following you for a bit, love [specific post or series they did]. Quick question. I'm launching an app called Tryps on May 2. It makes group trip planning stupid easy, right inside iMessage. Think "travel agent for your group chat." I'd love to get you early access before launch and see if you'd be down to create something together. Totally casual, no weird brand deal energy. Want me to send you the details?

**Key principles:**
- Lead with their work, not your app.
- Ask a question at the end. Questions get replies. Statements don't.
- "No weird brand deal energy" disarms them immediately. They've heard a thousand pitches.
- Keep it to one paragraph. If they have to scroll, you've lost.

### Template C: Text DM for Warm Contacts (Lily, Simone Stone)

> Hey! So I finally have a launch date for Tryps, May 2. Been building this thing for a while and it's actually ready. It's a group trip planner that works right in iMessage. Before I do the whole big launch thing, I wanted to see if you'd be down to try it and maybe post something about it? I'll send you everything and make it super easy. Would mean a lot to have you involved from day one.

**Why this is different:** No introduction needed. No "I love your content" because they already know each other. Goes straight to the ask but keeps it warm and personal. "Would mean a lot" hits different coming from someone you actually know.

### Follow-Up: 3 Days, No Response

> Hey! Just bumping this in case it got buried. Totally get it if the timing doesn't work. Either way, I'd still love to get you early access to Tryps before launch. No strings attached.

**Why:** Short, zero pressure, reframes it as a gift (early access) not an ask.

### Follow-Up: 7 Days, No Response

> Last one from me, promise haha. We're about 5 weeks out from launch and I'm putting together a small group of creators who get early access. Would love you in it. If you're interested at all, just say the word and I'll send everything over. If not, no worries at all. Your content is still fire regardless.

**Why:** Acknowledges you're following up again (self-aware, not pushy). Creates light urgency with "5 weeks out" and "small group." Compliments them on the way out. If they don't respond to this, move on. Three touches max.

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Micro-Influencers (1K-10K followers)

**Offer:** Product-only (early access + "founding creator" title).

- Free early access to Tryps before anyone else
- Their name/handle featured in launch content (if they post)
- Invite to a private group chat with Jake and other creators (builds community, makes them feel insider)
- Optional: $50-100 gift card as a thank-you after posting (not upfront, keeps it authentic)

**Why product-only works here:** Micro creators at this level rarely get brand deals. Being part of a launch is exciting for them. The "founding creator" framing makes it feel like an opportunity, not free labor. These creators also have the highest engagement rates (4-8% vs 1-2% for macro). They're your best ROI.

**Volume target:** 30-50 micro creators. This is your base layer.

### Mid-Tier (10K-100K followers)

**Offer:** Product + modest payment OR product + revenue share.

- Early access + everything above
- $200-500 per post (1 Reel or TikTok)
- $300-750 for a bundle (1 post + 2 Stories)
- Alternative: $150 flat + $1 per app download via their unique link (performance-based, aligns incentives)

**Travel/lifestyle niche rates context:** Travel creators in this range typically charge $300-800 per sponsored post. Tryps can come in slightly below market because the product is genuinely cool and the "founding creator" angle adds non-monetary value. Don't lowball. These people know their worth.

**Volume target:** 10-15 mid-tier creators.

### Macro (100K+ followers)

**Offer:** Paid partnership, negotiate individually.

- $1,000-3,000 per post depending on follower count and engagement
- $2,000-5,000 for a package (1 Reel + 1 TikTok + 3 Stories)
- For 500K+ creators, expect $3,000-7,000+ per post. Only pursue 1-2 of these and make sure they genuinely fit.
- Always include early access and the "founding creator" angle, even at this level. Money gets their attention but feeling like part of something keeps them engaged.

**Volume target:** 2-5 macro creators max. These are your headline names for press and credibility.

### When to Offer Product-Only vs Paid

**Product-only works when:**
- The creator has under 10K followers
- They've posted about trip planning, group travel, or iMessage before (organic fit)
- They're a friend-of-a-friend or warm contact
- They're an up-and-coming creator who values the association more than the cash
- They reach out to YOU first (inbound is always product-first)

**Pay them when:**
- They have over 10K engaged followers in your target demo
- You're asking for specific deliverables with a deadline
- They have a media kit and rates. If they send you a rate card, they expect to be paid. Respect that.
- You need guaranteed posting dates (launch week is non-negotiable timing)
- Their content quality is high enough to repurpose as ads

### Total Budget Estimate (for $5K ad budget allocation)

| Tier | Creators | Cost Each | Total |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Micro (1K-10K) | 30-50 | $0-100 | $0-2,500 |
| Mid (10K-100K) | 10-15 | $200-500 | $2,000-5,000 |
| Macro (100K+) | 2-3 | $1,000-3,000 | $2,000-6,000 |

Realistically, with $5K earmarked for influencer spend, focus on 30 micro (product-only) + 8-10 mid ($300 avg) + 1-2 macro ($1,500 avg). That lands around $5,400-6,000 total. Worth slightly over-indexing here because UGC from real creators will outperform paid ads 9 times out of 10 for an app launch.

## 4. Outreach Sequence

### Step 1: Follow + Engage (Days 1-3)

Before you DM anyone, warm them up. Cold DMs from accounts that just followed you feel like spam.

**Actions:**
- Follow the creator from @tryps brand account AND Jake's personal
- Like 2-3 of their recent posts (not all at once, spread across 2 days)
- Leave one genuine comment on a post. Not "love this!" but something specific: "this spot looks insane, where is that?" or "the way you edited this is so clean"
- Watch their Stories and react to at least one (fire emoji, laugh, or a quick reply)
- If they post about group trips, travel planning, or anything adjacent to Tryps, reply to that Story naturally

**Why this matters:** When your DM lands, they'll recognize the name. They might even check who liked their stuff. You go from "random brand" to "oh yeah, I've seen them around." That small recognition is the difference between a reply and being left on read.

### Step 2: First DM (Day 4)

Send using Template A (voice note) for your top picks, Template B (text) for the rest. Template C for warm contacts.

**Timing:** Tuesday through Thursday, 10am-1pm or 7-9pm in their timezone. Never Monday (inbox chaos) or Friday-Sunday (they're living their life, not checking DMs).

**Batch size:** Don't send more than 10-15 DMs per day. Instagram flags mass-messaging and you'll get shadowbanned.

### Step 3: Follow-Up (Days 7 and 11)

- Day 7 (3 days after first DM): Send the 3-day follow-up template
- Day 11 (7 days after first DM): Send the 7-day follow-up template
- If no response after 3 touches, mark them as "not now" and move on. Do NOT send a fourth message.

**Track everything.** Use a simple Google Sheet:

| Creator | Handle | Followers | Tier | DM Sent | Response | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|------|---------|----------|--------|-------|
| Lily | @lily___ | 45K | Mid | 3/28 | 3/28 | Agreed | Warm contact |

### Step 4: Close and Negotiate (Days 7-14 after response)

Once they respond positively, move fast. Here's the flow:

1. **Thank them and share the app.** "Amazing, sending you early access right now." Get the product in their hands immediately.
2. **Let them use it first.** Don't rush into deliverables. Give them 2-3 days to actually try Tryps. If they love it, the content will be better.
3. **Then send the brief.** "Here's what we're thinking for the collab. Super flexible, lmk what feels right."
4. **Negotiate if needed.** If their rate is above budget, counter with a bundle deal or performance bonus. "What if we did $300 for the post + $1 per download through your link? Could end up being way more."
5. **Confirm everything in writing.** Even casual deals need a simple confirmation message: deliverables, timeline, payment amount, usage rights. One paragraph is fine. You don't need a contract for deals under $1,000 but you do need a paper trail.

### Step 5: Brief and Content Guidelines (see Section 6)

Send within 24 hours of agreement. The faster you move, the more excited they stay.

## 5. Targeting Criteria

### What Makes a Good Tryps UGC Creator

**Must-haves:**
- Posts about travel, group activities, or friend hangouts. Even if they're not a "travel creator," if their feed is full of group content, they fit.
- Engagement rate above 3%. Check their last 10 posts. Average the likes, divide by follower count. Under 2% means fake or dead followers.
- Comments that are real conversations, not just emoji spam or bot comments. Read the comments. If people are asking "where is this?" or "how do I book this?", that audience buys things.
- Content quality is at least iPhone-level clean. Doesn't need to be cinematic, but it should look intentional.
- They actually go on trips with friends. This sounds obvious but a lot of "travel creators" travel solo or with a partner. Tryps is for groups.

**Strong signals:**
- They've posted about planning a trip (the pain point Tryps solves)
- They use iMessage (check if they screenshot texts, post iMessage convos, etc.)
- They're in the 22-35 age range (Tryps' core demo)
- They tag friends regularly and those friends tag them back (real social circle, not just posing)
- They post Reels/TikToks, not just static photos (video content is what Tryps needs)
- Their audience is US-based (check comments for context clues, language, locations)
- They've done brand deals before but aren't oversaturated (more than 1 in 3 posts being #ad is a red flag)

**Red flags, avoid:**
- Follower-to-engagement ratio is way off (100K followers, 200 likes per post = bought followers)
- They only post curated flat lays and hotel room tours. Tryps needs energy, not aesthetics.
- Their captions are generic or clearly written by an agency. You want personality.
- They post about luxury travel exclusively. Tryps is for the crew going to Cabo, not the influencer at the Four Seasons.
- They've never posted a Story in the last 2 weeks (inactive or only post for the feed, no casual engagement)

### Creator Archetypes That Fit Tryps

1. **"The Group Chat Leader"** - Always organizing, always texting "so are we doing this or not." Their followers see themselves in the chaos.
2. **"Weekend Trip Queen/King"** - Posts spontaneous road trips, cabin weekends, beach house content. Not luxury, just fun.
3. **"The Planner"** - Makes itineraries, packing lists, travel tip content. Tryps is literally their dream tool.
4. **"Friend Group Content"** - Their whole thing is being part of a funny, relatable crew. Vlogs, group challenges, travel with friends.
5. **"The College/Post-Grad Explorer"** - 22-27, doing their first real trips with friends. Budget-conscious, group-oriented, heavy iMessage users.
